1. basic /ˈbeɪsɪk/ adj. 基本的；根本的；基础的
📖 英文解释
forming an essential foundation; of the simplest kind, without complication or elaboration
✏️ 例句
Access to clean water and food are basic human rights that should be guaranteed for everyone.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
basic needs/rights/skills; basic principle; back to basics; basically (adv.); basics (n. 基础知识)
🧠 助记
base（基础）+ ic → 基础的 → 基本的
basic::/ˈbeɪsɪk/:adj. 基本的:Every child deserves a basic education.
2. fit /fɪt/ adj. 健康的；适合的 v. 适合；安装 n. 合身
📖 英文解释
in good health; suitable or appropriate for a purpose; to be the right size or shape for
✏️ 例句
Regular exercise helps keep your body fit and reduces the risk of chronic diseases.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
fit for; keep fit; fit in (融入); fit out; see fit to; fitness (n.); survival of the fittest
🧠 助记
fit → 想象一件衣服正好合身 → 适合、健康
fit::/fɪt/:adj. 健康的:The job candidate was considered fit for the position.
3. above /əˈbʌv/ prep./adv. 在…上面；超过；以上
📖 英文解释
at a higher level or layer than; more than a specified amount or level; mentioned or appearing earlier
✏️ 例句
The temperature remained above average for the entire month, raising concerns about climate change.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
above average/normal; above all (最重要的是); above-mentioned; rise above; above and beyond
🧠 助记
a（加强）+ bove（牛，拉丁语）→ 高于牛那个层次 → 在…上面
above::/əˈbʌv/:prep./adv. 在…上面:Temperatures have been well above normal this summer.
4. occur /əˈkɜːr/ v. 发生；出现；想到
📖 英文解释
to happen; to come to pass; to be found or present in a place; to come to mind
✏️ 例句
It suddenly occurred to the researcher that the data had been misinterpreted from the start.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
occur to sb (想到); occur in/at/on; occurrence (n.); rarely/frequently occurs; when it occurs
🧠 助记
oc（朝向）+ cur（跑，如current）→ 跑过来了 → 发生
occur::/əˈkɜːr/:v. 发生:Major earthquakes rarely occur in this region.
5. refer /rɪˈfɜːr/ v. 提及；参考；称…为；提交
📖 英文解释
to mention or allude to; to consult a book or authority; to direct to another for help
✏️ 例句
The author refers to several historical events to support the argument in the third chapter.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
refer to (提及/参考); refer sb to; reference (n.); as referred to; referral (n. 转介)
🧠 助记
re（再次）+ fer（带来）→ 再次带来相关信息 → 提及、参考
refer::/rɪˈfɜːr/:v. 提及:Please refer to the user manual for troubleshooting instructions.
6. die /daɪ/ v. 死；消失；渴望 n. 模具；骰子
📖 英文解释
to stop living; to cease to exist; to have a very strong desire for something
✏️ 例句
Many plant and animal species may die out if deforestation continues at the current rate.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
die of/from (死于); die out (灭绝); die for (渴望); die down (平息); dying (adj.); dead (adj.)
🧠 助记
die → d + ie → 直接想到「死」这个概念
die::/daɪ/:v. 死:Thousands of people die each year from preventable diseases.
7. town /taʊn/ n. 城镇；市区；市民
📖 英文解释
a built-up area with a name, defined boundaries, and local government, that is larger than a village
✏️ 例句
The small town was transformed into a bustling city after the discovery of oil nearby.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
downtown (市中心); out of town; hometown; town hall (市政厅); the whole town; go to town
🧠 助记
town → 想象一个有围墙的定居地（古英语 tun）→ 城镇
town::/taʊn/:n. 城镇:The town has a population of about 50,000 people.
8. maintain /meɪnˈteɪn/ v. 保持；维持；维修；坚持主张
📖 英文解释
to cause or enable a condition or situation to continue; to keep in good condition; to affirm insistently
✏️ 例句
It is difficult to maintain economic growth while simultaneously reducing carbon emissions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
maintain order/balance/standards; maintain that (坚持认为); maintenance (n.); well-maintained
🧠 助记
main（主要的）+ tain（握住）→ 握住主要部分 → 维持、保持
maintain::/meɪnˈteɪn/:v. 保持:The government must maintain a stable economic environment.
9. failure /ˈfeɪljər/ n. 失败；故障；失败者；未能做到
📖 英文解释
lack of success; the omission of expected or required action; a mechanical breakdown
✏️ 例句
Learning from failure is often more valuable than achieving success on the first attempt.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
end in failure; failure to do; heart failure (心力衰竭); system failure; doomed to failure; without failure
🧠 助记
fail（失败）+ ure（结果）→ 失败的结果 → 失败
failure::/ˈfeɪljər/:n. 失败:The project ended in failure due to poor planning.
10. composition /ˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃn/ n. 构成；作品；作文；成分
📖 英文解释
the way in which a whole is made up; a piece of writing or music; the action of putting things together
✏️ 例句
The chemical composition of the atmosphere has changed significantly since industrialization.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
composition of (构成); write a composition; musical composition; body composition; compose (v.)
🧠 助记
com（共同）+ position（位置）→ 各部分各就其位 → 构成、作品
composition::/ˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃn/:n. 构成:The ethnic composition of the city has changed dramatically.
11. attend /əˈtend/ v. 出席；参加；照看；专心于
📖 英文解释
to be present at; to go regularly to; to deal with; to pay attention to
✏️ 例句
Students who attend classes regularly tend to perform significantly better in examinations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
attend a meeting/school; attend to (照料/处理); attendance (n.); attendant (n.); pay attention
🧠 助记
at（朝向）+ tend（伸展）→ 向某处伸展 → 出席、照顾
attend::/əˈtend/:v. 出席:All staff are required to attend the annual safety training.
12. critical /ˈkrɪtɪkl/ adj. 批评的；关键的；危急的；批判性的
📖 英文解释
expressing adverse or disapproving comments; of decisive importance; at a point of crisis
✏️ 例句
Critical thinking skills are essential for students to evaluate information in the digital age.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
critical thinking; critical of; critical point/moment; critically ill; critic (n.); criticize (v.)
🧠 助记
critic（评论家）+ al → 像评论家那样 → 批评的；关键的（关键节点需要评判）
critical::/ˈkrɪtɪkl/:adj. 批评的:Access to clean water is critical to public health.
13. alone /əˈləʊn/ adj./adv. 单独的；仅仅；孤独地
📖 英文解释
having no one else present; only; without others' help
✏️ 例句
No single country can tackle the problem of climate change alone; international cooperation is vital.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
leave sb alone; all alone; alone in doing; stand-alone; alone is not enough; let alone (更别提)
🧠 助记
al（全部）+ one（一个）→ 全部只有一个 → 单独的、仅仅
alone::/əˈləʊn/:adj./adv. 单独的:Education alone cannot solve the problem of poverty.
14. meeting /ˈmiːtɪŋ/ n. 会议；集会；相遇
📖 英文解释

✏️ 例句

🧠 助记
meet（相见）+ ing → 相见的活动 → 会议、相遇
meeting::/ˈmiːtɪŋ/:n. 会议:The board meeting will be held next Tuesday.
15. tax /tæks/ n. 税；税款 v. 对……征税；使负担过重
📖 英文解释
a compulsory contribution to state revenue; to impose a tax on; to make heavy demands on
✏️ 例句
The government plans to raise taxes on high-income earners to fund public services.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
pay/cut/raise taxes; income/property tax; tax reform/cut; tax-free; taxpayer; taxing (adj. 繁重的)
🧠 助记
tax → 想象被task（任务）压着，税就是这种负担 → 税
tax::/tæks/:n. 税:The new tax policy aims to reduce inequality.
16. feature /ˈfiːtʃər/ n. 特征；容貌；特写 v. 以……为特色；起重要作用
📖 英文解释
a distinctive attribute or aspect; a part of the face; to have as an important characteristic
✏️ 例句

feature of/in; key/main feature; facial features; feature film (故事片); featured speaker
🧠 助记

feature::/ˈfiːtʃər/:n. 特征:The report features several case studies on urban development.
17. express /ɪkˈspres/ v. 表达；表示 adj. 特快的 n. 快车；快递
📖 英文解释
to convey thoughts or feelings in words; done or sent quickly; a fast service
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
express an opinion/concern; express train; express delivery; expression (n.); expressive (adj.)
🧠 助记

express::/ɪkˈspres/:v. 表达:She found it difficult to express her feelings in words.
18. shift /ʃɪft/ v. 转移；转换；改变 n. 轮班；转变
📖 英文解释
to move or change from one position to another; a change in position or direction; a period of work
✏️ 例句
There has been a fundamental shift in public attitudes toward environmental protection in recent years.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
shift from...to; shift blame; night/day shift; paradigm shift (范式转变); shift key; shift gears
🧠 助记
shift → 想象换档（shift gear）→ 转变、转移
shift::/ʃɪft/:v. 转移:The economy is undergoing a significant shift toward digital services.
19. simple /ˈsɪmpl/ adj. 简单的；简洁的；朴素的；单纯的

🧠 助记

simple::/ˈsɪmpl/:adj. 简单的:The instructions should be written in simple language.
20. successful /səkˈsesfl/ adj. 成功的；取得成就的

successful in doing; a successful career/business; successfully (adv.); success (n.); succeed (v.)

successful::/səkˈsesfl/:adj. 成功的:The campaign was highly successful in raising public awareness.
21. theory /ˈθɪəri/ n. 理论；学说；推测；观点
📖 英文解释
a system of ideas intended to explain something; an idea used to explain something that has not yet been proven

in theory (理论上); theory of evolution; theoretical (adj.); theorist (n.); conspiracy theory

theory::/ˈθɪəri/:n. 理论:The theory needs to be tested through rigorous experiments.
22. key /kiː/ n. 钥匙；关键 adj. 关键的；主要的

a small piece of shaped metal for opening a lock; the most important element; of great importance

key factor/role/element; the key to success; key in (输入); keyboard; off-key; keynote (主题)

key::/kiː/:n. 钥匙:Investment in infrastructure is key to economic growth.
23. image /ˈɪmɪdʒ/ n. 形象；肖像；图像；意象

image::/ˈɪmɪdʒ/:n. 形象:The company is trying to improve its public image.
24. couple /ˈkʌpl/ n. 情侣；夫妻；几个 v. 结合；连接
📖 英文解释
two people or things; a pair; to combine or link
✏️ 例句
A couple of studies have suggested a link between air pollution and cognitive decline.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
a couple of (几个); married couple; couple with (与...结合); coupling (n.); decouple (v.)
🧠 助记

couple::/ˈkʌpl/:n. 情侣:A couple of issues need to be addressed before the launch.
25. whose /huːz/ pron. 谁的；（关系代词）其…的
📖 英文解释
belonging to or associated with which person; of whom or of which (used as a relative pronoun)
✏️ 例句
The researcher whose paper was published in Nature received widespread recognition in the scientific community.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
whose + noun (所有格关系代词); whose idea/turn; a person whose; whose fault is it
🧠 助记
who（谁）+ se（所有格后缀）→ 谁的
whose::/huːz/:pron. 谁的:The author, whose novel won the prize, gave a speech.
