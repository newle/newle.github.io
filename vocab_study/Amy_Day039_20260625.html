1. perfect /ˈpɜːrfɪkt/ adj. 完美的；极好的 v. /pərˈfekt/ 使完善
📖 英文解释
having all the qualities you want; without faults; to make something as good as it can be
✏️ 例句
The weather was perfect for a picnic. She spent years perfecting her technique.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
perfect score(满分); perfect for(非常适合); practice makes perfect(熟能生巧); perfect tense(完成时); near-perfect(近乎完美)
🧠 助记
per(全部) + fect(做,如 effect) → 全部做好 → 完美的
perfect::/ˈpɜːrfɪkt/:adj. 完美的:His performance was not perfect, but it showed remarkable improvement.
2. genetic /dʒəˈnetɪk/ adj. 基因的；遗传的
📖 英文解释
relating to genes or genetics; inherited from parents through genes
✏️ 例句
Scientists discovered a genetic link to the disease. Genetic testing can reveal inherited conditions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
genetic engineering(基因工程); genetic disorder(遗传病); genetic code(遗传密码); genetic diversity(基因多样性); genetically modified(转基因的,GMO)
🧠 助记
gene(基因) + tic(形容词后缀) → 与基因有关的 → 基因的、遗传的
genetic::/dʒəˈnetɪk/:adj. 基因的:Advances in genetic research have transformed modern medicine.
3. competitive /kəmˈpetɪtɪv/ adj. 竞争的；有竞争力的；好胜的
📖 英文解释
involving competition; as good as or better than others; having a strong desire to win
✏️ 例句
The job market is highly competitive. She is very competitive and hates to lose.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
competitive advantage(竞争优势); competitive price(有竞争力的价格); competitive exam(竞争性考试); non-competitive(非竞争性的); competitive spirit(竞争精神)
🧠 助记
compete(竞争) → competitive(竞争的) → 想象运动员在赛场上激烈竞争
competitive::/kəmˈpetɪtɪv/:adj. 竞争的:To remain competitive, companies must constantly innovate and adapt.
4. vast /væst/ adj. 巨大的；大量的；广阔的
📖 英文解释
extremely large in area, amount, or extent
✏️ 例句
The vast majority of people support the new law. The Sahara is a vast desert.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
vast majority(绝大多数); vast amount(大量); vast expanse(广阔地带); vast knowledge(广博知识); a vast improvement(很大改进)
🧠 助记
v + ast(极度) → 极度宿广 → 巨大的、广阔的
vast::/væst/:adj. 巨大的:The vast majority of scientists agree on the reality of climate change.
5. series /ˈsɪriːz/ n. 系列；连续；丛书（单复数同形）
📖 英文解释
a number of things or events of the same kind that come one after another; a set of books, TV shows, etc.
✏️ 例句
She has published a series of articles on climate change. The documentary series ran for three seasons.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
a series of(一系列); TV series(电视连续剧); in series(串联地); lecture series(系列讲座); game series(系列游戏)
🧠 助记
ser(连,如 serial) + ies → 一连串的东西 → 系列
series::/ˈsɪriːz/:n. 系列:A series of unexplained events led investigators to suspect foul play.
6. deliver /dɪˈlɪvər/ v. 传递；传输；分娩；履行承诺
📖 英文解释
to take goods or a message to the place where it is expected; to give a speech; to provide what is promised; to give birth
✏️ 例句
The package was delivered this morning. The doctor delivered the baby. He delivered a powerful speech.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
deliver a speech(发表演讲); deliver on promises(兼现承诺); deliver results(取得成果); home delivery(上门配送); deliver a baby(接生)
🧠 助记
de(下,离) + liver(肝,生命核心) → 把东西从核心处取出送出去 → 传递、分娩
deliver::/dɪˈlɪvər/:v. 传递:The government has struggled to deliver on its campaign promises.
7. equipment /ɪˈkwɪpmənt/ n. 设备；装备（不可数）
📖 英文解释
the tools, machines, clothing, etc. needed for a particular purpose
✏️ 例句
The laboratory has state-of-the-art equipment. Medical equipment must be sterilized.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
equipment failure(设备故障); sports equipment(运动器材); heavy equipment(重型设备); office equipment(办公设备); a piece of equipment(一件设备)
🧠 助记
equip(装备) + ment(名词后缀) → 装备出来的物品 → 设备
equipment::/ɪˈkwɪpmənt/:n. 设备:The hospital urgently needs modern medical equipment to treat patients effectively.
8. visitor /ˈvɪzɪtər/ n. 访问者；游客；参观者
📖 英文解释
a person who visits a person or place
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
welcome visitors(欢迎访客); foreign visitor(外国访客); site visitor(网站访客); visitor center(游客中心); overnight visitor(住宿的访客)
🧠 助记
visit(参观、拜访) + or(人名后缀) → 进行参观的人 → 访问者
visitor::/ˈvɪzɪtər/:n. 访问者:The national park attracted over two million visitors during the summer season.
9. revenue /ˈrevənuː/ n. 财政收入；税收；营业额
📖 英文解释
the total income produced by a given source, especially the income received by a government or company
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
re(回来) + venue(来,来源) → 回流的金錢 → 财政收入
revenue::/ˈrevənuː/:n. 财政收入:The city relies heavily on tourism revenue to fund public services.
10. sector /ˈsektər/ n. 部门；扇区；行业；区域
📖 英文解释
a distinct part or branch of a nation's economy, society, or field of activity
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

sector::/ˈsektər/:n. 部门:The manufacturing sector has declined significantly in many developed countries.
11. inform /ɪnˈfɔːrm/ v. 通知；告诉；举报
📖 英文解释
to tell someone about a particular fact or situation; to have an influence on something
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

inform::/ɪnˈfɔːrm/:v. 通知:The doctor informed the patient of the risks before the surgery.

twice::/twaɪs/:adv. 两次:She checked the report twice before submitting it to avoid any errors.

thank::/θæŋk/:v. 感谢 n. (thanks) 谢意:She wrote a heartfelt letter to thank her mentor for years of guidance.
