1. criticism /ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/ n. 批评；评论；批判
📖 英文解释
the expression of disapproval of someone or something; the analysis and judgment of literary or artistic works
✏️ 例句
The plan received widespread criticism. She offered constructive criticism of the essay.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
literary criticism(文学评论); harsh criticism(严厉批评); face criticism(面临批评); constructive criticism(建设性批评); criticism of(对……的批评)
🧠 助记
critic(批评者) + ism(行为或学说) → 批评者的行为 → 批评
criticism::/ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/:n. 批评:The government's new policy drew heavy criticism from opposition leaders.
2. exchange /ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ/ v./n. 交换；兑换；交流
📖 英文解释
to give something and receive something else in its place; an act of giving and receiving
✏️ 例句
We exchanged phone numbers. The exchange rate is 1.2 dollars per euro.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
exchange rate(汇率); in exchange for(以……为交换); exchange ideas(交流想法); stock exchange(股票交易所); cultural exchange(文化交流)
🧠 助记
ex(出去) + change(变换) → 拿出去换来另一样 → 交换
exchange::/ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ/:v./n. 交换:A healthy exchange of ideas is essential for innovation and progress.
3. ship /ʃɪp/ n. 船舶；舰 v. 装运；运输
📖 英文解释
a large boat for transporting people or goods by sea; to send goods somewhere
✏️ 例句
The ship set sail at dawn. We can ship the order to your address.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
ship goods(装运货物); ocean liner(远洋轮船); shipping company(航运公司); shipment(装运;货物); cargo ship(货船)
🧠 助记
ship 与中文: 船 → 联想扬帆起航的大船
ship::/ʃɪp/:n. 船舶:The cargo ship was loaded with electronic goods destined for European markets.
4. label /ˈleɪbl/ n. 标签；标记 v. 加标签于；把……归类
📖 英文解释
a small piece of paper or other material attached to an object to give information; to fix a label to; to classify
✏️ 例句
The wine bottle has a French label. She was labeled a troublemaker at school.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
price label(价格标签); designer label(名牌); label as(把……标记为); food label(食品标签); warning label(警告标签)
🧠 助记
la(拉开) + bel → 拉开贴上的标记 → 标签
label::/ˈleɪbl/:n. 标签:It is unfair to label someone a failure based on a single setback.
5. differ /ˈdɪfər/ v. 与……不同；意见不同
📖 英文解释
to be unlike or dissimilar; to disagree
✏️ 例句
Our opinions differ on this matter. The two studies differ significantly in their methods.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
differ from(与……不同); differ in(在……方面不同); agree to differ(求同存异); differ on(在某事上意见不同); widely differ(差异巨大)
🧠 助记
di(两个) + ffer → 两个不一样 → 不同、有差异
differ::/ˈdɪfər/:v. 与……不同:The two experts differ significantly in their interpretation of the research data.
6. commit /kəˈmɪt/ v. 把……交托给；犯（罪）；承诺
📖 英文解释
to carry out a crime or mistake; to promise to do something; to send someone to prison or hospital
✏️ 例句
He committed a serious crime. She committed herself to the project.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
commit a crime(犯罪); commit to(承诺;致力于); commit suicide(自杀); commit resources(拨出资源); make a commitment(做出承诺)
🧠 助记
com(一起) + mit(送出) → 把自己送出去,投入其中 → 承诺;犯(罪)
commit::/kəˈmɪt/:v. 把……交托给:The organization has committed to reducing its carbon emissions by 50% by 2030.
7. admit /ədˈmɪt/ v. 承认；准许进入；接收入院
📖 英文解释
to confess to something; to allow someone to enter; to accept someone into a school or hospital
✏️ 例句
She admitted making a mistake. The university admitted 500 new students.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
admit to(承认); admit defeat(承认失败); admit into(接收进入); it must be admitted that(必须承认); admission fee(入场费/入学费)
🧠 助记
ad(向) + mit(送) → 向内送 → 准许进入;送出承认 → 承认
admit::/ədˈmɪt/:v. 承认:He finally admitted that he had been wrong about the situation all along.
8. objective /əbˈdʒektɪv/ adj. 客观的；不带主观感情的 n. 目的；目标
📖 英文解释
not influenced by personal feelings or opinions; existing independently of the mind; a goal or aim
✏️ 例句
Try to be objective in your assessment. Our main objective is to increase sales.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
objective analysis(客观分析); achieve an objective(达到目标); military objective(军事目标); objective evidence(客观证据); learning objective(学习目标)
🧠 助记
object(对象,客体) + ive → 从客体出发 → 客观的;目标(要达到的对象)
objective::/əbˈdʒektɪv/:adj. 客观的:The report aims to provide an objective assessment of the current economic situation.
9. contribution /ˌkɒntrɪˈbjuːʃn/ n. 贡献；捐款；投稿
📖 英文解释
something that you do or give to help produce or achieve something; a sum of money given
✏️ 例句
She made a significant contribution to science. Your contribution to the charity is appreciated.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
make a contribution(做出贡献); financial contribution(财务贡献); contribution to(对……的贡献); voluntary contribution(自愿捐款); academic contribution(学术贡献)
🧠 助记
contribute(贡献) + ion(名词后缀) → 贡献这件事 → 贡献、捐款
contribution::/ˌkɒntrɪˈbjuːʃn/:n. 贡献:Her contributions to medical research have saved countless lives worldwide.
10. context /ˈkɒntekst/ n. 上下文；环境；背景
📖 英文解释
the circumstances that form the setting for an event or idea; the parts of a text that surround a particular word or passage
✏️ 例句
You need to understand the context before drawing conclusions. Words change meaning with context.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in context(在背景下); out of context(断章取义); historical context(历史背景); put in context(放在背景中理解); cultural context(文化背景)
🧠 助记
con(共同,一起) + text(文本) → 围绕文本的环境 → 上下文、背景
context::/ˈkɒntekst/:n. 上下文:It is important to consider the historical context when analyzing political events.
11. complain /kəmˈpleɪn/ v. 抱怨；投诉；申诉
📖 英文解释
to say that you are unhappy or dissatisfied about something; to formally report a problem
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
complain about(抱怨……); complain to(向……投诉); complain that(抱怨说……); lodge a complaint(提出正式投诉); constant complaining(不停抱怨)
🧠 助记
com(一起) + plain(平白地说出来) → 平白说出不满 → 抱怨
complain::/kəmˈpleɪn/:v. 抱怨:Customers began to complain about the long wait times at the service counter.
12. fine /faɪn/ adj. 很好的；晴朗的；精细的 n./v. 罚款
📖 英文解释

fine::/faɪn/:adj. 很好的:She was issued a heavy fine for failing to declare her income accurately.

14. yield /jiːld/ v. 放弃；屈服；生产 n. 收益；产量

yield::/jiːld/:v. 放弃:The new farming techniques yielded a much larger harvest than the previous year.

video::/ˈvɪdioʊ/:n. 录像:The video footage clearly showed what had happened during the incident.

the city that functions as the seat of government; a letter of the alphabet in its larger form; money or assets used in business

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

20. circumstance /ˈsɜːrkəmstæns/ n. 情况；环境；事件（常用复数）
📖 英文解释
a fact or condition connected with or relevant to an event or action; one's financial and social situation
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

circumstance::/ˈsɜːrkəmstæns/:n. 情况:The judge took into account the defendant's difficult personal circumstances.

il(into) + lustr(光,如 luster) + ate → 用光照亮,使人看清楚 → 说明、图解
illustrate::/ˈɪləstreɪt/:v. 说明:This case study effectively illustrates the dangers of poor financial planning.
