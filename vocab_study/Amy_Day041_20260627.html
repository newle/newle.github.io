1. window /ˈwɪndoʊ/ n. 窗户；窗口；机会
📖 英文解释
an opening in a wall or screen to admit light or air; a transparent panel in a door; an opportunity
✏️ 例句
She looked out of the window at the rain. This is a window of opportunity.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
window of opportunity(机会窗口); stained glass window(彩色玻璃窗); window shopping(只看不买); shop window(橱窗); pop-up window(弹出窗口)
🧠 助记
wind(风) + ow → 风进来的地方 → 窗户
window::/ˈwɪndoʊ/:n. 窗户:This trade agreement opens a window of opportunity for small businesses to expand abroad.
2. alter /ˈɔːltər/ v. 改变；修改；变更
📖 英文解释
to change in character, appearance, or composition; to make different without completely changing
✏️ 例句
She altered the dress to fit her better. The experience altered his view of the world.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
alter a plan(修改计划); alter one's course(改变方向); alter the terms(修改条款); slightly alter(略微改变); radically alter(彻底改变)
🧠 助记
alt(其他,如 alternate) + er → 变成另一种样子 → 改变、修改
alter::/ˈɔːltər/:v. 改变:The discovery fundamentally altered scientists' understanding of the universe.
3. sensitive /ˈsensɪtɪv/ adj. 敏感的；易受伤害的；保密的
📖 英文解释
quick to detect or respond to slight changes; easily offended or upset; requiring careful handling
✏️ 例句
She is very sensitive to criticism. This is a sensitive political issue.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
sensitive issue(敏感话题); emotionally sensitive(情感敏感); sensitive data(敏感数据); sensitive skin(敏感肌肤); culturally sensitive(文化敏感的)
🧠 助记
sense(感觉) + itive → 感觉非常灵敏的 → 敏感的
sensitive::/ˈsensɪtɪv/:adj. 敏感的:Negotiators must be sensitive to the cultural differences between the two parties.
4. gather /ˈgæðər/ v. 聚集；搜集；推断；逐渐增多
📖 英文解释
to come together in a group; to collect or accumulate; to infer or understand from indirect evidence
✏️ 例句
Clouds were gathering on the horizon. She gathered all the evidence carefully.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
gather information(收集信息); gather around(聚集在……周围); gather momentum(积聚势头); gather strength(积聚力量); I gather that(我推断……)
🧠 助记
gath(get,获得) + er → 把东西都得到并聚在一起 → 聚集、收集
gather::/ˈgæðər/:v. 聚集:Researchers gathered extensive data over ten years to support their conclusions.
5. requirement /rɪˈkwaɪərmənt/ n. 要求；必要条件；必需品
📖 英文解释
something that is needed or obligatory; a condition that must be met
✏️ 例句
English is a requirement for the course. He meets all the requirements for the job.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
meet requirements(满足要求); academic requirement(学业要求); legal requirement(法律要求); minimum requirement(最低要求); satisfy requirements(符合要求)
🧠 助记
require(要求) + ment(名词后缀) → 被要求的事 → 要求、必要条件
requirement::/rɪˈkwaɪərmənt/:n. 要求:All applicants must meet the minimum academic requirements for admission.
6. critic /ˈkrɪtɪk/ n. 批评者；评论家
📖 英文解释
a person who expresses disapproval; a person who judges the merits of literary or artistic works
✏️ 例句
She is a film critic for a major newspaper. Critics praised the novel.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
film critic(影评人); harsh critic(严厉的批评者); social critic(社会批评家); become a critic of(成为……的批评者); literary critic(文学评论家)
🧠 助记
crit(判断,如 criterion) + ic(人) → 进行判断评价的人 → 评论家、批评者
critic::/ˈkrɪtɪk/:n. 批评者:Critics of the policy argued that it would increase inequality rather than reduce it.
7. property /ˈprɒpərti/ n. 财产；性质；特性
📖 英文解释
a thing or things belonging to someone; a building and the land it stands on; a quality or characteristic
✏️ 例句
The property was sold for a high price. Water has unique chemical properties.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
property market(房地产市场); intellectual property(知识产权); private property(私有财产); physical property(物理性质); property rights(产权)
🧠 助记
proper(适当的、自己的) + ty → 属于自己的东西 → 财产;物质的特性
property::/ˈprɒpərti/:n. 财产:Protecting intellectual property rights is crucial for encouraging innovation.
8. eliminate /ɪˈlɪmɪneɪt/ v. 排除；淘汰；消灭
📖 英文解释
to completely remove or get rid of something; to exclude from consideration; to knock out of a competition
✏️ 例句
We need to eliminate all risks. The team was eliminated in the first round.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
eliminate risk(消除风险); eliminate poverty(消除贫困); eliminate from(从……中排除); eliminate the possibility(排除可能性); systematic elimination(系统性消除)
🧠 助记
e(out) + limin(门槛,如 limit) + ate → 越过门槛赶出去 → 排除、消灭
eliminate::/ɪˈlɪmɪneɪt/:v. 排除:The new treatment aims to eliminate the virus completely from the patient's body.
9. artificial /ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃl/ adj. 人造的；人工的；做作的
📖 英文解释
made or produced by human beings rather than occurring naturally; not natural; lacking sincerity
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
artificial intelligence(人工智能); artificial light(人工照明); artificial flavor(人工香料); artificial turf(人工草皮); artificial sweetener(人工甜味剂)
🧠 助记
arti(手艺,如 art) + ficial(做成的) → 用手工艺做出来的 → 人造的
artificial::/ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃl/:adj. 人造的:Artificial intelligence is transforming industries from healthcare to transportation.
10. engineer /ˌendʒɪˈnɪər/ n. 工程师 v. 设计；策划；基因改造
📖 英文解释

✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

engineer::/ˌendʒɪˈnɪər/:n. 工程师 v. 设计:Engineers are working to develop more efficient and sustainable energy systems.
11. neither /ˈniːðər/ det./pron. 两个都不 conj. 也不
📖 英文解释
not one nor the other of two things; used to add a further negative statement
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

neither::/ˈniːðər/:det./pron. 两个都不 conj:Neither candidate managed to win an outright majority in the first round.

busy::/ˈbɪzi/:adj. 忙碌的:Despite her busy schedule, she always found time to volunteer in the community.
