1. register /ˈredʒɪstər/ v./n. 注册；登记；记录
📖 英文解释
To officially record a name or details in a list; a formal record or list.
✏️ 例句
All students must register for classes before the semester begins.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
register for（注册参加）；cash register（收银机）；registered nurse（注册护士）
🧠 助记
regis-ter → regi（王）+ ter（地方）→ 王家登记处 → 注册
register::/ˈredʒɪstər/:v./n. 注册:You need to register online to attend the conference.
2. declare /dɪˈkleər/ v. 宣布；申报；声明
📖 英文解释
To say something officially or formally; to announce openly.
✏️ 例句
The government declared a state of emergency after the earthquake.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
declare war（宣战）；declare independence（宣布独立）；declaration n. 声明；考研政治、历史类文章常见
🧠 助记
de-clare → clear（清晰）地说出来 → 宣布
declare::/dɪˈkleər/:v. 宣布:The company declared bankruptcy after years of losses.
3. everywhere /ˈevriwer/ adv. 到处；处处；各地
📖 英文解释
In every place; in all places.
✏️ 例句
Smartphones are everywhere now, changing the way we communicate.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
everywhere you look（放眼望去）；everywhere else（其他所有地方）；考研写作中常用来强调普遍性
🧠 助记
every + where → 每一个地方 → 到处
everywhere::/ˈevriwer/:adv. 到处:The influence of social media can be felt everywhere.
4. invent /ɪnˈvent/ v. 发明；捏造
📖 英文解释
To create or design something that has not existed before; to make up a false story.
✏️ 例句
Edison invented the phonograph, which could record and play back sound.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
invent a machine（发明机器）；invention n. 发明；inventor n. 发明家；inventive adj. 有发明才能的
🧠 助记
in-vent → 把 vent（通风口）装进去 → 发明通风装置 → 发明
invent::/ɪnˈvent/:v. 发明:The internet was not invented by any single person.
5. maybe /ˈmeɪbi/ adv. 也许；可能；大概
📖 英文解释
Used to suggest that something is possible but not certain.
✏️ 例句
Maybe we should reconsider our approach to this problem.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
= perhaps（考研写作替换词）；maybe not（也许不）；考研作文中表示不确定时常用
🧠 助记
may + be → 可能是（may be）→ 也许
maybe::/ˈmeɪbi/:adv. 也许:Maybe a different strategy would yield better results.
6. volume /ˈvɒljuːm/ n. 容积；音量；卷（书）；大量
📖 英文解释
The amount of space something occupies; the degree of loudness; a book forming part of a set.
✏️ 例句
The volume of international trade has increased significantly in recent years.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
volume of（大量的）；turn up/down the volume（调高/低音量）；考研经济、科技类文章常见
🧠 助记
vol（容）+ ume → 容量单位 → 容积、体积
volume::/ˈvɒljuːm/:n. 容积:The volume of data generated daily is staggering.
7. bar /bɑːr/ n. 条；酒吧；障碍 v. 禁止；阻拦
📖 英文解释
A long rigid piece of material; a place where alcoholic drinks are served; to prevent someone from doing something.
✏️ 例句
New regulations bar companies from using misleading advertising.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
bar sb from（禁止某人……）；behind bars（在监狱里）；bar chart（条形图）；考研法律类常见
🧠 助记
bar → 门栏（bar）拦住不让进 → 阻拦、禁止
bar::/bɑːr/:n. 条:He was barred from entering the country for five years.
8. east /iːst/ n./adj./adv. 东；东方的；向东
📖 英文解释
The direction toward the sunrise; the eastern part of a region.
✏️ 例句
Relations between the East and the West have long been complex.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
the Middle East（中东）；East Asia（东亚）；go east（向东走）；考研地理、政治类文章常见
🧠 助记
east → 'e'像日出的形状，太阳从东方升起
east::/iːst/:n./adj./adv. 东:East-West cultural exchange has accelerated in recent decades.
9. resume /rɪˈzjuːm/ v. 重新开始；恢复 n. 简历（/ˈrezjumeɪ/）
📖 英文解释
To begin again after a pause; a brief summary of one's career and qualifications.
✏️ 例句
The peace talks were expected to resume after the ceasefire agreement.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
resume work/talks（恢复工作/谈判）；résumé（简历，重音在第一音节）；考研写作常用
🧠 助记
re-sume → re（再）+ sume（假设继续）→ 重新继续
resume::/rɪˈzjuːm/:v. 重新开始:Negotiations resumed after a three-day break.
10. adolescent /ˌædəˈlesnt/ n./adj. 青少年；青春期的
📖 英文解释
A young person who is developing into an adult, typically between the ages of 13 and 18.
✏️ 例句
Adolescents today face unprecedented pressure from social media.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
adolescent behavior（青少年行为）；adolescence n. 青春期；考研教育、心理类阅读常见
🧠 助记
adol-escent → adol（成长中）+ escent（变化中）→ 正在成长的年龄
adolescent::/ˌædəˈlesnt/:n./adj. 青少年:Adolescent mental health has become a major social concern.
11. dance /dɑːns/ v./n. 跳舞；舞蹈；舞会
📖 英文解释
To move the body rhythmically to music; a series of movements performed to music.
✏️ 例句
Dance is used in many cultures as a way to tell stories and express emotions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
dance to the music（随音乐起舞）；dance around an issue（回避问题）；考研文化类文章常见
🧠 助记
dance → d + ance → 'ance'后缀表动作，跳舞就是一种 ance（动作）
dance::/dɑːns/:v./n. 跳舞:Traditional dances reflect a culture's history and values.
12. hill /hɪl/ n. 小山；山丘；坡
📖 英文解释
A naturally raised area of land, not as high as a mountain.
✏️ 例句
The village was perched on a hill overlooking a wide valley.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
over the hill（过了全盛期）；uphill（上坡，困难的）；考研地理、文学类文章常见
🧠 助记
hill → 像字母'h'站在地上，中间隆起的就是山丘
hill::/hɪl/:n. 小山:The uphill battle for reform requires sustained effort.
13. season /ˈsiːzn/ n. 季节；赛季 v. 使有味道；使适应
📖 英文解释
One of the four periods of the year; to add flavor to food; to make more experienced.
✏️ 例句
The rainy season in this region usually lasts from June to September.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in season（当季的）；off season（淡季）；season ticket（季票）；考研环境、体育类文章常见
🧠 助记
sea + son → 大海的儿子经历四季 → 季节
season::/ˈsiːzn/:n. 季节:Tourism peaks during the holiday season every year.
14. nevertheless /ˌnevəðəˈles/ adv./conj. 然而；尽管如此；不过
📖 英文解释
In spite of what has just been said or referred to; however.
✏️ 例句
The task was difficult; nevertheless, the team managed to finish on time.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
= nonetheless（考研写作同义词）；用于转折，比 but 更正式；考研写作中体现逻辑转折的高频连接词
🧠 助记
never + the + less → 永远不要少一点 → 尽管如此还是坚持
nevertheless::/ˌnevəðəˈles/:adv./conj. 然而:The evidence was inconclusive; nevertheless, action was taken.
15. congress /ˈkɒŋɡrəs/ n. 国会；代表大会；会议
📖 英文解释
The national legislative body of the USA; a formal meeting or series of meetings.
✏️ 例句
Congress passed a new bill to strengthen environmental regulations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
US Congress（美国国会）；congressional adj. 国会的；考研政治、美国文化类文章必备词
🧠 助记
con-gress → con（一起）+ gress（走）→ 一起走来开会 → 国会
congress::/ˈkɒŋɡrəs/:n. 国会:Congress voted to increase funding for public education.
16. fifty /ˈfɪfti/ num. 五十
📖 英文解释
The number equivalent to the product of five and ten; 50.
✏️ 例句
Over fifty countries signed the international climate agreement.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
fifty-fifty（各半；对半）；the Fifty States（美国五十州）；fifty percent（百分之五十）
🧠 助记
fif-ty → five + ty（十倍后缀）→ 五×十 = 五十
fifty::/ˈfɪfti/:num. 五十:Fifty percent of respondents supported the new policy.
17. urgent /ˈɜːrdʒənt/ adj. 紧迫的；紧急的；强烈要求的
📖 英文解释
Requiring immediate action or attention; very important and needing to be dealt with quickly.
✏️ 例句
The doctor made an urgent call for more blood donations at the hospital.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
urgent matter/need（紧急事项/迫切需要）；urgency n. 紧迫性；urge v. 强烈要求；考研写作高频词
🧠 助记
ur-gent → urge（冲动）+ ent → 有冲动想立刻做的 → 紧迫的
urgent::/ˈɜːrdʒənt/:adj. 紧迫的:There is an urgent need to address climate change now.
18. category /ˈkætəɡɔːri/ n. 类别；种类；范畴
📖 英文解释
A class or division of people or things regarded as having particular shared characteristics.
✏️ 例句
The awards were divided into three main categories: fiction, non-fiction, and poetry.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
fall into a category（属于某一类别）；categorize v. 分类；categorical adj. 明确的；考研学术类文章常见
🧠 助记
cate-gory → cat（猫）+ gory（故事）→ 按故事中的猫来分类 → 类别
category::/ˈkætəɡɔːri/:n. 类别:Art can be divided into broad categories like visual and performing.
19. infant /ˈɪnfənt/ n. 婴幼儿；幼儿 adj. 初期的
📖 英文解释
A very young child or baby; relating to the beginning or early stage of something.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
infant mortality（婴儿死亡率）；infant school（幼儿园）；infancy n. 幼年；考研社会、医学类文章常见
🧠 助记
in-fant → 'in'（进来）+ 'fant'（幻想）→ 刚进入世界的幻想者 → 婴儿
infant::/ˈɪnfənt/:n. 婴幼儿:Infant nutrition programs have improved outcomes in rural areas.
20. convenient /kənˈviːniənt/ adj. 方便的；便利的；合适的
📖 英文解释
Fitting in well with a person's needs, activities, and plans; easy to use or access.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
convenient for（对……方便）；convenience n. 方便；convenience store（便利店）；考研写作常用词
🧠 助记
con-ven-ient → convention（惯例）的地方当然方便 → 方便的
convenient::/kənˈviːniənt/:adj. 方便的:Digital tools make remote work far more convenient.
21. battle /ˈbætl/ n./v. 战斗；奋斗；斗争

battle::/ˈbætl/:n./v. 战斗:The battle against poverty requires global cooperation.
22. vulnerable /ˈvʌlnərəbl/ adj. 易受攻击的；脆弱的；容易受影响的
📖 英文解释
Susceptible to physical or emotional harm; open to attack or criticism.
✏️ 例句

vulnerable::/ˈvʌlnərəbl/:adj. 易受攻击的:Policies should prioritize protection of vulnerable groups.
23. committee /kəˈmɪti/ n. 委员会；全体委员
📖 英文解释
A group of people appointed for a specific function, typically acting on behalf of a larger body.
✏️ 例句

committee::/kəˈmɪti/:n. 委员会:The committee approved the budget after a lengthy debate.
