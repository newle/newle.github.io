1. contest /ˈkɒntest/ (n.) /kənˈtest/ (v.) n. 比赛；竞争 v. 竞争；对……提出异议
📖 英文解释
An event in which people compete for supremacy; to compete for something; to dispute or challenge.
✏️ 例句
She entered the singing contest and won first prize.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：enter a contest（参加比赛）；contest an election（参选；对选举提出异议）；contest results（对结果提出异议）
🧠 助记
con（共同）+ test（测试）→ 大家一起测试比拼 → 比赛
contest::/ˈkɒntest/ (n.) /kənˈtest/ (v.):n. 比赛:contest the validity of research findings
2. illegal /ɪˈliːɡ(ə)l/ adj. 不合法的；非法的
📖 英文解释
Contrary to or forbidden by law; not authorized or sanctioned by law.
✏️ 例句
It is illegal to drive without a seatbelt in most countries.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：illegal immigration（非法移民）；illegal trade（非法贸易）；make sth. illegal（将……列为非法）；illegally adv.
🧠 助记
il（否定）+ legal（合法的）→ 不合法的 → 非法的
illegal::/ɪˈliːɡ(ə)l/:adj. 不合法的:crack down on illegal dumping of industrial waste
3. zone /zəʊn/ n. 区域；地带；时区 v. 分区
📖 英文解释
An area or stretch of land having a particular characteristic, purpose, or use; to divide into or assign to zones.
✏️ 例句
The factory is located in an industrial zone.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：time zone（时区）；comfort zone（舒适区）；economic zone（经济区）；zone in on（聚焦于）
🧠 助记
联想 zone = 划定的范围 → 区域、地带
zone::/zəʊn/:n. 区域:establish an exclusive economic zone
4. besides /bɪˈsaɪdz/ prep./adv. 除了……之外；而且；此外
📖 英文解释
In addition to; furthermore; apart from.
✏️ 例句
Besides English, she speaks French and Spanish.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：besides that（除此之外）；besides doing sth.（除了做某事之外）；区别 except（排除在外）、in addition to（加在其上）
🧠 助记
beside（在旁边）+ s → 在旁边还有其他的 → 此外、除了之外
besides::/bɪˈsaɪdz/:prep./adv. 除了……之外:besides economic growth, social welfare must improve
5. philosophy /fɪˈlɒsəfɪ/ n. 哲学；人生哲学；世界观
📖 英文解释
The study of fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, values, reason, and mind; a theory or set of beliefs.
✏️ 例句
Her philosophy in life is to treat others as you want to be treated.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：political/moral philosophy（政治/道德哲学）；philosophy of education（教育哲学）；philosophical adj.；philosopher n.
🧠 助记
philo（爱）+ sophy（智慧，如 sophisticated）→ 热爱智慧 → 哲学
philosophy::/fɪˈlɒsəfɪ/:n. 哲学:the philosophy of pragmatism emphasizes practical outcomes
6. modify /ˈmɒdɪfaɪ/ v. 修改；调整；缓和
📖 英文解释
To make partial or minor changes to something; to make less extreme or severe.
✏️ 例句
The government modified its policy after public criticism.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：modify a plan/behavior（修改计划/行为）；genetically modified（基因改造的）；modification n.；modifier n.（修饰语）
🧠 助记
mod（改变）+ ify（使……）→ 使它改变 → 修改
modify::/ˈmɒdɪfaɪ/:v. 修改:modify teaching methods to suit individual learners
7. rural /ˈrʊər(ə)l/ adj. 农村的；乡村的；田园的
📖 英文解释
In, relating to, or characteristic of the countryside rather than the town.
✏️ 例句
Many young people are leaving rural areas to find work in cities.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：rural area（农村地区）；rural population（农村人口）；rural development（乡村发展）；反义词 urban（城市的）
🧠 助记
rur（乡村，拉丁词根）+ al → 乡村的
rural::/ˈrʊər(ə)l/:adj. 农村的:rural poverty remains a challenge in developing countries
8. god /ɡɒd/ n. 神；上帝；偶像
📖 英文解释
A superhuman being worshipped as having power over nature; (in Christianity) the creator of the universe; a greatly admired person.
✏️ 例句
Many cultures believe in the existence of a god or gods.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：belief in God（信仰上帝）；god-like（神一样的）；act of God（天灾）；godfather（教父）
🧠 助记
god → 与 good（好）谐音 → 神明是至善的
god::/ɡɒd/:n. 神:freedom of religion includes the right not to believe in god
9. reluctant /rɪˈlʌkt(ə)nt/ adj. 不情愿的；勉强的
📖 英文解释
Unwilling and hesitant; disinclined to do something.
✏️ 例句
She was reluctant to accept the job offer because it required relocation.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：be reluctant to do sth.（不愿意做某事）；reluctant agreement（勉强同意）；reluctance n.；reluctantly adv.
🧠 助记
re（反）+ luct（挣扎）+ ant → 反复挣扎不想做 → 不情愿的
reluctant::/rɪˈlʌkt(ə)nt/:adj. 不情愿的:governments are often reluctant to impose austerity measures
10. sharp /ʃɑːp/ adj. 尖的；清晰的；急剧的；敏锐的
📖 英文解释
Having a thin cutting edge or fine point; (of an image) clear and distinct; (of a change) sudden and marked.
✏️ 例句
There was a sharp rise in prices last month.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：sharp increase/decline（急剧增长/下降）；sharp contrast（鲜明对比）；sharp mind（敏锐的头脑）；sharp criticism（尖锐批评）
🧠 助记
sharp 尖锐 → 像刀刃一样锋利、清晰、尖锐
sharp::/ʃɑːp/:adj. 尖的:a sharp rise in unemployment triggered social unrest
11. adequate /ˈædɪkwət/ adj. 充足的；足够的；适当的
📖 英文解释
Satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity; enough for a particular purpose.
✏️ 例句
We need to ensure all children have access to adequate nutrition.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：adequate funding/resources（充足的资金/资源）；adequate response（适当的回应）；inadequate adj.（不足的）；adequacy n.
🧠 助记
ad（到）+ equ（相等）+ ate → 达到等量要求 → 足够的、充足的
adequate::/ˈædɪkwət/:adj. 充足的:ensure adequate healthcare for all citizens
12. milk /mɪlk/ n. 牛奶；乳汁 v. 挤奶；榨取
📖 英文解释
A white liquid produced by cows and other animals as food; to draw milk from an animal; to exploit someone.
✏️ 例句
The global demand for milk products has increased significantly.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研语境：milk production（牛奶生产）；milk the situation（榨取利益）；dairy industry（乳业）；milk quotas（牛奶配额）
🧠 助记
基础词 milk 牛奶 → 也引申为榨取（like milking a cow）
milk::/mɪlk/:n. 牛奶:milk powder is a major export for New Zealand
13. mystery /ˈmɪst(ə)rɪ/ n. 神秘；谜；神秘事物
📖 英文解释
Something that is difficult or impossible to understand or explain; a secret or unexplained matter; a genre of fiction.
✏️ 例句
The disappearance of the aircraft remains a mystery.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：remain a mystery（仍是谜）；unsolved mystery（未解之谜）；mystery novel（推理小说）；mysterious adj.
🧠 助记
myst（神秘，见 mystical）+ ery → 神秘事物 → 谜
mystery::/ˈmɪst(ə)rɪ/:n. 神秘:the origin of the universe remains a profound mystery
14. literacy /ˈlɪt(ə)rəsɪ/ n. 读写能力；文化水平；素养
📖 英文解释
The ability to read and write; competence or knowledge in a specified area.
✏️ 例句
Improving literacy rates is a priority for developing nations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：literacy rate（识字率）；digital literacy（数字素养）；functional literacy（实用识字能力）；literate adj.
🧠 助记
liter（文字，如 literature 文学）+ acy → 与文字相关的能力 → 读写能力
literacy::/ˈlɪt(ə)rəsɪ/:n. 读写能力:promote financial literacy among young people
15. emergency /ɪˈmɜːdʒ(ə)nsɪ/ n. 紧急情况；突发事件 adj. 紧急的
📖 英文解释
A serious, unexpected, and often dangerous situation requiring immediate action.
✏️ 例句
The hospital has a dedicated emergency department.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：state of emergency（紧急状态）；emergency measure（紧急措施）；declare a national emergency（宣布国家紧急状态）；emergency services
🧠 助记
emerge（浮现）+ ency → 突然浮现出来的危险情况 → 紧急情况
emergency::/ɪˈmɜːdʒ(ə)nsɪ/:n. 紧急情况:declare a state of emergency following the natural disaster
16. bridge /brɪdʒ/ n. 桥；桥梁 v. 架桥；跨越（差异）

Education can serve as a bridge between social classes.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：bridge the gap（弥合差距）；bridge differences（跨越分歧）；suspension bridge（悬索桥）；bridge the divide（消除对立）
🧠 助记
bridge 桥 → 连接两岸 → 也比喻弥合差距
bridge::/brɪdʒ/:n. 桥:bridge the digital divide between urban and rural areas

基础词 grass 草 → 延伸到基层、根基（grass roots）
grass::/ɡrɑːs/:n. 草:grass-roots activism drives political change

The abolition of the slave trade was a major historical milestone.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：slave trade（奴隶贸易）；slave labor（奴役劳动）；slave to（沦为……的奴隶）；slavery n.；abolish slavery（废除奴隶制）
🧠 助记
slave 奴隶 → 被迫为人服务 → 也比喻沉迷于某事
slave::/sleɪv/:n. 奴隶:the legacy of slavery continues to affect modern society
19. privilege /ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ/ n. 特权；优先权；荣幸
📖 英文解释
A special right, advantage, or immunity available only to a particular person or group; an advantage that most people do not have.
✏️ 例句
Access to quality education should be a right, not a privilege.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：privilege of（……的特权）；check your privilege（反思特权）；privileged background（特权背景）；privileged adj.
🧠 助记
privi（私人）+ lege（法律）→ 私人的法律权利 → 特权
privilege::/ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ/:n. 特权:challenge systems that protect privilege over merit
20. restore /rɪˈstɔː/ v. 恢复；归还；修复
📖 英文解释
To bring back to a former condition or position; to return something that was lost or taken; to repair a building or work of art.
✏️ 例句
The government promised to restore order after the riots.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：restore confidence/order/peace（恢复信心/秩序/和平）；restore to（归还给）；restoration n.
🧠 助记
re（再次）+ store（存储）→ 再次放回原处 → 恢复、归还
restore::/rɪˈstɔː/:v. 恢复:restore public trust in democratic institutions
21. upset /ˈʌpset/ (n./adj.) /ʌpˈset/ (v.) adj. 沮丧的；难过的 v. 打乱；使不安 n. 意外的结果
📖 英文解释
Unhappy, disappointed, or worried; to make someone feel unhappy or disappointed; to knock something over.
✏️ 例句
She was upset by his rude comment.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：be upset about/over（对……感到沮丧）；upset the balance（打破平衡）；upset stomach（肠胃不适）；upsets plans（打乱计划）
🧠 助记
up（颠倒）+ set（设定）→ 把原来设定好的颠倒了 → 打乱；不安
upset::/ˈʌpset/ (n./adj.) /ʌpˈset/ (v.):adj. 沮丧的:sudden policy changes upset long-term business planning

📖 英文解释

✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：international counterpart（国际同行）；private sector counterpart（私营部门对应机构）；counterpart in（在……中的对应者）
🧠 助记
counter（对面）+ part（部分）→ 对面的相应部分 → 对等物、同行
counterpart::/ˈkaʊntəpɑːt/:n. 对应的人或物:the minister met with her European counterpart

ac（加强）+ celer（速度）+ ate → 加快速度 → 加速
accelerate::/əkˈseləreɪt/:v. 加速:digital technology accelerates economic transformation

false::/fɔːls/:adj. 错误的:spread false information to manipulate public opinion
