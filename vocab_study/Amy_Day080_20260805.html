1. obscure /əbˈskjʊə(r)/ adj. 不知名的；晦涩的；模糊的；v. 使难理解；遮蔽
📖 英文解释
Not discovered or known about; not clearly expressed or understood; to make unclear.
✏️ 例句
The report was written in such obscure language that few readers could understand its key findings.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ an obscure writer / figure 名不见经传的作家/人物 ★ obscure the truth / issue 遮掩真相/问题（写作常用） ★ obscurity n. 默默无闻；晦涩 辨析：obscure / vague：obscure 侧重不知名或难以理解；vague 侧重模糊不清晰
🧠 助记
ob（遮住）+ scure（secure清晰）——把清晰遮住，就是晦涩/不知名的
obscure::/əbˈskjʊə(r)/:adj. 不知名的:The new regulation was written in obscure legal language.
2. faith /feɪθ/ n. 信念；信仰；信任
📖 英文解释
Complete trust or confidence in someone or something; a strong belief in a religion.
✏️ 例句
Citizens' faith in government institutions has declined sharply over the past decade.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ have faith in sb/sth 对……有信心（写作高频） ★ in good faith 诚心诚意地（act in good faith） ★ keep faith with sb 对某人守信 ★ faithful adj. 忠实的；faithfully adv.
🧠 助记
faith 谐音「肺思」——发自肺腑的思念与信任，就是信念/信仰
faith::/feɪθ/:n. 信念:She has great faith in her team's ability to succeed.
3. shock /ʃɒk/ n. 震惊；冲击；休克；v. 使震惊
📖 英文解释
A sudden upsetting disturbance to a person's emotions; a violent shaking impact; to cause someone to feel very surprised and upset.
✏️ 例句
The sudden economic shock caused by the pandemic disrupted supply chains across the globe.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ economic / culture shock 经济冲击/文化冲击 ★ in shock 处于震惊中 ★ shocking adj. 令人震惊的；shock wave 冲击波 ★ be shocked at/by 对……感到震惊
🧠 助记
shock 谐音「傻壳」——傻了壳了，就是被震惊了
shock::/ʃɒk/:n. 震惊:The news came as a shock to everyone in the office.
4. strive /straɪv/ v. 努力；奋斗；力求
📖 英文解释
To make great efforts to achieve or obtain something; to struggle or fight vigorously.
✏️ 例句
Governments around the world must strive to balance economic development with environmental protection.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ strive for sth 为……而努力（strive for excellence / equality） ★ strive to do sth 努力做某事（写作高频） ★ striving n. 奋斗；strive after sth 力求得到某事物
🧠 助记
strive 谐音「死拼儿」——死命拼，就是努力奋斗
strive::/straɪv/:v. 努力:We must strive for a more just and equal society.
5. grasp /ɡrɑːsp/ v. 抓住；理解；领会；n. 抓握；理解力
📖 英文解释
To seize and hold firmly; to understand something fully; the ability to understand or achieve something.
✏️ 例句
A firm grasp of historical context is essential for understanding modern international relations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ grasp an opportunity 抓住机会（写作常用） ★ grasp the idea / concept 理解概念 ★ within one's grasp 在某人能力范围内 ★ beyond sb's grasp 超出理解/能力范围
🧠 助记
grasp 谐音「抓死泼」——抓住不放，就是grasp（抓住/理解）
grasp::/ɡrɑːsp/:v. 抓住:She grasped the key concepts quickly.

Strong and barely controllable emotion; an intense enthusiasm for something.

Her passion for social justice drove her to dedicate her career to human rights advocacy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ passion for sth 对……的热情（passion for learning / music） ★ with passion 充满激情地（写作增色词） ★ passionate adj. 充满热情的（be passionate about）
🧠 助记
passion 谐音「泼神」——泼出来的神力，就是激情
passion::/ˈpæʃn/:n. 激情:He pursued his passion for music throughout his life.
7. settlement /ˈsetlmənt/ n. 解决；定居；定居点；协议
📖 英文解释
An official agreement that ends a dispute; the action of settling in a new place; a place where people establish a community.
✏️ 例句
The two companies reached an out-of-court settlement to avoid a lengthy and costly legal battle.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ reach a settlement 达成和解（法律/外交高频） ★ peace settlement 和平协议 ★ settlement of a dispute 争端解决 ★ settler n. 定居者（历史语境）
🧠 助记
settle（解决/定居）+ ment（名词后缀）——解决/定居这件事，就是settlement
settlement::/ˈsetlmənt/:n. 解决:The two sides reached a peaceful settlement.
8. sudden /ˈsʌdn/ adj. 突然的；意想不到的
📖 英文解释
Happening or coming unexpectedly, or without warning.
✏️ 例句
The sudden change in government policy left many businesses uncertain about their future plans.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ all of a sudden 突然（口语/写作常用，替换suddenly） ★ sudden death 猝死；加时赛（体育） ★ suddenly adv. 突然地 ★ sudden change / drop 突然的变化/下降
🧠 助记
sudden 谐音「撒顿」——嗙一撒，顿时发生，就是突然
sudden::/ˈsʌdn/:adj. 突然的:Her sudden resignation shocked the entire company.

📖 英文解释
The scientific study of living organisms; the biological features and characteristics of something.
✏️ 例句
Advances in molecular biology have revolutionized our understanding of genetic diseases.

★ biology lab / class 生物实验室/课 ★ marine / molecular biology 海洋/分子生物学 ★ biological adj. 生物的；biological weapon 生物武器 ★ biologist n. 生物学家
🧠 助记
bio（life生命）+ logy（学科）——研究生命的学科，就是生物学
biology::/baɪˈɒlədʒi/:n. 生物学:She studies biology at a leading research university.
10. parliament /ˈpɑːləmənt/ n. 议会；国会
📖 英文解释
The highest law-making body in a country, consisting of elected representatives.
✏️ 例句
The bill was passed by parliament after months of heated debate among representatives.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ member of parliament (MP) 议员（英国） ★ parliamentary adj. 议会的（parliamentary democracy） ★ European Parliament 欧洲议会 ★ 注意：美国用 Congress，英国用 Parliament
🧠 助记
parlia（parler说话）+ ment——大家集在一起说话的地方，就是议会
parliament::/ˈpɑːləmənt/:n. 议会:The new law was debated and passed by parliament.
11. map /mæp/ n. 地图；图谱；v. 绘制地图；规划

A diagram of an area showing geographical features; to record or plan in detail.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ map out a plan / strategy 规划计划/策略（写作高频比喻义） ★ road map 路线图；改革路径图（比喻） ★ mind map 思维导图 ★ put sth on the map 使……出名
🧠 助记
map 像地图的M形——像山脉的起伏，就是地图
map::/mæp/:n. 地图:The government has mapped out a plan for economic recovery.
12. penalty /ˈpenəlti/ n. 处罚；罚款；点球

✏️ 例句
The court imposed a heavy financial penalty on the company for violating environmental regulations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ impose / face a penalty 施加/面临处罚 ★ death penalty 死刑（考研社会议题热词） ★ penalty kick 点球（足球） ★ under penalty of 以……为处罚
🧠 助记
penal（刑罚的）+ ty——刑罚的结果，就是处罚/罚款
penalty::/ˈpenəlti/:n. 处罚:Companies face heavy penalties for violating the regulations.
13. tooth /tuːθ/ n. 牙齿（复数 teeth）；齿轮齿

The legislation lacks the teeth needed to actually enforce compliance among major polluters.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ tooth and nail 拼命地（fight tooth and nail） ★ give sth teeth 使……有约束力（写作比喻用法） ★ sweet tooth 嗜甜的人 ★ toothless 没有牙齿的；没有约束力的（比喻）
🧠 助记
tooth 谐音「图思」——图它思想，牙齿咬住问题，就是teeth（力量）
tooth::/tuːθ/:n. 牙齿（复数 teeth）:The new law needs more teeth to be effective.

★ yesterday's news 昨日黄花，过时的事物（比喻） ★ not born yesterday 不是天真/容易被骗的（口语） ★ as recently as yesterday 就在昨天（强调之近）
🧠 助记
yester（过去的）+ day（天）——过去的那一天，就是昨天
yesterday::/ˈjestədeɪ/:n./adv. 昨天:Yesterday's solutions may not work for today's problems.
15. distract /dɪˈstrækt/ v. 使分心；使转移注意力
📖 英文解释
To prevent someone from giving their full attention to something; to divert attention.
✏️ 例句
Constant notifications from smartphones distract students from focusing on their academic work.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ distract sb from sth 使某人从……分心（写作高频） ★ distraction n. 分心；令人分心的事物 ★ be distracted by 被……分散注意力 辨析：distract / disrupt：distract 侧重分散注意力；disrupt 侧重打断/破坏
🧠 助记
dis（分散）+ tract（拉/拖）——把注意力拉散，就是使分心
distract::/dɪˈstrækt/:v. 使分心:Loud music can distract students from studying.

enemy::/ˈenəmi/:n. 敌人:Procrastination is the enemy of success.
17. abstract /ˈæbstrækt/ (n./adj.) /æbˈstrækt/ (v.) adj. 抽象的；n. 摘要；抽象概念；v. 提取
📖 英文解释
Existing in thought or as an idea but not having physical existence; a summary of a piece of writing.
✏️ 例句
The concept of justice is abstract, yet it serves as the foundation of every legal and ethical system.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ abstract idea / concept 抽象概念 ★ abstract (of a paper) 论文摘要（学术阅读必知） ★ in the abstract 抽象地，脱离实际地 辨析：abstract / concrete：abstract 抽象的；concrete 具体的（考研常见对比）
🧠 助记
ab（离开）+ stract（拉/draw）——从具体事物中抽离出来，就是抽象
abstract::/ˈæbstrækt/ (n./adj.) /æbˈstrækt/ (v.):adj. 抽象的:The report begins with a short abstract of the main findings.

automobile::/ˈɔːtəməbiːl/:n. 汽车（正式/美式）:The automobile industry is shifting toward electric vehicles.
