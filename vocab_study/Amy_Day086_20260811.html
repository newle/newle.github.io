1. coal /kəʊl/ n. 煤；煤炭
📖 英文解释
A hard black mineral used as fuel; pieces of this mineral.
✏️ 例句
Coal was the primary source of energy during the Industrial Revolution.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ coal mine/mining 煤矿/煤炭开采 ★ coal-fired power plant 燃煤发电厂 ☆ 考研环境类文章：coal → carbon emission → climate change
🧠 助记
coal 谐音可奥 = 可以燃烧的黑石头 = 煤
coal::/kəʊl/:n. 煤:The country is gradually replacing coal with renewable energy sources.
2. setback /ˈsetbæk/ n. 挫折；阻碍
📖 英文解释
A reversal or check in progress; a problem that delays or prevents progress.
✏️ 例句
The company suffered a major setback when its leading product was recalled.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ suffer/face a setback 遭受挫折 ★ a temporary setback 暂时的挫折 ☆ set back v. 推迟；使受挫(注意 set back 动词短语) 辨析：setback / obstacle / barrier：setback 强调前进中的倒退；obstacle 指障碍物；barrier 指结构性障碍
🧠 助记
set(设置) + back(后退) = 被打回去 = 挫折
setback::/ˈsetbæk/:n. 挫折:Despite the early setback, the team remained committed to achieving its goals.
3. deadline /ˈdedlaɪn/ n. 最后期限；截止时间
📖 英文解释
A time by which something must be done.
✏️ 例句
She worked through the night to meet the project deadline.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ meet/miss a deadline 赶上/错过截止时间 ★ set a deadline 设定截止期限 ★ deadline pressure 截止期限压力 ☆ dead(死) + line = 不能越过的死亡线 = 截止期限
🧠 助记
dead(死) + line(线) = 越过这条线就没救了 = 最后期限
deadline::/ˈdedlaɪn/:n. 最后期限:The government set a firm deadline for the completion of the project.
4. spelling /ˈspelɪŋ/ n. 拼写；拼法
📖 英文解释
The process or activity of writing or naming letters in order; the way a word is spelled.
✏️ 例句
Good spelling is considered a basic requirement in professional writing.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ correct spelling 正确拼写 ★ spelling mistake/error 拼写错误 ☆ spell v. 拼写；speller n. 拼写者
🧠 助记
spell(拼写) + ing = 进行拼写的动作/能力 = 拼写
spelling::/ˈspelɪŋ/:n. 拼写:Many students struggle with the spelling of irregular English words.
5. hinder /ˈhɪndə(r)/ v. 阻碍；妨碍；adj. 后面的
📖 英文解释
To make it difficult for someone to do something; to slow down.
✏️ 例句
Poor infrastructure continues to hinder economic development in the region.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ hinder sb from doing sth 阻止某人做某事 ★ hinder progress/development 阻碍进步/发展 ☆ hindrance n. 阻碍；hindering adj. 阻碍性的 辨析：hinder / prevent / obstruct：hinder 最温和，使困难；prevent 完全阻止；obstruct 正式阻挡
🧠 助记
hind(后面) + er = 把人往后拖 = 阻碍
hinder::/ˈhɪndə(r)/:v. 阻碍:Bureaucratic red tape often hinders the development of new businesses.
6. ear /ɪə(r)/ n. 耳朵；听觉；麦穗
📖 英文解释
The organ of hearing; the ability to hear well; a head of grain.
✏️ 例句
She has a good ear for music and can recognize melodies instantly.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ have an ear for 对……有天赋 ★ keep an ear to the ground 注意风吹草动 ★ turn a deaf ear to 充耳不闻 ☆ earring n. 耳环；eardrum n. 鼓膜
🧠 助记
ear = 耳(形状像耳朵的符号) = 耳朵
ear::/ɪə(r)/:n. 耳朵:A good writer has an ear for the natural rhythms of spoken language.

A period devoted to a particular activity; a meeting of an official body.

★ training/study session 训练/学习阶段 ★ parliamentary session 议会会期 ★ recording/photo session 录音/拍照场次 ☆ 考研：academic session 学期；session law 会议法令

session = 坐(sess) + ion = 大家坐在一起开会 = 会议/阶段
session::/ˈseʃn/:n. 会议:The brainstorming session produced several creative solutions.

narrative::/ˈnærətɪv/:n. 叙述:The politician sought to control the public narrative about the scandal.

dislike::/dɪsˈlaɪk/:v. 不喜欢:His dislike for formal meetings was evident from his frequent absences.

semester::/sɪˈmestə(r)/:n. 学期(半年制):Students must register for their courses before the semester begins.

16. hence /hens/ adv. 因此；从此；今后
📖 英文解释
As a consequence; as a result; from this time.
✏️ 例句
The new policy reduced costs, and hence increased profitability.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ hence the name 因此得名 ★ hence it follows that 由此可推出 ☆ 写作高频词：hence = therefore / thus / consequently 辨析：hence / therefore / thus：三者均表因此；hence 最书面；therefore 较通用；thus 偏正式
🧠 助记
hence = 因此(henc 谐音很可) = 很可以直接推出结论 = 因此
hence::/hens/:adv. 因此:The evidence was inconclusive; hence, no charges were filed.

19. forever /fərˈevə(r)/ adv. 永远；总是；n. 永远

for + ever(永远) = 为了永远 = 永远
forever::/fərˈevə(r)/:adv. 永远:The invention of the internet changed human communication forever.

cens(计数) + us = 对我们所有人进行计数 = 人口普查
census::/ˈsensəs/:n. 人口普查:The census revealed a significant shift in the country's demographic structure.
