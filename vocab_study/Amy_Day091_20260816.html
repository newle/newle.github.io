1. sequence /ˈsiːkwəns/ n. 顺序；序列；一连串
📖 英文解释
A particular order in which events follow each other; a set of related events or things in order.
✏️ 例句
The steps must be followed in the correct sequence for the experiment to succeed.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：in sequence (按顺序)；out of sequence (顺序混乱)；DNA sequence (DNA序列)；time sequence (时间顺序)；sequential adj. 顺序的。
🧠 助记
seque(跟随，来自拉丁 sequi) + nce → 一个跟随另一个 → 顺序/序列。联想 sequel(续集)，按顺序的下一集。
sequence::/ˈsiːkwəns/:n. 顺序:Scientists mapped the complete genetic sequence of the human genome.
2. systematic /ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk/ adj. 系统的；有条不紊的；有计划的
📖 英文解释
Done or acting according to a fixed plan or system; methodical and thorough.
✏️ 例句
A systematic review of all available evidence is necessary before drawing conclusions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频：systematic approach/review/study/investigation (系统的方法/综述/研究/调查)；systematically adv. 系统地；systematize v. 系统化；system n. 体系。
🧠 助记
system(系统) + atic(形容词) → 有系统的 → 系统的/有条理的。构词清晰直接。
systematic::/ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk/:adj. 系统的:A systematic approach to problem-solving will yield more reliable results.
3. horizon /həˈraɪzn/ n. 地平线；眼界；视野；范围
📖 英文解释
The line where the earth meets the sky; the limits of someone's knowledge or interests.
✏️ 例句
Studying abroad broadened her academic horizons significantly.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：broaden/expand one's horizons (开阔眼界)；on the horizon (即将出现/在地平线上)；beyond the horizon (超越视野)；new horizons (新天地)。
🧠 助记
horizon 联想：hor(hori) + izon → 水平线(horizontal)上的地平线 → horizon。与 horizontal(水平的) 同根。
horizon::/həˈraɪzn/:n. 地平线:New technological developments are appearing on the horizon of medical research.
4. apparent /əˈpærənt/ adj. 明显的；表面的；显而易见的
📖 英文解释
Clearly visible or understood; seeming real or true but possibly not so.
✏️ 例句
It became apparent that the project would not be completed on time.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频：it is apparent that (显然…)；apparent contradiction/reason/success (表面上的矛盾/原因/成功)；apparently adv. 显然地/表面上看来；heir apparent (法定继承人)。
🧠 助记
ap(ad-到) + parent(父母) → 像父母一样明显可见 → 明显的。或联想：父母(parent)的想法总是一目了然(apparent)。
apparent::/əˈpærənt/:adj. 明显的:It was apparent to everyone that the new manager lacked the necessary experience.
5. guidance /ˈɡaɪdəns/ n. 指导；引导；建议
📖 英文解释
Help and advice about how to do something or about how to deal with problems.
✏️ 例句
Students can seek academic guidance from their tutors at any time.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：under the guidance of (在…指导下)；career/professional/academic guidance (职业/专业/学术指导)；guidance counselor (辅导老师)；guide v. 引导 → guidance。
🧠 助记
guide(引导) + ance(名词) → 引导的行为 → 指导。guide → guidance，规则构词。
guidance::/ˈɡaɪdəns/:n. 指导:Young entrepreneurs benefit greatly from the guidance of experienced mentors.
6. passive /ˈpæsɪv/ adj. 被动的；消极的；顺从的
📖 英文解释
Accepting or allowing what happens without active response; not acting but acted upon.
✏️ 例句
A passive attitude toward injustice allows it to continue unchallenged.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：passive voice (被动语态)；passive smoking (被动吸烟)；passive resistance (消极抵抗)；passive income (被动收入)；passively adv.；passivity n. 被动性。
🧠 助记
pass(通过/经历) + ive → 只是让事情经过自己，不主动 → 被动的。联想 active(主动) 的反义。
passive::/ˈpæsɪv/:adj. 被动的:Passive consumption of social media can negatively impact mental health.
7. creature /ˈkriːtʃər/ n. 生物；动物；受…摆布的人
📖 英文解释
A living being, especially an animal; a person dominated by a specified influence.
✏️ 例句
Marine biologists discovered several new creature species in the deep ocean.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：creature of habit (习惯性的人)；creature comforts (物质享受)；fellow creature (同类生物)；living creature (生物)；mysterious creature (神秘生物)。
🧠 助记
creat(创造) + ure → 被创造出来的东西 → 生物。联想 create(创造), Creator(造物主) 创造了 creatures。
creature::/ˈkriːtʃər/:n. 生物:Humans are creatures of habit who resist change even when it would benefit them.
8. fierce /fɪərs/ adj. 凶猛的；激烈的；强烈的
📖 英文解释
Very aggressive and violent; extremely strong or intense.
✏️ 例句
The two companies engaged in fierce competition for market dominance.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：fierce competition/debate/battle/opposition (激烈的竞争/辩论/战斗/反对)；fiercely adv. 激烈地；fierce storm/wind (猛烈的风暴/风)；fierce loyalty (强烈的忠诚)。
🧠 助记
fierce 联想 pierce(刺穿) → 凶猛地刺穿 → 激烈的/凶猛的。f + ierce，想象愤怒的表情(fierce face)。
fierce::/fɪərs/:adj. 凶猛的:She faced fierce opposition from traditionalists when proposing the reforms.
9. mix /mɪks/ v. 混合；交往 n. 混合物；混合
📖 英文解释
To combine or blend different substances; to socialize with different people; a combination of things.
✏️ 例句
The cocktail is made by mixing three different juices with sparkling water.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：mix up (混淆/弄乱)；mixed feelings (复杂的感情)；mixed economy (混合经济)；mix with (与…混合/交往)；mixture n. 混合物。
🧠 助记
mix 是基础词，和 fix(修复), six(六), six fingers mixing 联想。混合在一起 = mix。
mix::/mɪks/:v. 混合:The policy resulted in a mixed response from both supporters and opponents.
10. trigger /ˈtrɪɡər/ v. 触发；引起；激起 n. 扳机；诱因
📖 英文解释
To cause something to happen; the lever that fires a gun; a cause of a reaction.
✏️ 例句
The announcement triggered widespread protests across the country.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频：trigger a reaction/response/crisis/debate (引发反应/回应/危机/辩论)；pull the trigger (扣动扳机/做出决定)；trigger mechanism (触发机制)；stress trigger (压力诱因)。
🧠 助记
trigger 联想：tri(三) + gger → 三个手指扣扳机 → trigger。或记：'trig'(扳机部分) + ger(动作) = 扣扳机触发。
trigger::/ˈtrɪɡər/:v. 触发:A single spark can trigger a massive forest fire during drought conditions.
11. cue /kjuː/ n. 提示；暗示；信号 v. 给暗示；发信号
📖 英文解释
A signal for something to begin; a hint or indication; a long stick for playing billiards.
✏️ 例句
The director gave the actors their cue to begin the scene.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：take one's cue from (从…处得到提示/效仿)；on cue (恰好此时)；visual/verbal cue (视觉/言语提示)；cue sb in (给某人提示)。注意与 queue(排队)发音相同。
🧠 助记
cue 发音和 queue 一样，都是 /kjuː/，排队也是等待信号(cue)动的时候。
cue::/kjuː/:n. 提示:Rising unemployment rates were a cue for the government to rethink its economic policy.
12. disable /dɪsˈeɪbl/ v. 使丧失能力；使残障；禁用
📖 英文解释
To make someone unable to function or to make something unable to work properly.
✏️ 例句
The cyber attack disabled the hospital's computer systems for several hours.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：disabled adj. 残障的；disability n. 残疾/缺陷；enable 反义词；people with disabilities (残疾人)；disabled parking (残疾人停车位)。
🧠 助记
dis-(否定) + able(有能力的) → 使无能力 → 使残障/禁用。dis + enable 逆过来就是 disable。
disable::/dɪsˈeɪbl/:v. 使丧失能力:The new law aims to prevent discrimination against disabled employees in the workplace.
13. hostile /ˈhɒstaɪl/ adj. 敌对的；不友好的；恶劣的
📖 英文解释
Showing or feeling opposition; of an enemy; very unfavorable.
✏️ 例句
The new policy received a hostile reception from the business community.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：hostile takeover (敌意收购)；hostile environment (恶劣环境)；hostile to/towards (对…敌对)；hostility n. 敌意；hostilities n. 战争行为。
🧠 助记
host(主人) + ile → 不像主人(host)那样热情，反而充满敌意 → 敌对的。好的host热情，hostile相反。
hostile::/ˈhɒstaɪl/:adj. 敌对的:The explorers encountered a hostile climate that tested their endurance to the limit.
14. integrity /ɪnˈteɡrɪti/ n. 诚实正直；完整；完好
📖 英文解释
The quality of being honest and having strong moral principles; the state of being whole and undivided.
✏️ 例句
A politician's integrity is essential for maintaining public trust.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频：personal/moral/academic integrity (个人/道德/学术诚信)；territorial integrity (领土完整)；structural integrity (结构完整性)；integrity of data (数据完整性)。
🧠 助记
integr(完整，来自 integer) + ity → 完整无缺的品格 → 诚实正直。联想 integrate(整合), integer(整数)，都来自'完整'。
integrity::/ɪnˈteɡrɪti/:n. 诚实正直:Academic integrity requires students to submit only their own original work.
15. precise /prɪˈsaɪs/ adj. 精确的；准确的；细心的
📖 英文解释
Marked by exactness and accuracy; used to emphasize that you are referring to exactly that thing.
✏️ 例句
The surgeon performed the operation with precise, controlled movements.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：to be precise (确切地说)；precise meaning/location/moment (确切的含义/位置/时刻)；precisely adv. 恰好地；precision n. 精确度；imprecise 反义词。
🧠 助记
pre-(之前) + cise(切割) → 提前精准地切割 → 精确的。联想 scissors(剪刀)，precise cut 精准剪切。
precise::/prɪˈsaɪs/:adj. 精确的:The report lacked precise data to support its ambitious conclusions.
16. suppress /səˈpres/ v. 压制；镇压；抑制；隐瞒
📖 英文解释
To put an end to by force; to prevent from being known; to restrain or hold back.
✏️ 例句
The government attempted to suppress news of the disaster to prevent panic.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：suppress a rebellion/uprising (镇压叛乱/起义)；suppress emotions/laughter (压抑情绪/笑声)；suppress evidence/information (压制证据/信息)；suppression n. 压制；suppressant n. 抑制剂。
🧠 助记
sup(下) + press(压) → 从上往下压 → 压制/镇压。联想 depress(使沮丧), compress(压缩)，press 都有压的意思。
suppress::/səˈpres/:v. 压制:Attempts to suppress the truth only fueled more public anger and mistrust.
17. blow /bləʊ/ v. 吹；爆炸；挥霍 n. 打击；一阵风
📖 英文解释
To move air with force; a hard hit; a sudden shock or disappointment; to waste money.
✏️ 例句
The unexpected job loss was a severe blow to his confidence.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：a blow to (对…的打击)；blow up (爆炸/发怒/放大)；blow over (平息)；blow out (吹灭/爆胎)；deal a blow (给予打击)；come to blows (大打出手)。
🧠 助记
blow 联想：B + LOW → 把东西吹LOW(低) → 吹倒，也是打击。风吹(blow)就会产生打击(blow)。
blow::/bləʊ/:v. 吹:The factory closure dealt a devastating blow to the local economy.
18. bone /bəʊn/ n. 骨；骨头 v. 剔除骨头
📖 英文解释
Any of the pieces of hard tissue making up the skeleton; to remove bones from fish or meat.
✏️ 例句
She studied bone density to assess patients' risk of osteoporosis.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：bone of contention (争议焦点)；close to the bone (直击痛处)；bone up on (临阵磨刀/苦读)；to the bone (彻底地/到骨子里)；backbone n. 脊梁/骨气。
🧠 助记
bone 联想：B-ONE → be one → 骨头是一块硬的东西(one piece of hard stuff)。
bone::/bəʊn/:n. 骨:The question of budget allocation remained a bone of contention within the committee.
19. gate /ɡeɪt/ n. 门；大门；登机口；(比赛)入场人数
📖 英文解释
A movable barrier used to close an opening in a fence or wall; an airport boarding entrance.

Passengers for flight 237 should proceed to gate 15 immediately.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：floodgate (水闸/洪流)；golden gate (金门)；gate fee (入场费)；-gate 后缀构词：Watergate(水门事件)表示政治丑闻。
🧠 助记
gate 联想 great(伟大) → 伟大的大门。airport gate(登机口) 就是通往世界的大门。
gate::/ɡeɪt/:n. 门:The government's decision opened the floodgates to widespread immigration.
20. abundant /əˈbʌndənt/ adj. 丰富的；大量的；充裕的
📖 英文解释
Existing or available in large quantities; more than enough.
✏️ 例句
The region has abundant natural resources but struggles with economic development.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频：abundant evidence/resources/opportunities (大量的证据/资源/机会)；abundantly adv. (大量地；abundantly clear 非常清楚)；abundance n. 丰富/充裕；in abundance (大量地)。
🧠 助记
abund(溢出，来自 unda 波浪) + ant → 波浪溢出来 → 丰富的/充裕的。联想 redundant(多余的)，都有过多的意思。
abundant::/əˈbʌndənt/:adj. 丰富的:Abundant scientific evidence confirms the link between smoking and lung cancer.
21. appliance /əˈplaɪəns/ n. 装置；器具；家用电器

🧠 助记
apply(应用) + ance(名词) → 被应用的东西 → 器具/电器。用来应用的设备就是 appliance。
appliance::/əˈplaɪəns/:n. 装置:Energy-efficient appliances can significantly reduce household electricity bills.
22. relax /rɪˈlæks/ v. 放松；松弛；减轻（管制）

re-(再次) + lax(松弛，来自 laxus) → 再次变松弛 → 放松。联想 lax(松弛的)，relax = 让自己lax。
relax::/rɪˈlæks/:v. 放松:The central bank decided to relax monetary policy to stimulate economic growth.

24. appetite /ˈæpɪtaɪt/ n. 食欲；欲望；兴趣

A natural desire to satisfy a bodily need, especially for food; a strong desire or liking for something.

The economic crisis dampened consumers' appetite for luxury goods.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

appetite::/ˈæpɪtaɪt/:n. 食欲:The initial success of the venture whetted investors' appetite for further expansion.
