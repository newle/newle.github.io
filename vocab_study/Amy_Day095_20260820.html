1. comparable /ˈkɒmpərəbəl/ adj. 可比较的；类似的；相当的
📖 英文解释
Able to be likened to another; of equivalent quality; similar; corresponding.
✏️ 例句
Wages in this sector are not comparable to those in the financial industry.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：comparable to/with（与…相当/相似）；comparable in size/quality（在规模/质量上相当）；incomparable（无与伦比的）；comparably（相当地）
🧠 助记
compare（比较）+ able → 能够比较的 → 可比较的；相当的
comparable::/ˈkɒmpərəbəl/:adj. 可比较的:The two cities are comparable in terms of population and economic development.
2. cast /kæst/ v. 投；铸造；抛弃；投票 n. 演员阵容；石膏；铸件
📖 英文解释
To throw something forcefully; to assign roles to actors; to pour metal into a mold; to cause light or shadow to appear on a surface; the actors in a play or film.
✏️ 例句
The film has an impressive cast of award-winning actors.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：cast doubt on（对…产生怀疑）；cast a shadow（投下阴影）；cast a vote（投票）；cast aside（扔掉；抛弃）；broadcast（广播）
🧠 助记
联想：cast 谐音'快斯特'，快速地投掷出去 → 投；铸造；演员阵容（选人投入角色）
cast::/kæst/:v. 投:New evidence cast doubt on the existing theory.
3. mutual /ˈmjuːtʃuəl/ adj. 相互的；共同的；互助的
📖 英文解释
Experienced or done by each of two or more parties toward the other or others; held in common by two or more parties.
✏️ 例句
The agreement was reached through mutual respect and compromise.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：mutual benefit/understanding/respect（互利/相互理解/尊重）；mutual fund（共同基金）；on mutual terms（在相互基础上）；mutually（相互地）
🧠 助记
mutual 来自拉丁 mutuus（相互的），联想 mutual = two 对调 → 相互的
mutual::/ˈmjuːtʃuəl/:adj. 相互的:The two countries established diplomatic relations for their mutual benefit.
4. organisation /ˌɔːrɡənɪˈzeɪʃən/ n. 组织；机构；团体；安排
📖 英文解释
An organized group of people with a particular purpose, such as a business or government department; the action of organizing something.
✏️ 例句
The humanitarian organisation provided aid to thousands of refugees.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：international organisation（国际组织）；non-governmental organisation（NGO，非政府组织）；organise（v. 组织）；organisational（adj. 组织的）
🧠 助记
organ（器官/工具）+ isation → 像有机体一样有各器官的组织 → 组织；机构
organisation::/ˌɔːrɡənɪˈzeɪʃən/:n. 组织:The organisation launched a new initiative to promote literacy.
5. row /roʊ/（争吵）or /raʊ/（英式，争吵） n. 一排；行；争吵 v. 划船；争吵
📖 英文解释
A number of people or things in a straight line; an act of rowing a boat; a noisy argument; to propel a boat using oars.
✏️ 例句
They had a terrible row about money and didn't speak for days.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：a row of（一排…）；in a row（连续；成排地）；have a row（吵架）；row a boat（划船）；death row（死刑犯牢房）
🧠 助记
row 一词多义：一排（排排坐）/ 划船（连续动作）/ 吵架（排成战线对吵）
row::/roʊ/（争吵）or /raʊ/（英式，争吵）:n. 一排:The two politicians had a bitter public row over budget cuts.
6. cheer /tʃɪər/ v. 欢呼；为…加油；使振奋 n. 欢呼声；欢乐
📖 英文解释
To shout for joy or in praise or encouragement; to make someone feel happier; a shout of encouragement, praise, or joy.
✏️ 例句
The crowd cheered loudly as the team scored the final goal.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：cheer sb up（使某人振奋）；three cheers for（为…欢呼三声）；cheer on（为…加油）；cheerful（adj. 愉快的）
🧠 助记
联想：cheer 谐音'稀儿'，小孩高兴地叫 → 欢呼；使振奋
cheer::/tʃɪər/:v. 欢呼:The good news cheered everyone up considerably.
7. intensity /ɪnˈtensɪti/ n. 强度；烈度；紧张；激烈
📖 英文解释
The quality of being intense; measurable amount of some quality; the extreme strength or force of something.
✏️ 例句
The intensity of the storm increased overnight.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：intensity of emotion/effort/competition（情感/努力/竞争的强度）；high/low intensity（高/低强度）；intensify（v. 加强）；intense（adj. 强烈的）
🧠 助记
intense（强烈的）+ ity → 强烈的程度/状态 → 强度；烈度
intensity::/ɪnˈtensɪti/:n. 强度:The intensity of the drought shocked even experienced farmers.
8. nurture /ˈnɜːrtʃər/ v./n. 培育；养育；滋养；照料
📖 英文解释
To care for and protect someone while they are growing; to help develop and encourage; the upbringing and care of a person.
✏️ 例句
The teacher helped nurture the student's love of literature.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：nurture talent/creativity（培育才能/创造力）；nature vs nurture（先天与后天）；nurturing environment（培育性环境）
🧠 助记
nurt（nurse 护理）+ ure → 像护士一样照料培育 → 培育；养育
nurture::/ˈnɜːrtʃər/:v./n. 培育:The program was designed to nurture the next generation of scientists.
9. esteem /ɪˈstiːm/ n. 尊重；尊敬 v. 尊重；认为
📖 英文解释
Respect and admiration; to regard with respect and admiration.
✏️ 例句
She was held in high esteem by her colleagues.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：hold sb in (high) esteem（对某人非常敬重）；self-esteem（自尊；自我评价）；esteem sb as/for（因…尊重某人）；esteemed（受尊重的）
🧠 助记
e + steam（蒸汽），蒸蒸日上被人仰望 → 尊重；尊敬
esteem::/ɪˈstiːm/:n. 尊重:Her research was held in high esteem by scientists worldwide.
10. liberate /ˈlɪbəreɪt/ v. 解放；释放；使自由
📖 英文解释
To set someone free from imprisonment, slavery, or oppression; to free someone from a situation that limits their freedom.
✏️ 例句
The country was liberated from decades of authoritarian rule.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：liberate sb from sth（从…中解放某人）；liberation（n. 解放；解脱）；liberation movement（解放运动）；liberator（解放者）
🧠 助记
liber（自由，来自拉丁 liber）+ ate → 使自由 → 解放；释放
liberate::/ˈlɪbəreɪt/:v. 解放:Education can liberate people from the cycle of poverty.

persist::/pərˈsɪst/:v. 坚持:Cold weather persisted throughout March and well into April.

undergo::/ˌʌndərˈɡoʊ/:v. 经历:The city underwent a dramatic transformation in the last decade.
