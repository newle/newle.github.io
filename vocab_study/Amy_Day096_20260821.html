1. forbid /fəˈbɪd/ v. 禁止；不准；阻止
📖 英文解释
To refuse to allow something; to order someone not to do something.
✏️ 例句
The school forbids students from using mobile phones during class.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
forbid sb to do/from doing 禁止某人做；forbidden adj. 被禁止的；forbidden fruit 禁果
🧠 助记
for（反对）+ bid（出价/命令），反对命令→禁止
forbid::/fəˈbɪd/:v. 禁止:The law forbids discrimination based on race or gender.
2. rational /ˈræʃənl/ adj. 理性的；合理的；有理智的
📖 英文解释
Based on or in accordance with reason or logic; having the ability to reason.
✏️ 例句
It is important to make rational decisions when investing money.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
rational thinking/behavior/decision 理性思考/行为/决定；反义词 irrational；名词 rationality
🧠 助记
ratio（比率，数学中有理数）+ nal，符合比率规律的→理性的、合理的
rational::/ˈræʃənl/:adj. 理性的:A rational person considers all options before making a choice.
3. heel /hiːl/ n. 脚跟；鞋跟 v. 倾斜；跟随
📖 英文解释
The back part of the foot below the ankle; the part of a shoe that covers this.
✏️ 例句
She wore high-heeled shoes to the formal dinner.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
high heel 高跟鞋；Achilles heel 致命弱点；head over heels 完全地（in love）；heel turn 转变立场
🧠 助记
heel 形状像脚后跟；和 feel（感觉）押韵，用脚跟感受地面
heel::/hiːl/:n. 脚跟:His overconfidence proved to be his Achilles heel.
4. desperate /ˈdespərət/ adj. 绝望的；不顾一切的；极度渴望的
📖 英文解释
Feeling or showing a hopeless sense that a situation is so bad as to be impossible to deal with; giving little hope of success and requiring extreme measures.
✏️ 例句
She was desperate for help after losing her job and savings.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
desperate for 极度渴望；desperate situation/measures 绝境/极端措施；desperately adv. 拼命地
🧠 助记
de（失去）+ sperate（=hope希望），失去希望→绝望的
desperate::/ˈdespərət/:adj. 绝望的:The country is in desperate need of humanitarian aid.
5. twelve /twelv/ num. 十二 n. 十二个
📖 英文解释
The number 12; equivalent to the product of three and four.
✏️ 例句
There are twelve months in a year.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
twelve months/hours/years；the Twelve Apostles 十二使徒；twelfth 第十二
🧠 助记
tw（two）+ elve，two和else合并，二加十→十二
twelve::/twelv/:num. 十二 n. 十二个:The jury consists of twelve members.
6. hesitate /ˈhezɪteɪt/ v. 犹豫；踌躇；不情愿
📖 英文解释
To pause before saying or doing something, especially due to uncertainty or reluctance.
✏️ 例句
Don't hesitate to ask if you have any questions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
hesitate to do 犹豫做某事；without hesitation 毫不犹豫；hesitation n.；hesitant adj.
🧠 助记
hesi（谐音"喝西"）+ tate，喝西北风的时候犹豫要不要喝→犹豫
hesitate::/ˈhezɪteɪt/:v. 犹豫:She hesitated before accepting the job offer abroad.
7. index /ˈɪndeks/ n. 索引；指数；指标 v. 编索引
📖 英文解释
An alphabetical list at the end of a book; a number showing the relative level of something; to provide with an index.
✏️ 例句
The consumer price index rose by 2% last month.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
stock/price/index finger 股指/价格指数/食指；index of 反映…的指标；indexed adj.
🧠 助记
index（in + dex），in（在内）+ dex（手指），手指指向→索引、指标
index::/ˈɪndeks/:n. 索引:Look up the term in the index at the back of the book.
8. attain /əˈteɪn/ v. 达到；获得；实现
📖 英文解释
To succeed in achieving something, especially after a lot of effort.
✏️ 例句
She worked tirelessly to attain her goal of becoming a doctor.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
attain a goal/level/success 达到目标/水平/成功；attainable adj. 可达到的；attainment n.
🧠 助记
at（到达）+ tain（=tain 保持，如contain），到达并保持住→达到、获得
attain::/əˈteɪn/:v. 达到:Through years of practice, he attained mastery of the instrument.
9. frighten /ˈfraɪtn/ v. 使害怕；吓唬；受惊
📖 英文解释
To make someone feel fear; to be made afraid.
✏️ 例句
The sudden noise frightened the sleeping baby.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
frighten sb away/off 把某人吓跑；frightened adj. 感到害怕的；frightening adj. 令人害怕的
🧠 助记
fright（恐惧）+ en（动词化），使产生恐惧→使害怕
frighten::/ˈfraɪtn/:v. 使害怕:The thunderstorm frightened the young children.
10. appoint /əˈpɔɪnt/ v. 任命；委派；指定（时间/地点）
📖 英文解释
To assign a job or role to someone; to fix or arrange a time or place.
✏️ 例句
The president appointed her as the new chief justice.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
appoint sb as/to 任命某人为；appointed time/place 约定的时间/地点；appointment n.
🧠 助记
ap（=to）+ point（指），指向某人→任命，给某人指定职位
appoint::/əˈpɔɪnt/:v. 任命:The board appointed a new CEO to lead the company.
11. drama /ˈdrɑːmə/ n. 戏剧；剧本；戏剧性事件
📖 英文解释
A play or performance; a situation involving exciting or intense events; an exciting or emotional situation.
✏️ 例句
The television drama attracted millions of viewers each episode.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
Greek drama 古希腊戏剧；make a drama out of sth 小题大做；dramatic adj. 戏剧性的
🧠 助记
drama 来自希腊语，drama queen 爱戏剧化的人→戏剧、戏剧性
drama::/ˈdrɑːmə/:n. 戏剧:The rescue at sea was described as a real-life drama.
12. satisfactory /ˌsætɪsˈfæktəri/ adj. 令人满意的；合格的；足够的
📖 英文解释
Fulfilling expectations or needs; adequate but not especially good.
✏️ 例句
The inspector rated the facility's safety standards as satisfactory.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
satisfactory performance/result/answer 令人满意的表现/结果/答案；反义词 unsatisfactory
🧠 助记
satisfy（满足）→ satisfactory，能满足要求的→令人满意的
satisfactory::/ˌsætɪsˈfæktəri/:adj. 令人满意的:His academic progress this semester has been satisfactory.
13. thesis /ˈθiːsɪs/ n. 论文；论点；命题
📖 英文解释
A long essay on a particular subject, especially one written for a university degree; a statement or theory to be maintained or proved.
✏️ 例句
She spent three years writing her doctoral thesis on climate change.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
doctoral/master's thesis 博士/硕士论文；defend a thesis 答辩；thesis statement 论点陈述
🧠 助记
the + sis（系统），有系统地写出来的→论文；复数 theses
thesis::/ˈθiːsɪs/:n. 论文:His thesis argues that economic inequality leads to social unrest.
14. clue /kluː/ n. 线索；提示 v. 给提示
📖 英文解释
A piece of evidence or information that helps to solve a problem or mystery.
✏️ 例句
The detective found a crucial clue that helped solve the case.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
clue sb in 告诉某人内情；have no clue 毫不知道；clue about/to sth …的线索
🧠 助记
clue 像 glue（胶水），线索像胶水把事件粘连起来→线索
clue::/kluː/:n. 线索:The investigators found no clues at the crime scene.
15. defeat /dɪˈfiːt/ v./n. 击败；挫败；使受挫
📖 英文解释
To win a victory over someone in a battle, competition, or election; an instance of being beaten.
✏️ 例句
The underdog team managed to defeat the reigning champions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
defeat an opponent/enemy/bill 击败对手/敌人/否决法案；suffer a defeat 遭受失败；defeatism n.
🧠 助记
de（去除）+ feat（功绩），去除对手的功绩→击败
defeat::/dɪˈfiːt/:v./n. 击败:The proposal was defeated by a narrow margin in Parliament.
16. oxygen /ˈɒksɪdʒən/ n. 氧；氧气
📖 英文解释
A colorless, odorless reactive gas that constitutes about 21% of the Earth's atmosphere, essential for most forms of life.
✏️ 例句
Plants release oxygen during the process of photosynthesis.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
oxygen mask/tank/level 氧气面罩/氧气罐/氧气水平；lack of oxygen 缺氧；oxygen bar 氧吧
🧠 助记
oxy（acid 酸）+ gen（产生），最初认为产生酸的→氧气（历史命名错误但记住了）
oxygen::/ˈɒksɪdʒən/:n. 氧:High altitude climbers often carry supplemental oxygen.
17. ball /bɔːl/ n. 球；舞会；子弹 v. 揉成球
📖 英文解释
A round object used in games; a formal social event with dancing; to form into a round shape.
✏️ 例句
The charity ball raised over a million dollars for the hospital.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
ball game 球赛；have a ball 玩得很开心；on the ball 机灵/有效率；ball park 棒球场/大致范围
🧠 助记
ball 既是球也是舞会，想象在球形大厅里跳舞的舞会
ball::/bɔːl/:n. 球:The annual charity ball was attended by hundreds of guests.
18. exclusive /ɪkˈskluːsɪv/ adj. 独家的；排外的；高档的 n. 独家新闻
📖 英文解释
Excluding or not admitting other things; restricted to a particular person or group; high-class and expensive.
✏️ 例句
The magazine published an exclusive interview with the president.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
exclusive right/access/club 专有权/独家访问/高级俱乐部；mutually exclusive 互相排斥的
🧠 助记
ex（出去）+ clusive（=close关），关门出去不让其他人进→排外的、独家的
exclusive::/ɪkˈskluːsɪv/:adj. 独家的:The hotel offers exclusive services to its premium members.
19. headache /ˈhedeɪk/ n. 头痛；令人头痛的问题
📖 英文解释
A continuous pain in the head; something that causes worry or difficulty.
✏️ 例句
The budget deficit has become a major headache for the new administration.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
have a headache 头痛；give sb a headache 让某人头疼；splitting headache 剧烈头痛
🧠 助记
head（头）+ ache（疼痛），头+疼→头痛（直接合成）
headache::/ˈhedeɪk/:n. 头痛:Managing remote teams can be a real headache for managers.
20. membership /ˈmembəʃɪp/ n. 会员资格；成员身份；会员总数
📖 英文解释
The state of being a member of a group; the number of members in a group.
✏️ 例句
Gym membership fees have increased by 10% this year.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
membership fee/card/number 会费/会员卡/会员人数；apply for membership 申请会员资格
🧠 助记
member（成员）+ ship（状态/身份），成员的身份→会员资格
membership::/ˈmembəʃɪp/:n. 会员资格:Club membership gives you access to all facilities.
21. twin /twɪn/ n. 双胞胎之一 adj. 孪生的；双重的 v. 结为姊妹
📖 英文解释
Either of two children or animals born at the same time from the same mother; consisting of two matching parts.
✏️ 例句
Identical twins share the same DNA but can develop different personalities.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
identical/fraternal twins 同卵/异卵双胞胎；twin room/bed 双人床间；twin city 姊妹城市
🧠 助记
twin = two + in（里面），两个在里面→双胞胎
twin::/twɪn/:n. 双胞胎之一 adj. 孪生的:The twin towers were once the tallest buildings in the world.
22. underground /ˈʌndəɡraʊnd/ adj./adv. 地下的；秘密的 n. 地铁；地下组织
📖 英文解释
Beneath the surface of the earth; operating in secret; a railway system in a city that runs below ground.
✏️ 例句
The resistance movement operated underground during the occupation.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
underground railway/station 地铁/地铁站；go underground 转入地下；underground economy 地下经济
🧠 助记
under（下面）+ ground（地面），在地面下面→地下的
underground::/ˈʌndəɡraʊnd/:adj./adv. 地下的:The London Underground is one of the oldest metro systems in the world.
23. upward /ˈʌpwəd/ adj./adv. 向上的；上升的
📖 英文解释
Moving or leading towards a higher place; directed or moving towards a higher level or number.
✏️ 例句
There has been an upward trend in house prices over the last decade.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
upward trend/mobility/pressure 上升趋势/向上流动/上行压力；upwards of 超过…；upwardly adv.
🧠 助记
up（上）+ ward（方向），向上的方向→向上的
upward::/ˈʌpwəd/:adj./adv. 向上的:The graph shows an upward movement in sales throughout the year.
24. architect /ˈɑːrkɪtekt/ n. 建筑师；设计者；缔造者
📖 英文解释
A person who designs buildings; a person who is responsible for creating something important.
✏️ 例句
She became the chief architect of the country's economic reform.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
architect of sth 某事的设计者/缔造者；landscape architect 景观建筑师；architecture n. 建筑学
🧠 助记
archi（首要的）+ tect（建造，如construct），首要建造者→建筑师
architect::/ˈɑːrkɪtekt/:n. 建筑师:He was the principal architect of the peace agreement.
25. consultant /kənˈsʌltənt/ n. 顾问；咨询师；会诊医生
📖 英文解释
A person who provides expert advice professionally; a senior hospital doctor.
✏️ 例句
The company hired a management consultant to improve efficiency.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
management/financial/legal consultant 管理/财务/法律顾问；consultancy n. 咨询公司
🧠 助记
consult（咨询）+ ant（人），进行咨询的人→顾问
consultant::/kənˈsʌltənt/:n. 顾问:The firm brought in an outside consultant to review its strategy.
