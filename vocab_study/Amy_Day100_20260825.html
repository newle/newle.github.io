1. somebody /ˈsʌmbədi/ pron. 某人；n. 重要人物
📖 英文解释
An unknown or unspecified person; a person of importance.
✏️ 例句
There is somebody waiting for you outside.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常用：somebody important（重要人物）；be somebody（有地位）；辨析 someone（更正式）
🧠 助记
some（某）+body（人）→ 某个人
somebody::/ˈsʌmbədi/:pron. 某人:It takes years of hard work to become somebody in this industry.
2. clinic /ˈklinik/ n. 诊所；门诊；培训班
📖 英文解释
A place where patients receive medical treatment; a practical lesson for students.
✏️ 例句
She visited the clinic for her annual health checkup.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常用：community clinic（社区诊所）；outpatient clinic（门诊）；dental clinic（牙科诊所）
🧠 助记
clin（倾斜/床）+ic → 卧床的地方 → 诊所
clinic::/ˈklinik/:n. 诊所:Free health clinics provide essential services to underserved populations.
3. dull /dʌl/ adj. 枯燥的；阴暗的；迟钝的；v. 使迟钝
📖 英文解释
Lacking interest or excitement; not bright, intense, or sharp.
✏️ 例句
The lecture was so dull that half the audience fell asleep.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研多义：dull ache（隐隐作痛）；dull market（疲软市场）；dull weather（阴暗天气）
🧠 助记
dull → dul（迟钝）→ 大脑迟钝 → 枯燥/迟钝
dull::/dʌl/:adj. 枯燥的:Dull economic data suggests the recovery is slower than expected.
4. tight /tait/ adj. 紧的；拮据的；严格的；adv. 紧紧地
📖 英文解释
Fixed, closed, or fastened firmly; of money, scarce and difficult to obtain.
✏️ 例句
The deadline is tight, so we need to work efficiently.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研多义：tight budget（预算紧张）；tight security（严密安保）；tight-knit（关系紧密的）
🧠 助记
tight → t+ight（紧）→ 紧紧的
tight::/tait/:adj. 紧的:With a tight budget, the government had to make difficult spending cuts.
5. wonderful /ˈwʌndəfl/ adj. 精彩的；极好的；令人惊叹的
📖 英文解释
Inspiring delight, pleasure, or admiration; extremely good.
✏️ 例句
The concert was a wonderful experience that left a lasting impression.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研基础词：wonderful opportunity/achievement/performance；wonder（n./v.）
🧠 助记
wonder（奇迹）+ful → 充满奇迹的 → 精彩的
wonderful::/ˈwʌndəfl/:adj. 精彩的:She had a wonderful ability to connect with people from all walks of life.
6. accommodate /əˈkɒmədeit/ v. 容纳；适应；供应住宿；满足需求
📖 英文解释
To provide lodging or sufficient space for; to adapt to; to do a favour for.
✏️ 例句
The new stadium can accommodate up to 80,000 spectators.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频：accommodate sb with sth；accommodation（n.住处）；辨析 adapt, adjust
🧠 助记
ac（to）+commod（方便）+ate → 使方便 → 容纳/适应
accommodate::/əˈkɒmədeit/:v. 容纳:The policy was revised to better accommodate the needs of disabled citizens.
7. destructive /diˈstrʌktiv/ adj. 破坏性的；有害的；毁灭性的
📖 英文解释

🧠 助记

destructive::/diˈstrʌktiv/:adj. 破坏性的:Social media can have a destructive effect on teenagers mental health.
8. chase /tʃeis/ v. 追逐；追求；驱赶；n. 追赶

chase::/tʃeis/:v. 追逐:Young graduates should chase their dreams while staying realistic.
9. steep /stiːp/ adj. 陡峭的；急剧的；过高的；v. 浸泡
📖 英文解释
Rising or falling sharply; of price, very high; to soak in liquid.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研多义：steep cliff/hill（陡峭的悬崖/山丘）；steep rise/fall（急剧上升/下降）；steeped in history（历史悠久）
🧠 助记
steep → st（stand直立）+eep → 直立着的山 → 陡峭
steep::/stiːp/:adj. 陡峭的:The steep increase in housing prices has made homeownership unaffordable.
10. deed /diːd/ n. 行为；事迹；契约；v. 以证书转让

deed::/diːd/:n. 行为:The charity recognized her generous deeds with a lifetime achievement award.
