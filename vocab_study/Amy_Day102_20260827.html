1. misfortune /mɪsˈfɔːtʃuːn/ n. 不幸；灾难
📖 英文解释
Bad luck or an unfortunate condition or event.
✏️ 例句
She bore her misfortune with remarkable courage and grace.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
suffer misfortune 遭遇不幸；a stroke of misfortune 一次不幸；companion in misfortune 难友
🧠 助记
mis（错误）+ fortune（幸运）→ 错误的幸运 → 不幸
misfortune::/mɪsˈfɔːtʃuːn/:n. 不幸:He faced misfortune after misfortune without losing hope.
2. rob /rɒb/ v. 抢劫；剥夺
📖 英文解释
To take property unlawfully from a person or place; to deprive someone of something.
✏️ 例句
The thief robbed the bank at gunpoint.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
rob sb of sth 剥夺某人某物；rob Peter to pay Paul 拆东墙补西墙；近义：steal, deprive
🧠 助记
rob → 谐音 抢 → 抢劫
rob::/rɒb/:v. 抢劫:Illness robbed him of the chance to compete in the finals.
3. surgeon /ˈsɜːdʒən/ n. 外科医生
📖 英文解释
A doctor who is qualified to perform surgery.
✏️ 例句
The surgeon operated for eight hours to save the patient's life.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
brain/heart surgeon 脑/心脏外科医生；Surgeon General 卫生部长（美）；plastic surgeon 整形外科医生
🧠 助记
surg（手术）+ eon → 做手术的人 → 外科医生
surgeon::/ˈsɜːdʒən/:n. 外科医生:The surgeon's skill was credited with saving hundreds of lives.
4. transmission /trænzˈmɪʃn/ n. 传播；传送；变速器
📖 英文解释
The act of transmitting something; the spreading of a disease; a mechanism for transferring power.
✏️ 例句
The transmission of the virus could be slowed by wearing masks.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
disease transmission 疾病传播；live transmission 直播；automatic transmission 自动变速箱
🧠 助记
trans（穿越）+ mission（任务）→ 穿越传递的任务 → 传播
transmission::/trænzˈmɪʃn/:n. 传播:Understanding disease transmission is key to preventing outbreaks.
5. orbit /ˈɔːbɪt/ n. 轨道；势力范围 v. 绕轨道运行
📖 英文解释
The curved path of a planet or satellite around a star or planet; the sphere of influence.
✏️ 例句
The satellite was placed into orbit around the Earth.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in orbit 在轨道上；orbit the Earth 绕地球运转；fall into sb's orbit 进入某人的势力圈
🧠 助记
orb（球体）+ it → 围绕球体运动 → 轨道
orbit::/ˈɔːbɪt/:n. 轨道:New technologies have brought many countries into the orbit of global trade.
6. reservation /ˌrezəˈveɪʃn/ n. 预订；保留意见；保留地
📖 英文解释
The action of booking a place in advance; a doubt or qualification; an area of land for native peoples.
✏️ 例句

🧠 助记

reservation::/ˌrezəˈveɪʃn/:n. 预订:She expressed reservations about the new plan during the meeting.

8. gadget /ˈɡædʒɪt/ n. 小装置；小玩意儿

gadget::/ˈɡædʒɪt/:n. 小装置:The latest gadgets at the tech fair attracted thousands of visitors.

11. worthwhile /ˌwɜːθˈwaɪl/ adj. 值得做的；有价值的

worthwhile::/ˌwɜːθˈwaɪl/:adj. 值得做的:Volunteering abroad is a worthwhile experience for young people.
