1. underlie /ˌʌndəˈlaɪ/ v. 位于…之下；是…的基础
📖 英文解释
To be the basis or cause of something; to lie under something.
✏️ 例句
Structural inequality underlies many of the problems the country faces.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
underlying adj. 根本的；underlie a theory 构成理论基础；underlying cause 根本原因
🧠 助记
under（下面）+ lie（位于）→ 在下面 → 是基础
underlie::/ˌʌndəˈlaɪ/:v. 位于…之下:A lack of trust underlies most failures in workplace communication.
2. precede /prɪˈsiːd/ v. 在…之前；先于
📖 英文解释
To come before something in time, order, or position.
✏️ 例句
A period of economic instability preceded the outbreak of the conflict.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
precede sth 先于某事；preceding adj. 前面的；precedent n. 先例；unprecedented 史无前例的
🧠 助记
pre（之前）+ cede（走）→ 走在前面 → 先于
precede::/prɪˈsiːd/:v. 在…之前:A thorough review should precede any major policy decision.
3. quota /ˈkwəʊtə/ n. 配额；限额；定额
📖 英文解释
A limited or fixed number or amount of people or things.
✏️ 例句
The government introduced import quotas to protect domestic industry.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
import/export quota 进/出口配额；fill a quota 完成配额；quota system 配额制度
🧠 助记
quot（分配）+ a → 被分配到的量 → 配额
quota::/ˈkwəʊtə/:n. 配额:The fishing industry called for an increase in the annual catch quota.
4. surplus /ˈsɜːpləs/ n./adj. 剩余；过剩；顺差
📖 英文解释
An amount of something left over when requirements have been met; more than what is needed.
✏️ 例句
The trade surplus increased significantly due to rising export demand.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
trade surplus 贸易顺差；budget surplus 财政盈余；surplus labour 剩余劳动力；surplus to requirements 多余的
🧠 助记
sur（超过）+ plus（加）→ 超过所需的加量 → 剩余/顺差
surplus::/ˈsɜːpləs/:n./adj. 剩余:The country has a large trade surplus with its main trading partners.
5. drastic /ˈdræstɪk/ adj. 极端的；激烈的；猛烈的
📖 英文解释
Likely to have a strong or far-reaching effect; severe or extreme.
✏️ 例句
The government took drastic measures to bring the public debt under control.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
drastic action/measures 极端行动/措施；drastic cut 大幅削减；drastically adv. 大幅地
🧠 助记
drastic → 谐音 大减 → 大幅度地做 → 极端的
drastic::/ˈdræstɪk/:adj. 极端的:The company had to make drastic cuts to survive the economic downturn.
6. lest /lest/ conj. 以免；唯恐
📖 英文解释
With the intention of preventing; for fear that.
✏️ 例句
She spoke quietly lest she disturb the sleeping child.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
lest + should/do 唯恐；lest we forget 勿忘（铭文常见）；考研完形/语法常见连词
🧠 助记
lest → less（减少）+ t → 减少某事发生的可能 → 以免
lest::/lest/:conj. 以免:He kept the news secret lest it cause unnecessary panic.
7. supervise /ˈsuːpəvaɪz/ v. 监督；管理；指导
📖 英文解释
To observe and direct the execution of a task or activity.
✏️ 例句
She was appointed to supervise the construction of the new hospital.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
supervise a project 监管项目；supervise students 指导学生；supervision n. 监督；supervisor n. 监督者
🧠 助记
super（超过/在上）+ vise（看）→ 从上面看着 → 监督
supervise::/ˈsuːpəvaɪz/:v. 监督:All laboratory work must be supervised by a qualified technician.
8. liable /ˈlaɪəbl/ adj. 有责任的；易于…的；可能的
📖 英文解释
Responsible by law; likely to do or be something undesirable.
✏️ 例句
The company was held liable for the environmental damage caused by its operations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
liable for 对…负责任；liable to do sth 易于做某事；legally liable 法律上有责任
🧠 助记
li（拉）+ able（能）→ 能被拉来承担 → 负责任的
liable::/ˈlaɪəbl/:adj. 有责任的:Employers are liable for any injuries sustained by workers on site.
9. senator /ˈsenətə/ n. 参议员
📖 英文解释
A member of a senate.
✏️ 例句
The senator delivered a passionate speech in support of the healthcare bill.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
US Senator 美国参议员；Senator for 代表…州的参议员；Senate 参议院
🧠 助记
senate（参议院）→ senator → 参议院成员 → 参议员
senator::/ˈsenətə/:n. 参议员:The senator urged her colleagues to vote in favour of the reform.
10. abolish /əˈbɒlɪʃ/ v. 废除；废止
📖 英文解释
To formally put an end to a system, practice, or institution.
✏️ 例句
Many activists campaigned to abolish the death penalty.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
abolish a law/system 废除法律/制度；abolition n. 废除；abolitionist 废除主义者（如废奴运动）
🧠 助记
ab（去除）+ olish（老旧的）→ 去除旧制度 → 废除
abolish::/əˈbɒlɪʃ/:v. 废除:The new government promised to abolish tuition fees for higher education.
11. foresee /fɔːˈsiː/ v. 预见；预料
📖 英文解释
To be aware of something beforehand; to predict.
✏️ 例句
Nobody could have foreseen the scale of the financial crisis.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
foresee problems 预见问题；foreseeable future 可预见的未来；unforeseen 不可预见的
🧠 助记
fore（预先）+ see（看）→ 提前看到 → 预见
foresee::/fɔːˈsiː/:v. 预见:The engineers failed to foresee the structural weaknesses in the design.
12. hardship /ˈhɑːdʃɪp/ n. 艰辛；苦难
📖 英文解释
Severe suffering or privation.
✏️ 例句
Millions of people endure great hardship in regions affected by drought.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
economic hardship 经济困难；suffer hardship 忍受艰辛；hardship clause 艰难条款（合同法）
🧠 助记
hard（困难）+ ship（状态）→ 困难的状态 → 艰辛
hardship::/ˈhɑːdʃɪp/:n. 艰辛:The family faced considerable hardship following the loss of the main breadwinner.
13. hormone /ˈhɔːməʊn/ n. 激素；荷尔蒙
📖 英文解释
A regulatory substance produced in an organism that is transported in tissue fluids to stimulate specific cells.
✏️ 例句
Growth hormones play a crucial role in childhood development.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
growth hormone 生长激素；stress hormone 压力激素；hormone therapy 激素治疗
🧠 助记
hormon（激发）+ e → 激发身体活动的物质 → 激素
hormone::/ˈhɔːməʊn/:n. 激素:Stress triggers the release of hormones such as cortisol and adrenaline.
14. recipient /rɪˈsɪpiənt/ n. 接受者；受益人
📖 英文解释
A person who receives something.
✏️ 例句
The award recipient gave a moving acceptance speech.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
welfare recipient 福利受助者；organ recipient 器官受体；aid recipient 受援国/人
🧠 助记
recip（接收）+ ient → 接收的人 → 接受者
recipient::/rɪˈsɪpiənt/:n. 接受者:All recipients of the scholarship are required to maintain a high academic standard.
15. suspicion /səˈspɪʃn/ n. 怀疑；嫌疑；轻微感觉
📖 英文解释
A feeling or thought that something is possible, likely, or true; distrust.
✏️ 例句
The detective had a strong suspicion that the witness was not telling the truth.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
under suspicion 受嫌疑；arouse/raise suspicion 引起怀疑；beyond suspicion 无可怀疑
🧠 助记
sus（在下）+ picion（看）→ 暗中盯着 → 怀疑
suspicion::/səˈspɪʃn/:n. 怀疑:His sudden wealth aroused the suspicion of the tax authorities.
16. lag /læɡ/ v./n. 落后；滞后；延迟
📖 英文解释
To fall behind in movement or progress; a period of time between two events.
✏️ 例句
Investment in infrastructure has lagged behind economic growth for decades.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
jet lag 时差综合症；lag behind 落后于；time lag 时间滞后；cultural lag 文化滞后
🧠 助记
lag → 谐音 落 → 落后 → 滞后
lag::/læɡ/:v./n. 落后:Public understanding of science tends to lag behind scientific advances.
17. hypothesis /haɪˈpɒθɪsɪs/ n. 假设；假说
📖 英文解释
A supposition or proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence as a starting point.
✏️ 例句
The researchers developed a hypothesis to explain the unexpected experimental results.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
test a hypothesis 检验假说；working hypothesis 工作假设；hypothesis testing 假设检验（统计学）
🧠 助记
hypo（在下）+ thesis（论点）→ 尚未证明的论点 → 假设
hypothesis::/haɪˈpɒθɪsɪs/:n. 假设:The study was designed to test the hypothesis that exercise improves memory.
18. revive /rɪˈvaɪv/ v. 复苏；使苏醒；恢复
📖 英文解释
To regain life, consciousness, or strength; to restore something to use or notice.
✏️ 例句
The government launched a programme to revive the struggling economy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
revive the economy 复苏经济；revive a tradition 恢复传统；revive sb's interest 重燃某人兴趣
🧠 助记
re（再）+ vive（活着）→ 再次活着 → 复苏
revive::/rɪˈvaɪv/:v. 复苏:The director hopes to revive interest in classic literature through the new adaptation.
19. undertake /ˌʌndəˈteɪk/ v. 承担；从事；保证
📖 英文解释
To commit oneself to and begin an enterprise or responsibility; to formally guarantee.
✏️ 例句
She undertook a comprehensive review of the department's procedures.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
undertake a task 承担任务；undertake to do sth 承诺做某事；undertaking n. 承诺；事业
🧠 助记
under（下面）+ take（拿起）→ 把担子扛起来 → 承担
undertake::/ˌʌndəˈteɪk/:v. 承担:The company undertook to complete the project within the agreed timeframe.
20. deficiency /dɪˈfɪʃənsi/ n. 缺乏；不足；缺陷

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
vitamin deficiency 维生素缺乏；immune deficiency 免疫缺陷；deficiency in 在…方面的不足
🧠 助记

deficiency::/dɪˈfɪʃənsi/:n. 缺乏:The audit revealed significant deficiencies in the company's internal controls.
21. enrol /ɪnˈrəʊl/ v. 注册；入学；招募

🧠 助记
en（使）+ rol（名单）→ 使名字进入名单 → 注册/入学
enrol::/ɪnˈrəʊl/:v. 注册:Parents were encouraged to enrol their children in the summer reading programme.
22. peaceful /ˈpiːsfl/ adj. 和平的；平静的；宁静的

peaceful::/ˈpiːsfl/:adj. 和平的:The community lived in peaceful coexistence for generations.
23. realise /ˈrɪəlaɪz/ v. 意识到；实现；变现
📖 英文解释
To become fully aware of something; to cause to happen; to convert into money.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

realise::/ˈrɪəlaɪz/:v. 意识到:The team failed to realise the full potential of the new technology.
24. resemble /rɪˈzembl/ v. 与…相似；像
📖 英文解释
To have a similar appearance to or qualities in common with someone or something.
✏️ 例句
The new proposal closely resembles the plan put forward three years ago.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
resemble each other 互相相似；bear a resemblance to 与…有相似之处；strikingly resemble 非常像
🧠 助记
re（再次）+ semble（似乎）→ 再次看起来相同 → 相似
resemble::/rɪˈzembl/:v. 与…相似:His argument closely resembles that made by his opponent.

spiritual::/ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl/:adj. 精神的:He described his time in the mountains as a deeply spiritual experience.
