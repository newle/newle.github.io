1. fool /fuːl/ n. 傻瓜；v. 欺骗；愚弄
📖 英文解释
a person who acts unwisely; to trick or deceive someone
✏️ 例句
Don't be fooled by his innocent appearance.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：fool around（鬼混）；fool sb into doing（骗某人做某事）；April Fool's Day（愚人节）
🧠 助记
fool和'负'谐音，负能量的人就是傻瓜
fool::/fuːl/:n. 傻瓜:You can't fool all the people all the time.
2. rocket /ˈrɒkɪt/ n. 火箭；v. 迅速增加
📖 英文解释
a cylindrical spacecraft propelled by engines; to increase rapidly
✏️ 例句
Housing prices have rocketed in the city center.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：rocket science（复杂的事情）；rocket to fame（迅速成名）；rocket launch（火箭发射）
🧠 助记
rocket看起来像rock（石头）+ et，石头射出去就像火箭
rocket::/ˈrɒkɪt/:n. 火箭:The startup's valuation rocketed after receiving venture capital funding.
3. alien /ˈeɪliən/ n. 外星人；外国人；adj. 陌生的；外来的
📖 英文解释
a being from another world; a foreigner; unfamiliar and strange
✏️ 例句
The concept of working remotely was completely alien to older generations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：alien to（对……陌生）；alien culture（外来文化）；illegal alien（非法移民，旧称）
🧠 助记
alien = ali + en，阿里巴巴也不认识的'异'类 = 外星人、陌生的
alien::/ˈeɪliən/:n. 外星人:The values of the new company were alien to the long-term employees.
4. devise /dɪˈvaɪz/ v. 设计；发明；想出（计划）
📖 英文解释
to plan or invent a complex procedure or mechanism; to think up
✏️ 例句
The team devised a new strategy to improve customer satisfaction.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：devise a plan/strategy/method（制定计划/策略/方法）；注意区分devise(设计)vs device(设备)
🧠 助记
de-（加强）+ vise（看、想）= 深思熟虑地想出 = 设计、发明
devise::/dɪˈvaɪz/:v. 设计:Scientists devised a new method to purify contaminated water.
5. exaggerate /ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/ v. 夸大；夸张；言过其实
📖 英文解释
to represent something as larger, better, or worse than it really is
✏️ 例句
The media tends to exaggerate the dangers of certain foods.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：exaggerate the importance of（夸大……的重要性）；exaggeration n.（夸张）；without exaggeration（毫不夸张）
🧠 助记
ex-（超出）+ aggerate（堆积）= 超出正常堆积 = 夸大
exaggerate::/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/:v. 夸大:It is not an exaggeration to say that climate change is a global crisis.
6. inherit /ɪnˈherɪt/ v. 继承；遗传；承受
📖 英文解释
to receive property or a title from someone when they die; to have a quality that came from a parent
✏️ 例句
She inherited her mother's talent for music.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：inherit from（从……继承）；inherit wealth/property（继承财富/财产）；inheritance n.（遗产；继承）
🧠 助记
in-（进入）+ herit（继承人）= 进入继承人的身份 = 继承
inherit::/ɪnˈherɪt/:v. 继承:The new government inherited a huge national debt from its predecessor.
7. intimate /ˈɪntɪmət/ adj. 亲密的；私人的；v. 暗示；n. 密友
📖 英文解释
closely acquainted; private and personal; to hint or suggest indirectly
✏️ 例句
They shared an intimate dinner to celebrate their anniversary.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：intimate relationship（亲密关系）；intimate knowledge（深入了解）；be intimate with（与……亲近）
🧠 助记
intimate = in + tim（时间）+ ate，花时间待在一起的 = 亲密的
intimate::/ˈɪntɪmət/:adj. 亲密的:The biography provides an intimate portrait of the artist's life.
8. opt /ɒpt/ v. 选择；决定选择
📖 英文解释
to make a choice or decision from a range of possibilities
✏️ 例句
Many students opt to study abroad for better opportunities.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：opt for（选择）；opt out of（选择退出）；opt in（选择加入）；option n.（选项）
🧠 助记
opt看起来短小，含义也简单：option的动词形式 = 选择
opt::/ɒpt/:v. 选择:She opted for a quieter lifestyle after retiring from the city.
9. ours /aʊərz/ pron. 我们的（所有物）
📖 英文解释
belonging to or associated with the speaker and one or more others
✏️ 例句
The responsibility for protecting the environment is ours.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研语法：ours是our的名词性物主代词，不需要跟名词；区分：our house = ours
🧠 助记
ours = our + s，our的名词形式，指'我们的东西'
ours::/aʊərz/:pron. 我们的（所有物）:The future of this planet is ours to protect.
10. troop /truːp/ n. 军队；一群；v. 成群移动
📖 英文解释
soldiers or armed forces; a group of people or animals; to move together in a group
✏️ 例句
The government deployed troops to restore order in the region.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：troop deployment（部队部署）；troop withdrawal（撤军）；a troop of（一群……）
🧠 助记
troop像'群'的形状，一大群士兵 = 军队；一群人
troop::/truːp/:n. 军队:Peacekeeping troops were sent to stabilize the conflict zone.
11. dilemma /dɪˈlemə/ n. 困境；两难局面；进退两难
📖 英文解释
a situation in which a difficult choice has to be made between two or more alternatives
✏️ 例句
She faced a dilemma: take the high-paying job or stay close to family.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：face a dilemma（面临困境）；ethical dilemma（伦理困境）；be in a dilemma（陷入两难）
🧠 助记
di-（两个）+ lemma（问题）= 两个问题都很棘手 = 两难困境
dilemma::/dɪˈlemə/:n. 困境:The manager was caught in a dilemma between cost-cutting and employee welfare.
12. spell /spel/ v. 拼写；意味着；n. 咒语；一段时间
📖 英文解释
to write or name the letters of a word; to lead to; a form of words with magic power; a period of time
✏️ 例句
Can you spell your last name for me?
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：spell out（详细说明）；spell trouble（意味着麻烦）；cold spell（一段寒冷天气）
🧠 助记
spell有多重含义：拼写、咒语（要记住所有字母）、一段时间
spell::/spel/:v. 拼写:The drought spelled disaster for farmers in the region.
13. thrill /θrɪl/ n./v. 激动；兴奋；使振奋
📖 英文解释
a sudden feeling of excitement or pleasure; to cause someone to feel very excited
✏️ 例句
She felt a thrill of excitement when she heard she had been accepted.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：thrill of（……的激动）；be thrilled at/by/with（对……感到兴奋）；thriller n.（惊悚片）
🧠 助记
thrill = thr + ill，兴奋到发抖，让你觉得'ill'（病态的兴奋）= 激动
thrill::/θrɪl/:n./v. 激动:The audience was thrilled by the performer's breathtaking acrobatics.
14. tribe /traɪb/ n. 部落；族群；一帮人
📖 英文解释
a social group in a traditional society; a group of people with a shared interest or character
✏️ 例句
The anthropologist spent years studying the customs of the indigenous tribe.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：tribal adj.（部落的）；tribal warfare（部落战争）；tribe of（一群……）
🧠 助记
tribe和'try'相关，部落里每个人都要努力try才能生存
tribe::/traɪb/:n. 部落:Social media has created new digital tribes based on shared interests.
15. blog /blɒɡ/ n. 博客；v. 写博客
📖 英文解释
a website where someone writes about their opinions, experiences, or activities regularly
✏️ 例句
She started a travel blog to document her adventures around the world.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研延伸：blogger n.（博主）；blog post（博文）；vlog（视频博客）= video + blog
🧠 助记
blog = web + log（日志），网络上的日志 = 博客
blog::/blɒɡ/:n. 博客:The company's CEO writes a weekly blog about industry trends.
16. deadly /ˈdedli/ adj. 致命的；极度的；adv. 极其
📖 英文解释
causing or able to cause death; extremely dangerous; very accurate
✏️ 例句
The snake's venom is deadly to humans.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：deadly disease/weapon（致命疾病/武器）；deadly serious（极其严肃）；deadly accurate（极其准确）
🧠 助记
dead（死）+ ly = 导致死亡的 = 致命的
deadly::/ˈdedli/:adj. 致命的:The researchers developed an antidote to the deadly toxin.
17. favorable /ˈfeɪvərəbl/ adj. 有利的；赞许的；顺利的
📖 英文解释
expressing approval; giving a positive impression; advantageous
✏️ 例句
The company received favorable reviews for its new product.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：favorable conditions（有利条件）；favorable impression（好印象）；favorable to（对……有利）
🧠 助记
favor（喜好、赞同）+ able = 可以被喜好的 = 有利的、受欢迎的
favorable::/ˈfeɪvərəbl/:adj. 有利的:The trade agreement was favorable to both countries.
18. herd /hɜːrd/ n. 兽群；v. 放牧；聚集
📖 英文解释
a large group of animals that live and feed together; to move or drive people or animals together
✏️ 例句
A herd of elephants crossed the river at sunset.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：herd mentality（从众心理）；herd of cattle/sheep（一群牛/羊）；herd sb into（把……赶进）
🧠 助记
herd和'赫'谐音，赫赫有名的'一大群'动物 = 兽群
herd::/hɜːrd/:n. 兽群:The herd mentality can lead people to make irrational financial decisions.
19. steam /stiːm/ n. 蒸汽；v. 蒸；充满干劲
📖 英文解释
water in the form of vapor; to cook using steam; to move fast or with great energy
✏️ 例句
The steam engine was one of the most important inventions of the Industrial Revolution.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：run out of steam（精疲力竭）；steam ahead（大力推进）；let off steam（发泄情绪）
🧠 助记
steam = s + team，一个团队的热气腾腾干劲 = 蒸汽、干劲
steam::/stiːm/:n. 蒸汽:The reform was steaming ahead despite political opposition.
20. strip /strɪp/ v. 剥去；脱衣；剥夺；n. 条带；地带
📖 英文解释
to remove the covering from something; to take away possessions or power; a narrow piece of something
✏️ 例句
The new law stripped citizens of some basic rights.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：strip of（剥夺……的）；strip down（拆卸；精简）；landing strip（着陆跑道）；comic strip（连环漫画）
🧠 助记
strip = 'strip'（脱）= 把东西剥掉，像脱衣服一样 = 剥去、剥夺
strip::/strɪp/:v. 剥去:The court ordered that his title be stripped due to misconduct.
21. diagnose /ˌdaɪəɡˈnoʊz/ v. 诊断；判断（问题原因）
📖 英文解释
to identify the nature of an illness by examining symptoms; to determine the cause of a problem
✏️ 例句
The doctor diagnosed the patient with Type 2 diabetes.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：diagnose sb with（诊断某人患有……）；diagnosis n.（诊断）；misdiagnose（误诊）
🧠 助记
dia-（穿越）+ gnose（知道）= 穿越表象了解病因 = 诊断
diagnose::/ˌdaɪəɡˈnoʊz/:v. 诊断:Early screening can diagnose cancer at a more treatable stage.
22. scratch /skrætʃ/ v. 抓；划；n. 划痕；起点
📖 英文解释
to mark a surface with a sharp object; to rub with nails; a mark or sound made by scratching
✏️ 例句
She had to start from scratch after losing all her work.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：from scratch（从头开始）；up to scratch（达到标准）；scratch the surface（浅尝辄止）
🧠 助记
scratch发音像'scr + atch'，指甲划过'嘶嘶'作响 = 抓、划
scratch::/skrætʃ/:v. 抓:The team had to rebuild the entire system from scratch.
23. wet /wet/ adj. 湿的；多雨的；v. 弄湿
📖 英文解释
covered with or full of water or another liquid; rainy; to make something wet
✏️ 例句
Don't go outside without an umbrella—the forecast says it will be wet all day.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：wet weather（湿冷天气）；wet blanket（扫兴的人）；soaking wet（湿透）
🧠 助记
wet和'挖特'类似，水挖出来的地方就是湿的 = 湿的
wet::/wet/:adj. 湿的:The construction was delayed due to consistently wet conditions.
24. dip /dɪp/ v. 蘸；浸；下降；n. 蘸料；下降
📖 英文解释
to put something briefly into a liquid; to decrease slightly; a short swim; a sauce for dipping
✏️ 例句
Share prices dipped slightly after the announcement.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：dip into（涉猎；动用）；dip in prices/temperature（价格/温度小幅下降）；skinny-dip（裸泳）
🧠 助记
dip短小精悍，快速往下一蘸 = 蘸；价格往下小降 = 下降
dip::/dɪp/:v. 蘸:The poll showed a slight dip in the president's approval ratings.
25. distress /dɪˈstres/ n. 忧虑；悲痛；危难；v. 使痛苦
📖 英文解释
extreme anxiety or suffering; a state of danger; to make someone feel unhappy
✏️ 例句
The news of the accident caused great distress to her family.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研搭配：in distress（处于困境）；signal of distress（求救信号）；cause distress（引起痛苦）；distressing adj.（令人痛苦的）
🧠 助记
dis-（坏的）+ stress（压力）= 坏的压力 = 忧虑、悲痛
distress::/dɪˈstres/:n. 忧虑:The refugees were in great distress after losing their homes.
