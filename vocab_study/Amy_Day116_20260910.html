1. grab /ɡræb/ v./n. 抓取；夺取；获取
📖 英文解释
To seize suddenly and roughly; to take the opportunity to do something quickly.
✏️ 例句
She grabbed her bag and rushed out of the office when she realized the meeting had already started.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ grab an opportunity 抓住机会 ★ grab sb's attention 吸引某人注意 ★ up for grabs 可供争夺 ☆ grabby adj. 贪婪的；land grab 土地掠夺 辨析：grab / seize / snatch：grab 突然抓取；seize 正式用语；snatch 猛抢
🧠 助记
grab 谐音『够了巴』——够了，我要抢过来
grab::/ɡræb/:v./n. 抓取:The company grabbed the opportunity to expand into Southeast Asia.
2. miserable /ˈmɪzərəbəl/ adj. 悲惨的；痛苦的；很差的
📖 英文解释
Very unhappy or uncomfortable; causing unhappiness; of poor quality.
✏️ 例句
Living conditions in overcrowded urban slums are often miserable and lead to serious health problems.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ feel/look miserable 感觉/看起来很痛苦 ★ a miserable failure/performance 惨败/糟糕的表现 ☆ miserably adv. 悲惨地；misery n. 苦难 辨析：miserable / wretched / unfortunate：miserable 侧重主观痛苦体验；wretched 更文学化；unfortunate 侧重运气不好
🧠 助记
miser（守财奴）+ able——守财奴过着悲惨生活
miserable::/ˈmɪzərəbəl/:adj. 悲惨的:The refugees lived in miserable conditions without clean water or shelter.
3. practitioner /præk ˈtɪʃənər/ n. 从业者；执业者（尤指医疗/法律）
📖 英文解释
A person actively engaged in a profession, especially medicine or law.
✏️ 例句
General practitioners play a crucial role in the healthcare system by providing primary care to patients.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ medical/legal/general practitioner 医疗/法律/全科医生 ★ experienced practitioner 有经验的从业者 ☆ practice n. 执业；实践；practise v. 练习/执业 辨析：practitioner / professional / expert：practitioner 强调实际从业者身份
🧠 助记
practice（实践）+ ioner（人）——实践操作的人，就是从业者
practitioner::/præk ˈtɪʃənər/:n. 从业者:The medical practitioner recommended a course of antibiotics for the infection.
4. proposition /ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃən/ n. 命题；提议；主张；难题
📖 英文解释
A statement expressing a judgment or opinion; a proposal or suggestion; a task considered in terms of difficulty.
✏️ 例句
The committee rejected the proposition that corporate tax should be significantly reduced.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ make/put forward a proposition 提出命题/提议 ★ a tough/attractive proposition 棘手的/吸引人的提案 ☆ propose v. 提议；proposal n. 提案 辨析：proposition / proposal / suggestion：proposition 偏正式命题；proposal 偏正式提案；suggestion 一般建议
🧠 助记
pro（向前）+ position（位置）——把某个立场向前推出，就是命题/提议
proposition::/ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃən/:n. 命题:The business proposition was rejected due to its high financial risk.
5. qualify /ˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/ v. 合格；取得资格；限定
📖 英文解释
To be entitled to a particular benefit or privilege; to become officially recognized as a practitioner.
✏️ 例句
Students must complete a two-year internship to qualify as certified public accountants.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ qualify as/for 取得…资格/符合…条件 ★ qualify a statement 对陈述加以限定 ☆ qualification n. 资格；qualified adj. 有资质的 辨析：qualify / eligible：qualify 强调达到要求的过程；eligible 强调符合条件的状态
🧠 助记
quali（质量，quality）+ fy（使）——使达到质量要求，就是合格
qualify::/ˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/:v. 合格:You need a degree and five years' experience to qualify for the position.
6. sole /səʊl/ adj. 唯一的；单独的；n. 鞋底；比目鱼
📖 英文解释
One and only; belonging to one person; the undersole of a shoe; a type of flatfish.
✏️ 例句
As the sole breadwinner in the family, she felt enormous pressure to maintain her income.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ the sole purpose/reason/survivor 唯一目的/原因/幸存者 ★ sole responsibility/control 单独责任/控制 ☆ solely adv. 仅仅；唯一地 辨析：sole / only / single：sole 最正式，强调独一无二；only 通用；single 侧重单个
🧠 助记
sole 谐音『索了』——就索了这一个，唯一的
sole::/səʊl/:adj. 唯一的:The sole aim of the campaign was to raise awareness about mental health.
7. spark /spɑːk/ n./v. 火星；引起；激发
📖 英文解释
A small fiery particle; to ignite; to cause something to start or develop.
✏️ 例句
The publication of the report sparked a fierce public debate about the ethics of artificial intelligence.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ spark a debate/controversy/revolution 引发争论/争议/革命 ★ a spark of hope/inspiration 一线希望/灵感的火花 ☆ sparkle v. 闪烁；发光；sparkling adj. 辨析：spark / trigger / ignite：spark 引发（常为意外）；trigger 触发；ignite 点燃（较正式）
🧠 助记
spark 像火花的声音『叭』——引发出一粒火星
spark::/spɑːk/:n./v. 火星:The new policy sparked protests across the country.
8. sportsman /ˈspɔːrtsmən/ n. 运动员；体育健将
📖 英文解释
A person who takes part in sport, especially outdoor sports; a person who behaves fairly and generously.
✏️ 例句
A true sportsman accepts both victory and defeat with equal grace and dignity.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ true/great sportsman 真正的/伟大的运动员 ★ sportsmanship n. 体育精神 ☆ sportswoman n. 女运动员；sportsperson 性别中立 注意：sportsmanship（体育精神）是考研写作中的高频正面词汇
🧠 助记
sports（运动）+ man（人）——参与运动的人
sportsman::/ˈspɔːrtsmən/:n. 运动员:He showed true sportsmanship by congratulating his opponent after the loss.
9. tissue /ˈtɪʃuː/ n. 纸巾；组织（生物）；薄纸
📖 英文解释
Soft thin paper used as a disposable handkerchief; any of the distinct types of material of which animals and plants are made.
✏️ 例句
New technologies now allow scientists to grow human tissue in the laboratory for medical research.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ tissue paper 薄纸；卫生纸 ★ brain/muscle tissue 脑/肌肉组织 ★ a tissue of lies 一套谎言（固定表达） ☆ tissue engineering 组织工程（医学热词） 辨析：tissue / organ：tissue 是器官 organ 的组成部分
🧠 助记
tissue 谐音『提需』——提出需求时用纸巾擦眼泪
tissue::/ˈtɪʃuː/:n. 纸巾:Scientists successfully created artificial tissue to replace damaged cartilage.
10. toll /təʊl/ n. 损失；伤亡人数；通行费；v. 鸣钟
📖 英文解释
A charge paid for using a road, bridge, or other service; damage or suffering caused; to sound a bell slowly.
✏️ 例句
The years of conflict took a heavy toll on the nation's infrastructure and civilian population.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ take a (heavy) toll on 对…造成（沉重）损失 ★ toll road/bridge 收费公路/桥 ★ death toll 死亡人数（考研常见） ☆ tolling n. 钟声；toll-free adj. 免费拨打的 辨析：toll / cost / price：toll 强调代价/损耗；cost 经济代价；price 具体价格
🧠 助记
toll 谐音『夺了』——代价是夺走了你的某些东西
toll::/təʊl/:n. 损失:The drought took a severe toll on agriculture in the region.
11. verify /ˈverɪfaɪ/ v. 核实；证实；验证
📖 英文解释
To make sure or demonstrate that something is true, accurate, or justified.
✏️ 例句
Journalists must verify their sources before publishing any sensitive or controversial information.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ verify information/identity/data 核实信息/身份/数据 ★ verified account 经过认证的账户 ☆ verification n. 核实；verifiable adj. 可核实的 辨析：verify / confirm / authenticate：verify 核实事实；confirm 正式确认；authenticate 验证真实性
🧠 助记
veri（真，verify→very true）+ fy（使）——使之成为真实，就是核实
verify::/ˈverɪfaɪ/:v. 核实:Please verify your email address to complete the registration.
12. compulsory /kəmˈpʌlsəri/ adj. 强制的；义务的；必须的
📖 英文解释
Required by law or a rule; obligatory.
✏️ 例句
Compulsory education up to the age of 16 ensures that all children receive basic literacy and numeracy skills.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ compulsory education/military service 义务教育/兵役 ★ compulsory subject/course 必修课 ☆ compulsion n. 强迫；compel v. 强迫 辨析：compulsory / mandatory / obligatory：三者均表必须，compulsory 强调由法律/规定强制；mandatory 法律强制；obligatory 正式义务
🧠 助记
compul（强迫，compel）+ sory——强迫做的，就是义务/强制
compulsory::/kəmˈpʌlsəri/:adj. 强制的:Wearing a seatbelt is compulsory for all vehicle passengers.
13. cyberspace /ˈsaɪbəspeɪs/ n. 网络空间；虚拟空间

✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ in cyberspace 在网络空间中 ★ cyberspace security 网络空间安全 ☆ cybercrime n. 网络犯罪；cyberattack n. 网络攻击 注意：考研科技/社会类文章中高频词汇，尤其与网络安全、隐私相关
🧠 助记
cyber（网络）+ space（空间）——网络上的虚拟空间
cyberspace::/ˈsaɪbəspeɪs/:n. 网络空间:Protecting personal data in cyberspace is increasingly important.
14. dawn /dɔːn/ n. 黎明；开端；v. 破晓；开始理解
📖 英文解释
The first appearance of daylight; the beginning of a new period; to become evident to the mind.
✏️ 例句
The dawn of the digital age transformed how people work, communicate, and access information.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ at dawn 黎明时分 ★ the dawn of a new era 新时代的黎明 ★ it dawned on sb that 某人突然意识到… ☆ daybreak n. 黎明（较正式）；dusk 黄昏（对义词） 辨析：dawn / daybreak / sunrise：dawn 最广，含比喻义；daybreak 与 dawn 同义；sunrise 日出
🧠 助记
dawn 谐音『当』——当——天亮了，黎明到来
dawn::/dɔːn/:n. 黎明:The invention of the internet marked the dawn of a new era in communication.
15. kick /kɪk/ v./n. 踢；踢球；刺激感

✏️ 例句
The new incentive program was designed to kick-start innovation across all departments of the company.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ kick off 开始；拉开序幕 ★ kick-start sth 启动/激发某事（比喻） ★ get a kick out of 从…获得乐趣 ☆ kick the habit 戒掉习惯 辨析：kick / boot：kick 泛指踢；boot 特指用脚猛踢
🧠 助记
kick 发音像踢球的声音『咔』——踢出去
kick::/kɪk/:v./n. 踢:The government's stimulus package was intended to kick-start economic growth.

The elected head of government of a city or town.

★ the mayor of a city 某城市的市长 ★ run for mayor 竞选市长 ☆ mayoral adj. 市长的；mayoralty n. 市长职位 注意：考研阅读中 mayor 常与城市治理、政策、选举主题相关

mayor::/meər/:n. 市长:The mayor pledged to reduce air pollution by 50% within five years.
17. subjective /səbˈdʒektɪv/ adj. 主观的；个人的；不客观的
📖 英文解释
Based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes, or opinions; not objective.
✏️ 例句
Art criticism is inherently subjective, as different viewers respond differently to the same work.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ subjective judgment/opinion 主观判断/观点 ★ highly subjective 高度主观的 ☆ subjectivity n. 主观性；objective adj. 客观的（反义） 辨析：subjective / personal / biased：subjective 中性，指基于个人感受；biased 含负面偏见义
🧠 助记
subject（主体）+ ive——从主体自身出发的，就是主观的
subjective::/səbˈdʒektɪv/:adj. 主观的:Assessments of beauty are largely subjective and culturally influenced.
18. timely /ˈtaɪmli/ adj. 及时的；适时的
📖 英文解释
Done or occurring at a favorable or useful time; opportune.
✏️ 例句
The timely intervention of the paramedics saved the accident victim's life.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ a timely reminder/warning/response 及时的提醒/警告/回应 ★ timely manner 及时的方式 ☆ timeliness n. 及时性；timely ≠ timeless（永恒的） 辨析：timely / prompt / punctual：timely 强调时机合适；prompt 强调迅速反应；punctual 强调守时
🧠 助记
time（时间）+ ly（副词/形容词后缀）——把握时间，就是及时的
timely::/ˈtaɪmli/:adj. 及时的:The government issued a timely warning about the approaching typhoon.

★ hospital ward 医院病房 ★ ward off danger/illness 避开危险/疾病 ★ electoral ward 选区 ☆ warden n. 看守；监管人；wardrobe n. 衣橱（ward的延伸） 辨析：ward / ward off：ward（名词）病房；ward off（动词短语）避开

ward 谐音『保的』——保护人的地方，就是病房/监护
ward::/wɔːrd/:n. 病房:Volunteers were asked to help ward off loneliness among elderly patients.
20. wrap /ræp/ v./n. 包裹；裹；包装材料

wrap 谐音『绕啊』——绕着绕着，就是包裹起来
wrap::/ræp/:v./n. 包裹:She wrapped the gift carefully before placing it under the tree.
21. altitude /ˈæltɪtjuːd/ n. 高度；海拔；高处

✏️ 例句

🧠 助记
alti（高，alt键 = 替换）+ tude——高高在上的状态，就是海拔/高度
altitude::/ˈæltɪtjuːd/:n. 高度:The aircraft maintained a cruising altitude of 35,000 feet.

24. drown /draʊn/ v. 淹没；溺死；压过（声音）

★ drown in sth 被…淹没（比喻：drown in debt/work 债务/工作缠身） ★ drown out the noise 压过噪音 ★ drown one's sorrows 借酒浇愁 ☆ drowning adj. 溺水中的；drowsy adj. 昏昏欲睡（非同根词，但易混） 辨析：drown / submerge：drown 侧重致死；submerge 侧重浸入水中
🧠 助记
drown 谐音『溺完』——溺水完了，就是淹没
drown::/draʊn/:v. 淹没:She felt as if she were drowning in a sea of paperwork.
25. empire /ˈempaɪər/ n. 帝国；庞大组织；大企业

A group of countries ruled by a single authority; a large commercial organization under one ownership.

The Roman Empire's contributions to law, language, and architecture continue to influence Western civilization.

★ build an empire 建立帝国（比喻商业帝国） ★ the British Empire 大英帝国 ★ media/business empire 媒体/商业帝国 ☆ emperor n. 皇帝；imperial adj. 帝国的；imperialism n. 帝国主义 辨析：empire / kingdom / republic：empire 最大；kingdom 王国；republic 共和国
🧠 助记
em（=in，进入）+ pire（来自 pyre 火堆）——进入烈火之中，就是帝国的征服
empire::/ˈempaɪər/:n. 帝国:The tech giant has built a digital empire worth trillions of dollars.
