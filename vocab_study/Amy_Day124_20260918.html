1. shortcoming /ˈʃɔːtkʌmɪŋ/ n. 缺点；短处；不足之处
📖 英文解释
a fault or failure to reach a certain standard; a deficiency or weakness
✏️ 例句
Despite its shortcomings, the plan was approved due to time constraints.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
shortcomings of(…的缺点); overcome shortcomings(克服不足); personal shortcomings(个人缺点)
🧠 助记
short(不足)+coming(来到) -> 不足之处显现 -> 缺点;短处
shortcoming::/ˈʃɔːtkʌmɪŋ/:n. 缺点:The review identified several shortcomings in the current healthcare system.
2. subordinate /səˈbɔːdɪnət/ adj. 下级的；从属的；n. 下属；v. 使从属于
📖 英文解释
lower in rank or position; less important; a person under the authority of another
✏️ 例句
The manager always listened to suggestions from his subordinates.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
subordinate to(从属于); subordinate clause(从句); superior and subordinate(上下级)
🧠 助记
sub(下面)+ordin(排序,词根)+ate -> 排序在下面的 -> 下级的;从属的
subordinate::/səˈbɔːdɪnət/:adj. 下级的:Personal interests must sometimes be subordinated to the needs of the team.
3. drag /dræɡ/ v. 拖；拽；拖延；n. 阻力；令人厌烦的事
📖 英文解释
to pull something along the ground with difficulty; to move slowly; a hindrance or something tedious
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
drag on(拖延); drag one's feet(拖拖拉拉); main drag(主街道); drag effect(拖累效应)
🧠 助记
drag->发音像'拖'->拖着走->拖;拽
drag::/dræɡ/:v. 拖:The prolonged legal dispute dragged on and drained the company's resources.

expenditure::/ɪkˈspendɪtʃər/:n. 支出:The report revealed that defense expenditure had doubled over the past decade.

6. grey /ɡreɪ/ adj./n. 灰色(的)；灰暗的；v. 变灰
