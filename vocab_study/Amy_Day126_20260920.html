1. inertia /ɪˈnɜːrʃə/ n. 惯性；惰性；不活跃
📖 英文解释
the tendency to remain unchanged; in physics, the property of matter by which it continues in its existing state unless changed by external force
✏️ 例句
Organizational inertia prevented the company from adapting to market changes.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
overcome inertia（克服惰性）；political/social inertia；law of inertia（惯性定律）
🧠 助记
inert（不活跃的）+ ia → 不活跃的状态 → 惯性；惰性
inertia::/ɪˈnɜːrʃə/:n. 惯性:The government's inertia on climate policy drew widespread criticism.
2. lately /ˈleɪtli/ adv. 最近；近来
📖 英文解释
recently; in the recent past
✏️ 例句
Have you been sleeping well lately?
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
lately = recently，多用于现在完成时；lately he has been...；of late（近来，更正式）
🧠 助记
late（晚/近期）+ ly → 在近期 → 最近
lately::/ˈleɪtli/:adv. 最近:She has been working extremely hard lately.
3. lump /lʌmp/ n. 块；肿块 v. 归并；凑合接受
📖 英文解释
a compact mass of a substance; a swelling under the skin; to put together in one group
✏️ 例句
She found a small lump in her neck and went to see the doctor.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
lump sum（一次性总付）；lump together（归并）；lump it（忍受）；sugar lump（方糖）
🧠 助记
lump 发音像'蓝姆普'→ 想象一个蓝色小肿块 → 块；肿块
lump::/lʌmp/:n. 块:He was paid a lump sum for the project.
4. ninety /ˈnaɪnti/ num. 九十
📖 英文解释
the number 90
✏️ 例句
My grandmother is in her nineties and still very active.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in one's nineties（九十多岁）；the nineties（九十年代）；ninety percent（百分之九十）
🧠 助记
nine + ty（-ty 表示整十）→ 九十
ninety::/ˈnaɪnti/:num. 九十:Over ninety percent of students passed the exam.
5. plentiful /ˈplentɪfəl/ adj. 丰富的；大量的；充足的
📖 英文解释
existing in or producing large quantities; more than enough
✏️ 例句
Food was plentiful during the harvest season.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
plentiful supply/harvest；plentiful in（在…方面富足）；plenty n. → plentiful adj.
🧠 助记
plenty（大量）+ ful（充满）→ 充满大量的 → 丰富的
plentiful::/ˈplentɪfəl/:adj. 丰富的:The region has plentiful natural resources.
6. revolve /rɪˈvɒlv/ v. 旋转；围绕；以…为中心
📖 英文解释
to move in a circle around a central point; to have something as the main focus
✏️ 例句
The Earth revolves around the Sun once every 365 days.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
revolve around（围绕）；life revolves around sb/sth（生活以…为中心）；revolving door（旋转门）
🧠 助记
re-（反复）+ volve（滚动，来自 volume）→ 反复滚动 → 旋转
revolve::/rɪˈvɒlv/:v. 旋转:His entire world revolves around his work.
7. theirs /ðerz/ pron. 他们的（所有物）；她们的
📖 英文解释
used to refer to a thing or things belonging to or associated with two or more people previously mentioned
✏️ 例句
Our house is smaller than theirs.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
a friend of theirs（他们的一个朋友）；注意 theirs 无撇号，不是 their's
🧠 助记
their（他们的）+ s → 他们的（独立所有格）→ 他们的东西
theirs::/ðerz/:pron. 他们的（所有物）:The decision was theirs to make.
8. harmony /ˈhɑːrməni/ n. 和谐；协调；和睦；和声
📖 英文解释
a pleasing combination of different elements; a state of peaceful coexistence; musical notes played or sung together pleasantly
✏️ 例句
The two countries live in harmony despite their differences.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in harmony with（与…和谐）；racial/social harmony；harmony between/among；harmonious adj.
🧠 助记
harm（伤害）→ 去掉harm就是和谐；反面助记：没有伤害=和谐
harmony::/ˈhɑːrməni/:n. 和谐:The choir sang in perfect harmony.
9. breakdown /ˈbreɪkdaʊn/ n. 崩溃；故障；分解；统计细目
📖 英文解释
a failure to work or function; a physical or mental collapse; a detailed analysis of something
✏️ 例句
There was a breakdown in communication between the two teams.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
nervous breakdown（精神崩溃）；breakdown of talks（谈判破裂）；car breakdown（汽车故障）；breakdown of costs
🧠 助记
break（断裂）+ down（向下）→ 断裂向下 → 崩溃；故障
breakdown::/ˈbreɪkdaʊn/:n. 崩溃:The breakdown of negotiations led to increased tensions.
10. dash /dæʃ/ v. 猛冲；急奔 n. 短跑；破折号；少量
📖 英文解释
to run or go somewhere quickly; a sudden quick movement; a small amount of a liquid
✏️ 例句
She made a dash for the exit when she heard the fire alarm.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
dash off（匆忙离开/赶写）；dash sb's hopes（破灭某人希望）；100-metre dash（百米短跑）；a dash of salt
🧠 助记
dash 发音像'得刷'→ 刷的一下冲出去 → 猛冲
dash::/dæʃ/:v. 猛冲:He made a desperate dash to catch the last train.
11. flee /fliː/ v. 逃跑；逃离；消散
📖 英文解释
to run away from a place or situation of danger; to move quickly and suddenly
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
flee 联想 fly（飞走）→ 飞速逃跑 → 逃跑
flee::/fliː/:v. 逃跑:The family was forced to flee their home due to the flooding.
12. humorous /ˈhjuːmərəs/ adj. 幽默的；滑稽的；诙谐的

🧠 助记
humor（幽默）+ ous（形容词后缀）→ 充满幽默的 → 幽默的
humorous::/ˈhjuːmərəs/:adj. 幽默的:He is known for his humorous approach to serious topics.
13. kit /kɪt/ n. 装备；工具包；套件 v. 装备

a set of equipment or tools needed for a particular purpose; a set of parts to be assembled

🧠 助记
kit 联想 'kitted out'（全副武装）→ 装备；套件
kit::/kɪt/:n. 装备:She put together a survival kit for camping trips.
