1. bucket /ˈbʌkɪt/ n. 桶；大量；v. 倾盆而下
📖 英文解释
a roughly cylindrical container with a handle for carrying liquids; a large amount; to rain very heavily
✏️ 例句
She carried a bucket of water from the well.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
a bucket of water/sand；bucket list（临终清单）；kick the bucket（俚语：死亡）；it's bucketing down（倾盆大雨）
🧠 助记
buck(鹿，大)+et(小)→大小容器→桶
bucket::/ˈbʌkɪt/:n. 桶:The rain poured down in buckets, flooding the streets within minutes.
2. defect /ˈdiːfekt/ n. /dɪˈfekt/ v. n. 缺陷；缺点；v. 叛逃；变节
📖 英文解释
a fault or imperfection in something; to leave your country or organization and join an opposing one
✏️ 例句
The product was recalled due to a manufacturing defect.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
a defect in/of sth；birth defect（先天缺陷）；defect to（叛逃到）；defective adj.有缺陷的
🧠 助记
de(离开)+fect(做，perfect完美)→离开完美→缺陷；也指人离开组织→叛逃
defect::/ˈdiːfekt/ n. /dɪˈfekt/ v.:n. 缺陷:Quality inspectors found a critical defect in the aircraft's landing gear.
3. detective /dɪˈtektɪv/ n. 侦探；adj. 侦探的
📖 英文解释
a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
private detective（私家侦探）；detective novel/story（侦探小说）；detective work（侦查工作）
🧠 助记
detect(侦测)+ive(人)→专门侦测的人→侦探
detective::/dɪˈtektɪv/:n. 侦探:The detective pieced together the evidence to identify the prime suspect.
4. disrupt /dɪsˈrʌpt/ v. 扰乱；中断；颠覆
📖 英文解释
to interrupt something and prevent it from continuing normally; to cause disorder or problems in a system
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
disrupt services/plans/order；disruptive adj.破坏性的；disruption n.中断；tech: disruptive technology（颠覆性技术）
🧠 助记
dis(破坏)+rupt(断裂，rupture)→断裂破坏→扰乱，中断
disrupt::/dɪsˈrʌpt/:v. 扰乱:The new technology threatened to disrupt the entire financial industry.

identification::/aɪˌdentɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/:n. 识别:Passengers must present valid identification at the airport security checkpoint.
6. loose /luːs/ adj. 松的；宽松的；不精确的；v. 释放

loose::/luːs/:adj. 松的:The report contained some loose reasoning that critics quickly challenged.
