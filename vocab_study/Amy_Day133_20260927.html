1. duration /djʊˈreɪʃn/ n. 持续时间；期间
📖 英文解释
the length of time that something lasts or continues
✏️ 例句
The duration of the flight from London to New York is about seven hours.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
for the duration of（在…期间）；the duration of a contract；short/long duration；考研法律/合同类常见
🧠 助记
dur(持续，durable)+ation→持续下去的时间→持续时间
duration::/djʊˈreɪʃn/:n. 持续时间:Employees are required to attend all training sessions for their full duration.
2. resent /rɪˈzent/ v. 愤恨；不满；怨恨
📖 英文解释
to feel bitter or angry about something that you consider unfair
✏️ 例句
She resented being treated as inferior by her older colleagues.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
resent sb/sth；resent doing sth；resentment n.愤恨；resentful adj.愤恨的；bitter resentment（强烈愤恨）
🧠 助记
re(反)+sent(感受)→反向感受→怨恨，不满
resent::/rɪˈzent/:v. 愤恨:Many workers resented the new policy that reduced their annual leave entitlement.
3. slack /slæk/ adj. 松弛的；马虎的；n. 淡季；v. 偷懒
📖 英文解释
not tight or tense; not active or busy; to avoid working hard; a period of little business activity
✏️ 例句
The rope became slack after one end was untied.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
slack off（偷懒；减速）；take up the slack（弥补不足）；slack season（淡季）；cut sb some slack（对某人宽容点）
🧠 助记
sl(松弛的感觉)+ack→松松地→松弛的，懈怠的
slack::/slæk/:adj. 松弛的:The manager warned staff not to slack off during the quiet holiday period.
4. unify /ˈjuːnɪfaɪ/ v. 使统一；使成一体
📖 英文解释
to make separate things work together or join together as a whole
✏️ 例句
The new curriculum aims to unify educational standards across all schools.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
unify a country/party；unified adj.统一的；unification n.统一；考研历史/政治类：German unification（德国统一）
🧠 助记
uni(单一，uniform)+fy(使变成)→使变成一个整体→统一
unify::/ˈjuːnɪfaɪ/:v. 使统一:The new legislation sought to unify conflicting regulations across different provinces.
5. upright /ˈʌpraɪt/ adj. 竖直的；正直的；adv. 笔直地；n. 直立物
📖 英文解释
standing or sitting straight; morally correct and honest; in a vertical position
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
sit/stand upright；an upright citizen（正直的公民）；upright piano（立式钢琴）；keep upright（保持直立）
🧠 助记
up(向上)+right(正确)→向上挺直就是正确姿态→直立的，正直的
upright::/ˈʌpraɪt/:adj. 竖直的:He was known as an upright official who refused to accept any form of bribery.
6. chew /tʃuː/ v. 咀嚼；细想；n. 咀嚼物
📖 英文解释
to bite food into smaller pieces with the teeth before swallowing; to think carefully about something
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
chew=jaw(下巴)演变→用下巴嚼→咀嚼
chew::/tʃuː/:v. 咀嚼:She needed time to chew over the proposal before giving a definitive answer.

dense::/dens/:adj. 密集的:The dense urban population created significant pressure on local infrastructure.
