1. librarian /laɪˈbreəriən/ n. 图书管理员
📖 英文解释
A person who works in a library, managing and helping users access books and information resources.
✏️ 例句
The librarian helped her find books on ancient history.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
school/public librarian 学校/公共图书管理员；librarian skills 信息管理能力
🧠 助记
library（图书馆）+ -ian（人）→ 在图书馆工作的人 → 图书管理员
librarian::/laɪˈbreəriən/:n. 图书管理员:The librarian organized a reading program for local children.
2. lover /ˈlʌvər/ n. 情人；爱好者
📖 英文解释
A person who is in a romantic relationship; someone who enjoys something greatly.
✏️ 例句
She was a lover of classical music.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
music/art/nature lover 音乐/艺术/自然爱好者；take a lover 有情人；lover's lane 情人小径
🧠 助记
love（爱）+ -r → 爱的人 → 情人、爱好者
lover::/ˈlʌvər/:n. 情人:As a lover of literature, he spent hours browsing the bookstore.
3. marital /ˈmærɪtl/ adj. 婚姻的；夫妻的
📖 英文解释
Relating to marriage or the relationship between spouses.
✏️ 例句
Marital problems can arise from poor communication.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
marital status 婚姻状况；marital bliss/problems 婚姻幸福/问题；extramarital 婚外的
🧠 助记
mari-（= marry 婚姻）+ -tal（形容词后缀）→ 婚姻的；联想 married
marital::/ˈmærɪtl/:adj. 婚姻的:The survey asked respondents to report their marital status.
4. needle /ˈniːdl/ n. 针；指针 v. 激怒；针刺
📖 英文解释
A thin pointed metal instrument used in sewing or medical procedures; a pointer on a gauge; to provoke or tease.
✏️ 例句
She threaded the needle carefully before sewing the button.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
a needle in a haystack 大海捞针；needle sb 激怒某人；compass needle 指南针指针
🧠 助记
联想 need（需要）→ 缝纫必需品 → 针；like looking for a needle in a haystack
needle::/ˈniːdl/:n. 针:Finding a solution felt like looking for a needle in a haystack.
5. precedent /ˈpresɪdənt/ n. 先例；惯例 adj. 先前的
📖 英文解释
An earlier event or action that serves as an example or guide for future situations; a legal decision used as a basis for later cases.
✏️ 例句
The court's decision set a legal precedent for similar cases.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
set a precedent 树立先例；without precedent 史无前例；follow/break precedent 遵循/打破先例
🧠 助记
pre-（之前）+ cede（走）+ -nt → 走在前面的 → 先例；联想 president（总统先走一步）
precedent::/ˈpresɪdənt/:n. 先例:The landmark ruling set a precedent for future environmental litigation.
6. pub /pʌb/ n. 酒吧；酒馆
📖 英文解释
A place where people go to drink alcoholic beverages and socialize; a public house.
✏️ 例句
After work, they went to the pub for a drink.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
go to the pub 去酒吧；pub crawl 逛多家酒吧；local pub 附近的酒吧
🧠 助记
pub 是 public house 的缩写 → 公共饮酒场所 → 酒吧
pub::/pʌb/:n. 酒吧:The old pub at the corner of the street has been serving locals for over a century.
7. reckless /ˈrekləs/ adj. 鲁莽的；不顾后果的
📖 英文解释
Acting without thinking about the consequences; showing a lack of care about danger.
✏️ 例句
Reckless driving causes thousands of accidents each year.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
reckless driving/behavior/abandon 鲁莽驾驶/行为/放肆；recklessly adv.
🧠 助记
reck（考虑、在意）+ -less（无）→ 不在意后果 → 鲁莽的
reckless::/ˈrekləs/:adj. 鲁莽的:The reckless decision to cut costs led to a series of safety failures.
8. spill /spɪl/ v. 溢出；泄漏；散落 n. 溢出量
📖 英文解释
To cause a liquid to flow out of its container accidentally; to fall out or over; an instance of liquid spilling.
✏️ 例句
She accidentally spilled coffee on her laptop.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
oil spill 石油泄漏；spill over 蔓延；spill the beans 泄露秘密
🧠 助记
联想 split（分裂）→ 液体从容器中分出来 → 溢出、泄漏
spill::/spɪl/:v. 溢出:The oil spill caused serious damage to the coastal ecosystem.
9. stair /steər/ n. 楼梯（常用复数 stairs）
📖 英文解释
A series of steps leading from one floor to another in a building.
✏️ 例句
She walked up the stairs to the second floor.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
flight of stairs 一段楼梯；staircase 楼梯（整体）；stairway 楼道
🧠 助记
联想 stair 和 star → 顺着楼梯上去就能摘星星 → 楼梯
stair::/steər/:n. 楼梯（常用复数 stairs）:He tripped on the stairs and sprained his ankle.
10. ashamed /əˈʃeɪmd/ adj. 羞耻的；惭愧的
📖 英文解释
Feeling embarrassed or guilty because of one's own or another's behavior.
✏️ 例句
He was ashamed of his rude behavior at the meeting.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
be ashamed of 为……感到羞耻；feel ashamed 感到惭愧；shame n./v.
🧠 助记
a-（加强）+ shame（羞耻）+ -d → 充满羞耻感 → 惭愧的、羞耻的
ashamed::/əˈʃeɪmd/:adj. 羞耻的:She felt deeply ashamed after realizing she had spread false information.
11. aunt /ɑːnt/ n. 姑姑；姨母；伯母；婶婶
📖 英文解释
The sister of one's father or mother, or the wife of one's uncle.
✏️ 例句
My aunt lives in the countryside and keeps chickens.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
发音像"安"→ 安慰你的长辈 → 姑姑、姨母
aunt::/ɑːnt/:n. 姑姑:She spent summer holidays at her aunt's house in the countryside.
12. bald /bɔːld/ adj. 秃头的；单调的；直截了当的
📖 英文解释
Having no hair on the head; lacking natural covering; blunt and plain.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

bald::/bɔːld/:adj. 秃头的:The bald statement of facts left no room for misinterpretation.
13. bankrupt /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ adj. 破产的 v. 使破产 n. 破产者
📖 英文解释
Legally declared unable to pay debts; completely lacking in a particular quality; to ruin financially.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

bankrupt::/ˈbæŋkrʌpt/:adj. 破产的 v. 使破产 n. 破:Several small businesses went bankrupt during the economic downturn.
14. choke /tʃəʊk/ v. 窒息；抑制；梗塞 n. 阻塞
📖 英文解释
To prevent breathing by blocking the throat; to fill so as to block; to suppress or inhibit.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

choke::/tʃəʊk/:v. 窒息:She choked back tears as she delivered the farewell speech.

🧠 助记

cumulative::/ˈkjuːmjʊlətɪv/:adj. 累积的:The cumulative impact of small daily choices shapes long-term outcomes.

dubious::/ˈdjuːbiəs/:adj. 可疑的:The committee was dubious about the feasibility of the proposed plan.

eagle::/ˈiːɡl/:n. 鹰:She kept an eagle eye on market trends to identify investment opportunities.

gently::/ˈdʒentli/:adv. 温柔地:She gently reminded her students that deadlines were approaching.

integral::/ˈɪntɪɡrəl/:adj. 完整的:Trust is an integral part of any successful working relationship.
20. mediate /ˈmiːdieɪt/ v. 调停；斡旋；居中调解

To intervene between parties in a dispute to help them reach agreement; to bring about by acting as an intermediary.

mediate::/ˈmiːdieɪt/:v. 调停:A neutral third party was brought in to mediate the labor dispute.

organise::/ˈɔːɡənaɪz/:v. 组织:The volunteers organised a community cleanup drive over the weekend.
22. overtake /ˌəʊvəˈteɪk/ v. 赶上；超越；突然袭来
📖 英文解释
To catch up with and pass while traveling in the same direction; to surpass; to come upon suddenly.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
overtake a car 超车；be overtaken by events 被事态超越；overtaken by emotion 被情绪淹没
🧠 助记

overtake::/ˌəʊvəˈteɪk/:v. 赶上:Rising economies began to overtake traditional industrial powers in productivity.
23. pulse /pʌls/ n. 脉搏；脉冲；豆类 v. 跳动
📖 英文解释
The rhythmic beating of the heart felt in blood vessels; a single vibration; to beat rhythmically.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
take/check one's pulse 量脉搏；pulse rate 脉率；feel the pulse of 了解……的动态
🧠 助记
联想 push（推）→ 心脏一推一拉 → 脉搏跳动 → pulse
pulse::/pʌls/:n. 脉搏:The doctor monitored the patient's pulse throughout the procedure.

quantify risk/impact/data 量化风险/影响/数据；hard to quantify 难以量化
🧠 助记

quantify::/ˈkwɒntɪfaɪ/:v. 量化:Researchers struggled to quantify the long-term benefits of the new policy.

respective::/rɪˈspektɪv/:adj. 各自的:The two departments resumed their respective responsibilities after restructuring.
