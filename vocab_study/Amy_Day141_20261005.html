1. bleed /bliːd/ v. 出血；流血；渗色
📖 英文解释
To lose blood from the body; to draw blood; (of color) to spread into surrounding areas; to drain resources.
✏️ 例句
The wound was deep and continued to bleed despite the bandage.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考引申：bleed dry（榨干）；bleed money（持续亏损）。
🧠 助记
bleed → blood（血）→ 使血流出 → 出血
bleed::/bliːd/:v. 出血:The company was bleeding money due to poor management.
2. catalog /ˈkætəlɒɡ/ n. 目录；产品册 v. 编目
📖 英文解释
A complete list of items, especially one in alphabetical or systematic order; to make such a list.
✏️ 例句
The library has an online catalog of all its books.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常见词。catalog of errors（一连串错误）；product catalog（产品目录）。
🧠 助记
cata（按顺序）+ log（记录）→ 按顺序记录 → 目录；编目
catalog::/ˈkætəlɒɡ/:n. 目录:The museum cataloged thousands of new artifacts this year.
3. erupt /ɪˈrʌpt/ v. 爆发；喷出；突然发生
📖 英文解释
To burst out suddenly and violently; (of a volcano) to eject lava and gases; to break out with strong emotion.
✏️ 例句
The volcano erupted and covered nearby towns with ash.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考引申：erupt in anger（突然愤怒）；erupt into violence（爆发暴力）。
🧠 助记
e（出）+ rupt（破裂）→ 破裂着冲出 → 爆发
erupt::/ɪˈrʌpt/:v. 爆发:Protests erupted across the country following the controversial decision.
4. explosive /ɪkˈspləʊsɪv/ adj. 爆炸性的；暴躁的 n. 炸药
📖 英文解释
Capable of exploding; tending to explode violently; characterized by sudden rapid increase; a substance that can explode.
✏️ 例句
The region experienced explosive population growth in the 1990s.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研双义词。explosive growth（爆炸式增长）；explosive device（爆炸装置）。
🧠 助记
explo（爆炸）+ sive → 具有爆炸性质的 → 爆炸性的；炸药
explosive::/ɪkˈspləʊsɪv/:adj. 爆炸性的:The economy showed explosive growth after the reforms.
5. fluctuate /ˈflʌktʃueɪt/ v. 波动；起伏；涨落
📖 英文解释
To rise and fall irregularly in number, amount, or intensity; to change continually.
✏️ 例句
Oil prices fluctuate daily based on global supply and demand.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研经济类高频词。fluctuate between A and B（在A和B之间波动）。
🧠 助记
fluctu（流动）+ ate → 不停流动变化 → 波动；起伏
fluctuate::/ˈflʌktʃueɪt/:v. 波动:Stock prices fluctuated wildly during the financial crisis.
6. fur /fɜː(r)/ n. 毛皮；皮草；水垢
📖 英文解释
The thick coat of soft hair covering the skin of a mammal; animal skin with the fur still on it, used in clothing.
✏️ 例句
Animal rights activists protested against the sale of fur coats.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研环保类阅读词。fur trade（皮草贸易）；fur coat（皮草大衣）。
🧠 助记
fur → 毛茸茸的 → 动物的毛皮
fur::/fɜː(r)/:n. 毛皮:The ban on fur farming reflects growing animal welfare concerns.
7. gut /ɡʌt/ n. 肠道；直觉 v. 掏空；摧毁
📖 英文解释
The stomach or intestines; the belly; courage or determination; a strong intuitive feeling; to remove the inside of a building or fish.
✏️ 例句
Trust your gut feeling when making difficult decisions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考引申：gut feeling/instinct（直觉）；gut-wrenching（令人痛苦的）。
🧠 助记
gut → 肚子里面 → 肠道；直觉（来自内心深处）
gut::/ɡʌt/:n. 肠道:Her gut instinct told her something was wrong with the deal.
8. heal /hiːl/ v. 治愈；愈合；恢复
📖 英文解释
To make or become sound or healthy again; to repair (a rift or division); to alleviate (distress).
✏️ 例句
It took months for the broken bone to heal completely.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考引申：heal the wound（愈合伤口）；heal a divided society（弥合分裂社会）。
🧠 助记
heal → health（健康）的动词形式 → 使恢复健康 → 治愈
heal::/hiːl/:v. 治愈:Time and reconciliation can heal even the deepest social divisions.
9. irritate /ˈɪrɪteɪt/ v. 激怒；刺激（皮肤等）；使烦躁
📖 英文解释
To make (someone) angry or annoyed; to cause inflammation or other discomfort in (a part of the body).
✏️ 例句
Loud music from the neighbors irritated the residents.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研双义词。irritate the skin（刺激皮肤）；irritate sb.（激怒某人）。
🧠 助记
irri（引起）+ tate → 引起愤怒状态 → 激怒；刺激
irritate::/ˈɪrɪteɪt/:v. 激怒:Constant interruptions irritated the speaker during the lecture.
10. liberty /ˈlɪbəti/ n. 自由；释放；许可
📖 英文解释
The state of being free from oppressive restrictions; freedom from captivity or slavery; freedom to act as one wishes.
✏️ 例句
The constitution guarantees citizens the right to liberty.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频政治词汇。liberty vs freedom（自由，liberty强调从束缚中解放）；civil liberties（公民自由）。
🧠 助记
liber（自由）+ ty → 自由的状态 → 自由；解放
liberty::/ˈlɪbəti/:n. 自由:The revolution was fought in the name of liberty and equality.
11. parcel /ˈpɑːsl/ n. 包裹；一块地 v. 打包；分配
📖 英文解释
A package wrapped in paper or placed in a box; a piece of land; to divide into parts; to wrap up.
✏️ 例句
She received a parcel from her friend living abroad.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常见词。parcel of land（一块土地）；parcel out（分配）。
🧠 助记
parcel → partial（部分）→ 打包好的一部分 → 包裹；一块地
parcel::/ˈpɑːsl/:n. 包裹:The parcel arrived three days after it was shipped.
12. sheep /ʃiːp/ n. 羊；盲从者
📖 英文解释

People who blindly follow trends are sometimes called sheep.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

sheep → 羊群效应 → 跟风的人 → 盲从者
sheep::/ʃiːp/:n. 羊:Not everyone should follow the crowd like sheep.
13. summon /ˈsʌmən/ v. 传唤；召集；鼓起（勇气）
📖 英文解释
To authoritatively call someone to attend or do something; to call people to gather; to gather (a quality) within oneself.
✏️ 例句
The judge summoned the witness to testify in court.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：summon courage/strength（鼓起勇气/力量）；summon a meeting（召集会议）。
🧠 助记

summon::/ˈsʌmən/:v. 传唤:She summoned all her courage to speak in front of the crowd.
14. terminal /ˈtɜːmɪnl/ adj. 末端的；终期的 n. 终端；航站楼
📖 英文解释
At the end or forming the end of something; (of a disease) predicted to lead to death; a departure/arrival building at an airport; a computer input device.
✏️ 例句
The patient was diagnosed with a terminal illness.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频词。terminal disease（绝症）；airport terminal（航站楼）；computer terminal（终端）。
🧠 助记
termin（终点）+ al → 末端的；终点处 → 终期的；终端
terminal::/ˈtɜːmɪnl/:adj. 末端的:Passengers should check in at the international terminal.
15. tremble /ˈtrembl/ v. 颤抖；战栗；摇动
📖 英文解释
To shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety, excitement, or cold; to be in a state of extreme apprehension.
✏️ 例句
Her voice trembled as she delivered the difficult news.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研情感描写词。tremble with fear/cold（因恐惧/寒冷而颤抖）；tremble at the thought（一想起就发抖）。
🧠 助记
trem（颤）+ ble → 颤抖、发抖 → 颤抖；战栗
tremble::/ˈtrembl/:v. 颤抖:The child trembled with fear during the thunderstorm.
16. tribute /ˈtrɪbjuːt/ n. 贡品；赞扬；致敬

An act or gift given to show respect or admiration; a payment made by one state to another; something resulting from or indicating the excellence of someone.
✏️ 例句
The concert was organized as a tribute to the late musician.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：pay tribute to（向…致敬）；a tribute to sb.'s skills（对某人才能的证明）。
🧠 助记
tribute → contribute（贡献）同根 → 贡献出去 → 贡品；致敬
tribute::/ˈtrɪbjuːt/:n. 贡品:The ceremony was held as a tribute to fallen soldiers.

The supreme spirit of evil in many religions; an evil person; a very clever or daring person; used as an intensifier.
✏️ 例句
The devil is in the details, so review every clause carefully.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考短语：the devil is in the details（细节决定成败）；speak of the devil（说曹操曹操到）。
🧠 助记
devil → evil（邪恶）的变体 → 邪恶的存在 → 魔鬼
devil::/ˈdevl/:n. 魔鬼:The devil is in the details when drafting a legal contract.
18. diplomatic /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk/ adj. 外交的；有手腕的；圆滑的
📖 英文解释
Relating to diplomacy or diplomats; skilled in dealing with sensitive matters without causing offence.
✏️ 例句
The two countries agreed to restore diplomatic relations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

diplomatic::/ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk/:adj. 外交的:A diplomatic approach helped resolve the international dispute.
19. displace /dɪsˈpleɪs/ v. 取代；迁移；使离开
📖 英文解释
To take the place of; to force someone to leave their home or country; to move from the usual or correct place.
✏️ 例句
The war displaced millions of refugees from their homeland.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频词。displace workers（取代工人）；displaced persons（流离失所者）。
🧠 助记

displace::/dɪsˈpleɪs/:v. 取代:Automation is likely to displace many manufacturing jobs.
20. echo /ˈekəʊ/ n. 回声；回响 v. 回荡；附和
📖 英文解释

✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考引申：echo sb.'s views（附和某人观点）；echo chamber（信息茧房）。
🧠 助记
echo → 声音弹回来 → 回声；附和别人的话
echo::/ˈekəʊ/:n. 回声:The politician's speech echoed the public's growing anxiety.
21. evoke /ɪˈvəʊk/ v. 唤起；引起；使人想到
📖 英文解释
To bring or recall a feeling, memory, or image to the conscious mind; to invoke a spirit.
✏️ 例句
The old photographs evoked strong memories of her childhood.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研高频词。evoke emotions/memories（唤起情感/记忆）；evoke a response（引发回应）。
🧠 助记
e（出）+ voke（呼唤）→ 呼唤出来 → 唤起；引起
evoke::/ɪˈvəʊk/:v. 唤起:The music evoked a deep sense of nostalgia in the audience.
22. funeral /ˈfjuːnərəl/ n. 葬礼；丧礼
📖 英文解释
A ceremony held shortly after a person's death, usually including burial or cremation.
✏️ 例句
Hundreds of people attended the funeral of the beloved teacher.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研文化类阅读词。funeral service（葬礼仪式）；funeral home（殡仪馆）。
🧠 助记
fun（不，反义）+ eral → 最不fun（悲伤）的场合 → 葬礼
funeral::/ˈfjuːnərəl/:n. 葬礼:The family held a private funeral for their loved one.
23. mammal /ˈmæml/ n. 哺乳动物
📖 英文解释
A warm-blooded vertebrate animal that has hair or fur, gives birth to live young, and feeds them with milk.
✏️ 例句
Whales are the largest mammals on Earth.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研生物类阅读词。mammal vs reptile（哺乳动物 vs 爬行动物）。
🧠 助记
mamm（乳房）+ al → 用乳汁喂养的动物 → 哺乳动物
mammal::/ˈmæml/:n. 哺乳动物:Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals.
24. midst /mɪdst/ n. 中间；中央；当中
📖 英文解释
The middle part or point; surrounded by or in the middle of a situation or group.
✏️ 例句
In the midst of the chaos, she remained remarkably calm.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常见词组：in the midst of（在…中间/当中）；in our midst（在我们当中）。
🧠 助记
mid（中间）+ st → 中间的状态 → 中间；当中
midst::/mɪdst/:n. 中间:She made the difficult decision in the midst of a crisis.
25. offset /ˈɒfset/ v. 抵消；补偿 n. 抵消；偏移
📖 英文解释
To counterbalance or compensate for; something that counterbalances or compensates; a lateral shift.
✏️ 例句
The company planted trees to offset its carbon emissions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研经济、环保高频词。offset carbon emissions（抵消碳排放）；offset losses（弥补损失）。
🧠 助记
off（离开）+ set（设置）→ 偏离原来的设置来补偿 → 抵消
offset::/ˈɒfset/:v. 抵消:Higher revenues helped offset the rising production costs.
