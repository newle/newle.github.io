1. certainty /ˈsɜːrtnti/ n. 确定；必然的事；确信
📖 英文解释
The state of being definitely known or sure; a fact that is definitely true.
✏️ 例句
There is no certainty about when the project will be completed.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
with certainty 确定地；in all certainty 完全肯定；certainty of outcome 结果的确定性
🧠 助记
certain（确定）+ ty = 确定的状态 = 确定性
certainty::/ˈsɜːrtnti/:n. 确定:We cannot predict the outcome with absolute certainty.
2. clause /klɔːz/ n. 条款；从句
📖 英文解释
A particular part of a legal document or agreement; a unit of grammatical organization comprising a subject and a verb.
✏️ 例句
The contract had a clause that allowed either party to cancel within 30 days.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
escape clause 免责条款；subordinate clause 从句；penalty clause 违约条款
🧠 助记
clause 和 close（关闭）相近，条款关闭了某些可能性
clause::/klɔːz/:n. 条款:Read each clause of the agreement carefully before signing.
3. curse /kɜːrs/ n./v. 诅咒；咒骂；祸根
📖 英文解释
A solemn utterance intended to invoke a supernatural power to inflict harm; to invoke harm upon; to swear.
✏️ 例句
He shouted curses at the car that had nearly hit him.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
curse of 的祸根；under a curse 被诅咒；curse sb 咒骂某人
🧠 助记
curse 发音像'可死'，诅咒别人'可以去死'
curse::/kɜːrs/:n./v. 诅咒:Poverty was seen as both a curse and a challenge to be overcome.
4. disguise /dɪsˈɡaɪz/ v./n. 伪装；掩盖；假装
📖 英文解释
To give a different appearance to conceal the true identity; a means of altering one's appearance.
✏️ 例句
The spy wore a disguise to avoid being recognised.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in disguise 伪装的；blessing in disguise 因祸得福；disguise oneself as 伪装成
🧠 助记
dis（去除）+ guise（外貌）= 去除真实外貌 = 伪装
disguise::/dɪsˈɡaɪz/:v./n. 伪装:She disguised her disappointment with a polite smile.
5. doorway /ˈdɔːrweɪ/ n. 门口；门道；入口
📖 英文解释
An opening in a wall that is filled by a door; a means of access to something.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
stand in the doorway 站在门口；doorway to success 成功之门
🧠 助记
door（门）+ way（路）= 门的路 = 门口
doorway::/ˈdɔːrweɪ/:n. 门口:A figure appeared in the doorway, blocking the light.
6. faithful /ˈfeɪθfl/ adj. 忠实的；守信的；忠贞的
📖 英文解释

🧠 助记
faith（信念）+ ful（充满）= 充满信念 = 忠实的
faithful::/ˈfeɪθfl/:adj. 忠实的:She remained faithful to her principles despite external pressure.
7. ghost /ɡəʊst/ n. 鬼魂；幽灵；痕迹
📖 英文解释

ghost::/ɡəʊst/:n. 鬼魂:There was not a ghost of a chance that they would win.
8. greedy /ˈɡriːdi/ adj. 贪婪的；贪吃的；渴望的

greedy::/ˈɡriːdi/:adj. 贪婪的:The greedy corporation ignored environmental regulations in pursuit of profit.
9. illustration /ˌɪləˈstreɪʃn/ n. 插图；例证；说明

illustration::/ˌɪləˈstreɪʃn/:n. 插图:The report included charts and illustrations to support its findings.
10. imaginary /ɪˈmædʒɪnəri/ adj. 虚构的；想象的；幻想的

imaginary::/ɪˈmædʒɪnəri/:adj. 虚构的:The story takes place in an imaginary land where animals can talk.
11. marginal /ˈmɑːrdʒɪnl/ adj. 微小的；边际的；边缘的
📖 英文解释
Relating to a margin; of minor importance; barely adequate; relating to goods produced or sold at the margin.

marginal::/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnl/:adj. 微小的:The new policy made only a marginal impact on unemployment rates.
12. obsession /əbˈseʃn/ n. 困扰；强迫观念；痴迷
📖 英文解释
The state of being obsessed with someone or something; an idea or thought that continually preoccupies or intrudes on a person's mind.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
obsession with 对……的痴迷；become an obsession 成为一种执念；obsess v.
🧠 助记
ob + session（会话），被同一件事无限循环会话 = 痴迷
obsession::/əbˈseʃn/:n. 困扰:Her obsession with cleanliness started to affect her daily life.

offensive::/əˈfensɪv/:adj./n. 冒犯的:The army launched a major offensive along the northern front.

petroleum::/pəˈtrəʊliəm/:n. 石油:The discovery of petroleum transformed the nation's economy.
