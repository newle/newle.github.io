1. dome /dəʊm/ n. 圆屋顶；穹顶；半球形物
📖 英文解释
A rounded roof or ceiling that resembles the upper half of a sphere; any similar rounded shape.
✏️ 例句
The cathedral's magnificent dome dominates the city skyline.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：dome-shaped（圆顶状的）；glass dome（玻璃穹顶）；dome of the sky（苍穹）。考研建筑/文化类常见。
🧠 助记
dome 形状就像字母 D 横放——一个半圆形的穹顶（dome）。联想：阴暗的 dome 像个大锅扣着。
dome::/dəʊm/:n. 圆屋顶:The new sports arena features an impressive retractable dome roof.
2. lottery /ˈlɒtəri/ n. 彩票；摸彩；碰运气的事
📖 英文解释
A system of raising money by selling tickets and giving prizes to those with winning numbers; something that depends entirely on luck.
✏️ 例句
He spent years playing the lottery without winning a single prize.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：lottery ticket（彩票）；win the lottery（中彩票）；national lottery（国家彩票）；life is a lottery（人生如彩票，靠运气）。
🧠 助记
lot（签，运气）+ tery → 靠运气抽签的游戏 → 彩票。lot 本义是'签'，lottery 就是'抽签游戏'。
lottery::/ˈlɒtəri/:n. 彩票:Getting a place at the popular school felt like winning the lottery.
3. monster /ˈmɒnstə(r)/ n. 怪物；恶人；庞然大物 adj. 巨大的
📖 英文解释
An imaginary creature that is large, ugly, and frightening; a very cruel or wicked person; something unusually large.
✏️ 例句
The corporation became a monster that consumed smaller rivals without mercy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：sea monster（海怪）；monster truck（怪兽卡车）；green-eyed monster（嫉妒）；monster of greed（贪婪之魔）。
🧠 助记
monster 谐音'蒙斯特'—— 蒙着脸的斯特（monster）是个怪物（monster）。
monster::/ˈmɒnstə(r)/:n. 怪物:The green-eyed monster of jealousy destroyed their friendship completely.
4. constituent /kənˈstɪtjuənt/ n. 成分；组成部分；选民 adj. 组成的
📖 英文解释
A part of something that makes up the whole; a person who lives in a particular area and can vote for its representative.
✏️ 例句
The senator held regular meetings to address the concerns of her constituents.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：constituent parts（组成部分）；represent one's constituents（代表选民）；key constituent（关键成分）。近义词：component, element。
🧠 助记
constitute（组成，构成）+ nt → 组成成分 → 成分。constituent = 构成整体的各个组成部分。
constituent::/kənˈstɪtjuənt/:n. 成分:Water is a key constituent of all known living organisms.
5. eighteen /ˌeɪˈtiːn/ num. 十八
📖 英文解释
The number 18; one more than seventeen.
✏️ 例句
In most countries, eighteen is the minimum age for voting.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：eighteen years old（18岁）；the eighteenth century（十八世纪）；eighteen holes（高尔夫18洞）。考研中主要出现在语境数字辨认。
🧠 助记
eight（8）+ een（teen 十几）→ 8 + 10 = 18 → 十八。teens = 13-19岁青少年。
eighteen::/ˌeɪˈtiːn/:num. 十八:She turned eighteen and could finally vote in the national election.
6. emigrate /ˈemɪɡreɪt/ v. 移居海外；移民（出境）
📖 英文解释
To leave your own country in order to go and live permanently in another country.
✏️ 例句
Many families emigrated to Australia during the economic recession.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：emigrate to（移民到…）；emigrate from（从…移民出）；emigrant（移民，名词）；emigration（移民出境）。注意：emigrate（移出）vs immigrate（移入）。
🧠 助记
e（出去）+ migrate（迁移）→ 迁移出去 → 移居海外。exit + migrate = emigrate（离开本国）。
emigrate::/ˈemɪɡreɪt/:v. 移居海外:Thousands emigrated to North America in search of better economic opportunities.
7. hatred /ˈheɪtrɪd/ n. 憎恨；仇恨；强烈厌恶
📖 英文解释
An extremely strong feeling of dislike or hostility toward someone or something.
✏️ 例句
His speech was filled with hatred and anger toward political opponents.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：deep/intense hatred（深切的仇恨）；hatred of/for（对…的仇恨）；racial/ethnic hatred（种族仇恨）；hate speech（仇恨言论）。
🧠 助记
hate（恨）+ red（热）→ 热烈的恨意 → 仇恨。hatred = 被hate的热度积累成深切仇恨。
hatred::/ˈheɪtrɪd/:n. 憎恨:The leaders called on citizens to overcome hatred and embrace reconciliation.
8. indicative /ɪnˈdɪkətɪv/ adj. 表明的；象征的；陈述的 n. 陈述语气
📖 英文解释
Showing or suggesting something; (grammar) the form of a verb used for making statements; serving as a sign or symbol.
✏️ 例句
The falling stock prices are indicative of broader economic uncertainty.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：be indicative of（表明；是…的象征）；indicative mood（陈述语气）；indicative results（指示性结果）。考研语言/学术类重要词。
🧠 助记
indicat（indicate 指示）+ ive → 具有指示意义的 → 表明的。indicate → indicative（形容词形式）。
indicative::/ɪnˈdɪkətɪv/:adj. 表明的:High unemployment is often indicative of deeper structural problems in an economy.
9. lounge /laʊndʒ/ n. 休息室；休闲区；大厅 v. 懒散地躺
📖 英文解释
A room in a hotel, airport, or other building where people can sit comfortably and relax; to sit or lie in a relaxed way.
✏️ 例句
Passengers waited in the airport lounge for their delayed flight.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：airport/VIP lounge（机场/贵宾休息室）；lounge around（无所事事地混）；cocktail lounge（鸡尾酒吧）；lounge music（慵懒的背景音乐）。
🧠 助记
lounge 谐音'朗奇'—— 在朗奇（lounge）休息室懒洋洋地躺（lounge）着。
lounge::/laʊndʒ/:n. 休息室:Business class passengers have access to the exclusive airport lounge.
10. ore /ɔː(r)/ n. 矿石；矿砂
📖 英文解释
Rock or earth from which metal can be extracted; naturally occurring minerals containing valuable substances.
✏️ 例句
Iron ore is transported by train from the mines to the steel plant.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：iron ore（铁矿石）；copper/gold ore（铜/金矿石）；ore deposit（矿藏）；ore extraction（矿石开采）。考研地理/工业类常见。
🧠 助记
ore 谐音'哦'—— '哦，这里有矿石（ore）！'矿工发现了宝贵的矿石。
ore::/ɔː(r)/:n. 矿石:Australia is one of the world's largest exporters of iron ore.
11. pastime /ˈpɑːstaɪm/ n. 消遣；娱乐活动；嗜好
📖 英文解释
An activity that you do for enjoyment when you are not working; a hobby.
✏️ 例句
Reading was her favourite pastime during long winter evenings.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：favourite pastime（最喜欢的消遣）；popular pastime（流行的消遣活动）；national pastime（国民娱乐，如美国棒球）。
🧠 助记
pass（消磨）+ time（时间）→ 消磨时间的活动 → 消遣。pastime = pass + time（打发时间）。
pastime::/ˈpɑːstaɪm/:n. 消遣:Gardening became his favourite pastime after he retired from work.
12. plead /pliːd/ v. 恳求；辩护；以…为借口
📖 英文解释
To ask someone for something in an urgent and emotional way; to present a case in court; to offer as an excuse.
✏️ 例句
The defendant pleaded not guilty to all charges brought against him.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：plead guilty/not guilty（认罪/不认罪）；plead for mercy（恳求宽容）；plead with sb（恳求某人）；plead ignorance（以不知情为借口）。
🧠 助记
plead 谐音'普里德'—— '普里德'跪地恳求（plead），苦苦请求宽恕。
plead::/pliːd/:v. 恳求:She pleaded with the judge for leniency, citing her difficult circumstances.
13. recur /rɪˈkɜː(r)/ v. 复发；再发生；重新出现
📖 英文解释
To happen again or to happen many times; to return to one's mind repeatedly.
✏️ 例句
If the problem recurs, please contact the maintenance team immediately.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：recur frequently（频繁复发）；recurring theme/problem（反复出现的主题/问题）；recurrence（复发，名词）；recurring decimal（循环小数）。
🧠 助记
re（再次）+ cur（发生，occur 发生）→ 再次发生 → 复发。occur → recur：加 re- 表示再次发生。
recur::/rɪˈkɜː(r)/:v. 复发:The knee injury continued to recur despite extensive treatment and rest.
14. soak /səʊk/ v./n. 浸泡；湿透；吸收
📖 英文解释
To become wet all the way through; to leave something in liquid for a time; to absorb liquid.
✏️ 例句
Soak the beans overnight before cooking to reduce preparation time.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：soak in/up（吸收；尽情体验）；soaked to the skin（浑身湿透）；soak away（渗走）；soak the rich（向富人征重税）。
🧠 助记
soak 发音像'搜克'—— 把东西'搜'（浸入）进克（液体）里面 → 浸泡。
soak::/səʊk/:v./n. 浸泡:Tourists were encouraged to soak up the local culture during their visit.
15. sunrise /ˈsʌnraɪz/ n. 日出；黎明；朝霞
📖 英文解释
The time in the morning when the sun first appears above the horizon; the colors of the sky at this time.
✏️ 例句
They woke up early to watch the magnificent sunrise over the mountains.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：at sunrise（日出时）；sunrise industry（新兴产业）；from sunrise to sunset（从早到晚）。对比 sunset（日落）。
🧠 助记
sun（太阳）+ rise（升起）→ 太阳升起 → 日出。sunrise industry = 像旭日一样新兴的产业。
sunrise::/ˈsʌnraɪz/:n. 日出:Renewable energy is considered a sunrise industry with enormous growth potential.
16. susceptible /səˈseptɪbl/ adj. 易受影响的；敏感的；脆弱的
📖 英文解释
Easily influenced or affected by something; likely to be hurt or affected by a disease or problem.
✏️ 例句
Children are more susceptible to respiratory infections during cold weather.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：susceptible to disease/infection（易患病/感染）；susceptible to influence/persuasion（易受影响/劝说）；highly susceptible（高度易感）。
🧠 助记
sus（在下面）+ cept（接受）+ ible → 容易在下面接受影响的 → 易受影响的。suspect → susceptible。
susceptible::/səˈseptɪbl/:adj. 易受影响的:Elderly individuals are particularly susceptible to complications from influenza.
17. agony /ˈæɡəni/ n. 极度痛苦；苦恼；煎熬
📖 英文解释
Extreme physical or mental pain or suffering; great anguish.

He was in agony for hours after breaking his leg in the accident.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：in agony（极度痛苦中）；sheer agony（极度的痛苦）；agony aunt（专栏答问者）；agony column（读者来信解答专栏）；prolong the agony（延长痛苦）。
🧠 助记
agony 谐音'啊够你'—— 痛到喊'啊，够了！'，就是极度痛苦（agony）。
agony::/ˈæɡəni/:n. 极度痛苦:The long wait for the verdict was sheer agony for the defendant's family.

Very strange and unusual; hard to understand or explain because of its strangeness.
✏️ 例句
The artist was known for his bizarre but thought-provoking installations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：bizarre behaviour/appearance（怪异行为/外表）；bizarre coincidence（奇异的巧合）；bizarre situation（离奇情况）。近义词：strange, peculiar, eccentric。
🧠 助记
bizarre 谐音'比扎了'—— 比刺扎了还奇怪（bizarre），真是太离奇了！
bizarre::/bɪˈzɑː(r)/:adj. 奇异的:The series of bizarre coincidences made the whole story seem unbelievable.
19. conversely /ˈkɒnvɜːsli/ adv. 相反地；反过来说
📖 英文解释
Used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or reverses what has just been said.
✏️ 例句
Exercise improves health; conversely, a sedentary lifestyle increases health risks.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：conversely，…（相反地，…）；used at the start of a sentence to introduce a contrasting idea。考研写作中重要的逻辑连接词。
🧠 助记
converse（相反的对话）+ ly → 说相反的话 → 相反地。converse（交谈；对立的）+ -ly = conversely（相反地）。
conversely::/ˈkɒnvɜːsli/:adv. 相反地:High inflation erodes savings; conversely, deflation can reduce economic activity.
20. dedicate /ˈdedɪkeɪt/ v. 致力于；献身于；题献
📖 英文解释
To devote time, effort, or oneself to something; to say that a book, piece of music, etc. is written in honor of someone.
✏️ 例句
She dedicated her life to improving education in underprivileged communities.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：dedicate oneself to（致力于）；dedicated to（专注的；献给的）；dedication（奉献；题词，名词）；a dedicated team（专注的团队）。
🧠 助记
dedi（给予）+ cate（做）→ 把一切给予某事 → 致力于。dedicate to = give everything to a cause。
dedicate::/ˈdedɪkeɪt/:v. 致力于:The researcher dedicated twenty years to studying the effects of climate change.
21. extinguish /ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ/ v. 熄灭；扑灭；消灭；压制
📖 英文解释
To stop a fire or light from burning or shining; to put an end to a feeling or idea.
✏️ 例句
Firefighters worked through the night to extinguish the warehouse blaze.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：extinguish a fire/flame（扑灭火焰）；extinguish hope/memory（扑灭希望/抹去记忆）；fire extinguisher（灭火器）。
🧠 助记
ex（出）+ stingu（刺/刺入熄灭）→ 把火刺穿使其熄灭 → 熄灭。extinguish → fire extinguisher（灭火器）。
extinguish::/ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ/:v. 熄灭:Nothing could extinguish her burning desire to succeed despite all obstacles.

fetch::/fetʃ/:v. 取来:The rare painting fetched a record price of five million dollars at auction.

24. incur /ɪnˈkɜː(r)/ v. 招致；引起；遭受
📖 英文解释
To experience something unpleasant as a result of your actions; to bring something undesirable on yourself.
✏️ 例句
The company incurred heavy losses as a result of the failed investment strategy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：incur costs/expenses（承担费用）；incur losses/debts（遭受亏损/产生债务）；incur sb's anger/displeasure（招致某人愤怒/不悦）。
🧠 助记
in（进入）+ cur（奔跑/发生）→ 跑进麻烦里 → 招致（麻烦/损失）。cur = run/occur，进入负面情况。
incur::/ɪnˈkɜː(r)/:v. 招致:Travel expenses incurred during the trip will be reimbursed by the company.
25. invalid /ɪnˈvælɪd/ (adj.) / ˈɪnvəlɪd/ (n.) adj. 无效的；站不住脚的 n. 病弱者；残疾人
📖 英文解释
Not legally or officially acceptable; not based on correct reasoning; a person who is weak or disabled due to illness or injury.
✏️ 例句
The contract was declared invalid because it lacked the required signatures.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：invalid argument/claim（无效论点）；invalid passport（过期护照）；declare invalid（宣布无效）；invalid ticket（无效票）。注意双重读音。
🧠 助记
in（否定）+ valid（有效的）→ 无效的。invalid passport = 无效护照。注意两种读音对应不同词义。
invalid::/ɪnˈvælɪd/ (adj.) / ˈɪnvəlɪd/ (n.):adj. 无效的:The court declared the will invalid due to procedural errors in its signing.
