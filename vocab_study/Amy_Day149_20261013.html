1. loosen /ˈluːsn/ v. 松开；放松；解除（限制）
📖 英文解释
To make something less tight or strict; to become less tight or firm; to relax a restriction.
✏️ 例句
The government agreed to loosen restrictions on international travel.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：loosen restrictions/controls（放宽限制/管制）；loosen up（放松；热身）；loosen a tie/knot（解开领带/结）；loosen one's grip（松手）。
🧠 助记
loose（松的）+ n → 使变松 → 松开。loose → loosen，就像 tight → tighten（使变紧）。
loosen::/ˈluːsn/:v. 松开:The central bank decided to loosen monetary policy to stimulate economic growth.
2. orchestra /ˈɔːkɪstrə/ n. 管弦乐队；乐队席
📖 英文解释
A large group of musicians playing different instruments together, usually including strings, woodwind, brass, and percussion.
✏️ 例句
The symphony orchestra performed to a sold-out audience at the concert hall.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：symphony orchestra（交响乐团）；conduct an orchestra（指挥乐团）；orchestra pit（乐池）；chamber orchestra（室内乐团）。
🧠 助记
orchestra 谐音'哦客斯追啊'—— 乐队（orchestra）演奏让观众'哦'地叫出声追随。
orchestra::/ˈɔːkɪstrə/:n. 管弦乐队:The conductor raised his baton and the orchestra began to play.
3. pottery /ˈpɒtəri/ n. 陶器；制陶术；陶器厂
📖 英文解释
Objects made from clay that have been hardened by heating; the craft of making such objects; a place where pottery is made.
✏️ 例句
She took up pottery as a hobby to help reduce stress after work.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：pottery class（陶艺课）；ancient pottery（古代陶器）；pottery wheel（陶轮）；pottery making（制陶）。考研文化/历史类可见。
🧠 助记
pot（罐子）+ tery → 制作罐子的技艺/场所 → 陶器；制陶。pot = 陶罐，pottery = 制陶工艺。
pottery::/ˈpɒtəri/:n. 陶器:The museum's pottery collection spans five thousand years of human history.
4. quantitative /ˈkwɒntɪtətɪv/ adj. 定量的；数量的；定量研究的
📖 英文解释
Relating to or measured by quantity; expressed or measuring in numbers; relating to quantitative research methods.
✏️ 例句
The study used quantitative methods to analyse large datasets from 10,000 participants.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：quantitative research/analysis（定量研究/分析）；quantitative easing（量化宽松）；quantitative data（定量数据）。对比 qualitative（定性的）。
🧠 助记
quantity（数量）+ ative → 关于数量的 → 定量的。quantitative vs qualitative：量（quantity）vs 质（quality）。
quantitative::/ˈkwɒntɪtətɪv/:adj. 定量的:Quantitative easing was adopted by central banks to stimulate sluggish economies.
5. spectacle /ˈspektəkl/ n. 壮观的景象；奇观；眼镜（复数）
📖 英文解释
A visually striking performance or display; an impressive or unusual sight; (plural) glasses worn to correct vision.
✏️ 例句
The opening ceremony of the Olympics was a spectacular spectacle viewed by millions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：make a spectacle of oneself（出丑）；a spectacle of natural beauty（壮丽的自然景观）；spectacular（壮观的，形容词）。
🧠 助记
spect（看，如 spectator）+ acle → 值得观看的事物 → 壮观景象。inspect/spectator/spectacle 同词根。
spectacle::/ˈspektəkl/:n. 壮观的景象:The northern lights put on a breathtaking spectacle in the Arctic sky.

7. viewpoint /ˈvjuːpɔɪnt/ n. 观点；立场；视角
📖 英文解释
A place from which something can be seen; a particular attitude or way of considering a matter.
✏️ 例句
From an economic viewpoint, the policy will create more jobs and growth.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：from a…viewpoint（从…角度来看）；different viewpoints（不同观点）；share/hold a viewpoint（持有某观点）。近义词：perspective, standpoint, point of view。
🧠 助记
view（视线/观点）+ point（点）→ 观察的点 → 观点；视角。view + point = 站在某点看问题的角度。
viewpoint::/ˈvjuːpɔɪnt/:n. 观点:From an environmental viewpoint, the proposed development raises serious concerns.
8. alternate /ˈɔːltəneɪt/ (v.) / ˈɔːltənət/ (adj.) v. 交替；轮流 adj. 交替的；另一个的
📖 英文解释
To keep changing between two states or actions; to do something in turns; happening by turns.
✏️ 例句
The seasons alternate between wet and dry in this tropical region.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：alternate between（在…之间交替）；on alternate days（隔日）；alternate route（备用路线）；alternative（替代的，近义词）。
🧠 助记
alter（改变）+ nate → 不断改变的状态 → 交替。alter = change，alternate = change back and forth。
alternate::/ˈɔːltəneɪt/ (v.) / ˈɔːltənət/ (adj.):v. 交替:The committee members take turns chairing meetings on alternate months.

attendant::/əˈtendənt/:n. 服务员:The museum attendant guided visitors through the new exhibition.

13. converge /kənˈvɜːdʒ/ v. 汇聚；趋同；集中

converge::/kənˈvɜːdʒ/:v. 汇聚:Several major highways converge at this junction on the outskirts of the city.

18. explode /ɪkˈspləʊd/ v. 爆炸；激增；驳倒（理论）
📖 英文解释
To burst suddenly with great force; to increase very quickly; to show that a theory or belief is false.
✏️ 例句
The population of the city exploded from one million to five million in twenty years.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：explode a myth/theory（推翻神话/理论）；explode with anger（勃然大怒）；population explodes（人口激增）；explosion（爆炸/激增，名词）。
🧠 助记
ex（向外）+ plode（拍手/爆破）→ 向外爆破 → 爆炸。implode（向内爆炸）= im + plode。
explode::/ɪkˈspləʊd/:v. 爆炸:The study effectively exploded the myth that multitasking increases productivity.

handwriting::/ˈhændraɪtɪŋ/:n. 笔迹:Forensic experts used handwriting analysis to identify the anonymous letter's author.

invasion::/ɪnˈveɪʒn/:n. 入侵:The unauthorized collection of personal data was condemned as an invasion of privacy.
