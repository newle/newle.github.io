1. cripple /ˈkrɪpl/ n. 跛子 v. 使残废；严重削弱
📖 英文解释
a person unable to walk properly; to cause severe damage or weakness to something
✏️ 例句
The trade sanctions crippled the country's economy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
cripple the economy（削弱经济）；crippled by debt（被债务拖垮）；考研经济/社会类高频词
🧠 助记
crip（跛行 creep）+ ple → 爬行的人 → 跛子；引申使残废
cripple::/ˈkrɪpl/:n. 跛子 v. 使残废:Heavy fines crippled the small business.
2. mandate /ˈmændeɪt/ n. 授权；任务；命令 v. 授权；要求
📖 英文解释
an official order or commission to do something; authority given to a person or organization
✏️ 例句
The government received a clear mandate from voters to reform the tax system.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
have a mandate to do（有授权做…）；mandate that（规定…）；考研政治/法律类高频词
🧠 助记
man（人）+ date（日期）→ 人在某日期被授予权力 → 授权
mandate::/ˈmændeɪt/:n. 授权:The new president claimed a mandate for change.
3. microscope /ˈmaɪkrəskəʊp/ n. 显微镜
📖 英文解释
an optical instrument used for examining objects too small to be seen by the naked eye
✏️ 例句
Under the microscope, scientists observed the cell dividing.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
under the microscope（在显微镜下；受到仔细审查）；microscopic（微小的）；考研科学类
🧠 助记
micro（微小）+ scope（看）→ 看微小东西的仪器 → 显微镜
microscope::/ˈmaɪkrəskəʊp/:n. 显微镜:Every aspect of his conduct was placed under the microscope.
4. reign /reɪn/ v./n. 统治；支配；在位
📖 英文解释
to rule as a king or queen; the period during which a sovereign rules
✏️ 例句
Queen Victoria reigned for over 63 years.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
reign over（统治）；reign of terror（恐怖统治）；考研历史类文章，注意与 rain/rein 区分
🧠 助记
reign 谐音[瑞恩] → 瑞恩王国的统治者 → 统治
reign::/reɪn/:v./n. 统治:During his reign, the country enjoyed relative peace.
5. verdict /ˈvɜːdɪkt/ n. 裁决；判决；结论
📖 英文解释
a decision on an issue of fact in a civil or criminal case; a judgment or opinion
✏️ 例句
The jury returned a verdict of not guilty after three days of deliberation.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
reach/return a verdict（作出裁决）；verdict of guilty/not guilty（有罪/无罪裁决）；考研法律类
🧠 助记
ver（真实 verify）+ dict（说）→ 说出真实结论 → 裁决
verdict::/ˈvɜːdɪkt/:n. 裁决:The verdict was unanimous: guilty on all charges.
6. augment /ɔːɡˈment/ v. 增加；增大；扩充
📖 英文解释
to make something greater by adding to it; to increase in size, extent, or quantity
✏️ 例句
She took a part-time job to augment her income.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
augment income/resources（增加收入/资源）；augmented reality（增强现实）；考研经济/科技类
🧠 助记
aug（August 八月丰收）+ ment → 丰收增产 → 增加
augment::/ɔːɡˈment/:v. 增加:The company augmented its workforce to meet rising demand.
7. carve /kɑːv/ v. 雕刻；切割；开辟
📖 英文解释
to cut an object or design from a hard material; to make a career or path by determined effort
✏️ 例句
He carved his name into the old wooden desk.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
carve out（开辟；争得）；carve up（瓜分）；carve a career（开创事业）；考研中 carve out 高频
🧠 助记
carve 谐音[卡V] → 刻出V形 → 雕刻
carve::/kɑːv/:v. 雕刻:She carved out a successful career in finance.
8. compile /kəmˈpaɪl/ v. 编译；汇编；编辑
📖 英文解释
to produce a book or report by assembling material from other sources; to convert code into machine language
✏️ 例句
Researchers compiled data from over 200 studies for the report.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
compile a report/list（编制报告/列表）；compile information（汇编信息）；考研学术写作类
🧠 助记
com（共同）+ pile（堆积）→ 把材料堆积在一起 → 汇编
compile::/kəmˈpaɪl/:v. 编译:The committee compiled a comprehensive report on poverty.
9. foremost /ˈfɔːməʊst/ adj. 最重要的；最著名的 adv. 首先
📖 英文解释
most prominent in rank, importance, or position; above all others
✏️ 例句
She is one of the foremost experts in renewable energy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
first and foremost（首先，最重要的）；foremost in（在…方面最重要）；考研写作常用表达
🧠 助记
fore（前面）+ most（最）→ 排在最前面的 → 最重要的
foremost::/ˈfɔːməʊst/:adj. 最重要的:First and foremost, we must ensure public safety.
10. gasp /ɡɑːsp/ v./n. 喘气；喘息；倒抽气
📖 英文解释
to inhale sharply with the mouth open, from shock, surprise, or difficulty breathing
✏️ 例句
The audience gasped in astonishment when the magician disappeared.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
gasp for air/breath（喘气）；gasp in/with shock（震惊地倒抽一口气）；考研描写震惊场景
🧠 助记
gasp 谐音[哈斯普] → 哈地倒抽一口气 → 喘息
gasp::/ɡɑːsp/:v./n. 喘气:She gasped when she saw the price tag.
11. hygiene /ˈhaɪdʒiːn/ n. 卫生；卫生习惯
📖 英文解释
conditions or practices conducive to maintaining health and preventing disease, especially through cleanliness
✏️ 例句
Good personal hygiene is essential in preventing the spread of disease.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
personal/food/dental hygiene（个人/食品/牙齿卫生）；hygiene standards（卫生标准）；考研医疗类
🧠 助记
hy（high 高）+ giene（gene 基因）→ 高标准基因式洁净 → 卫生
hygiene::/ˈhaɪdʒiːn/:n. 卫生:Strict hygiene standards are enforced in the kitchen.
12. negligible /ˈneɡlɪdʒɪbl/ adj. 微不足道的；可忽略不计的
📖 英文解释
so small or unimportant as to be not worth considering
✏️ 例句
The difference in cost between the two options is negligible.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
negligible effect/impact/amount（微小的影响/数量）；almost negligible（几乎可以忽略）；考研数量比较
🧠 助记
negligible = neglect（忽略）+ ible → 可以忽略的 → 微不足道的
negligible::/ˈneɡlɪdʒɪbl/:adj. 微不足道的:The risk of side effects is negligible in most patients.

penny::/ˈpeni/:n. 便士:They hadn't a penny to spare after paying the rent.

16. stationary /ˈsteɪʃənri/ adj. 静止的；不动的；稳定的

stationary::/ˈsteɪʃənri/:adj. 静止的:Inflation has been stationary for the past two quarters.

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

tentative::/ˈtentətɪv/:adj. 暂时的:Tentative peace talks began between the two sides.

uphold::/ˌʌpˈhəʊld/:v. 维护:The appeal court upheld the original verdict.

agreeable::/əˈɡriːəbl/:adj. 令人愉快的:If you are agreeable, we can proceed with the plan.

anecdote::/ˈænɪkdəʊt/:n. 轶事:He shared a few anecdotes from his years working abroad.

flatter::/ˈflætə(r)/:v. 奉承:She was flattered by his attention but remained cautious.

✏️ 例句

knot::/nɒt/:n. 结:The negotiations hit a knot over trade tariffs.
