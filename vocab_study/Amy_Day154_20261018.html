1. naive /naɪˈiːv/ adj. 天真的；单纯的；缺乏经验的
📖 英文解释
showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment; natural and unaffected; innocent
✏️ 例句
It would be naive to assume that the process will be easy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
naive assumption/belief（天真的假设/信念）；politically naive（政治上幼稚的）；考研批评性分析
🧠 助记
naive 谐音[乃衣弗] → 乃是一个天真如衣服般纯白的人 → 天真的
naive::/naɪˈiːv/:adj. 天真的:It is naive to think technology alone can solve poverty.
2. outset /ˈaʊtset/ n. 开始；开端
📖 英文解释
the beginning of something
✏️ 例句
Problems were apparent from the outset of the project.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
from the outset（从一开始）；at the outset（在开始时）；考研写作中常用 from the outset 表示从一开始
🧠 助记
out（出）+ set（设置/开始）→ 开始向外出发 → 开始
outset::/ˈaʊtset/:n. 开始:From the outset, the plan faced strong opposition.
3. sacred /ˈseɪkrɪd/ adj. 神圣的；宗教的；庄严的
📖 英文解释
connected with God or a god or dedicated to a religious purpose; regarded with great respect
✏️ 例句
The river is sacred to the indigenous people of the region.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
sacred duty/right（神圣的职责/权利）；hold sth sacred（视某物为神圣）；考研宗教/文化类
🧠 助记
sac（sacrifice 牺牲）+ red（红色/血）→ 用鲜血献祭的 → 神圣的
sacred::/ˈseɪkrɪd/:adj. 神圣的:Freedom of speech is a sacred right in a democracy.
4. sovereign /ˈsɒvrɪn/ n. 君主 adj. 至高无上的；独立的
📖 英文解释
a supreme ruler, especially a monarch; possessing supreme or ultimate power; fully independent
✏️ 例句
Every sovereign nation has the right to determine its own policies.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
sovereign state/nation（主权国家）；sovereign power（最高权力）；考研政治/国际关系类
🧠 助记
sover（super 超级）+ eign（reign 统治）→ 超级统治者 → 君主/至高无上的
sovereign::/ˈsɒvrɪn/:n. 君主 adj. 至高无上的:China is a sovereign state that makes its own decisions.
5. turbulent /ˈtɜːbjʊlənt/ adj. 动荡的；狂暴的；不稳定的
📖 英文解释
moving in a disorderly and fast-moving manner; characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion
✏️ 例句
The country went through a turbulent period of political upheaval.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
turbulent times/period（动荡时期）；turbulent waters（湍流）；turbulent relationship（动荡关系）；考研政治/历史类
🧠 助记
turb（turbine 涡轮）+ ulent → 像涡轮一样混乱旋转 → 动荡的
turbulent::/ˈtɜːbjʊlənt/:adj. 动荡的:Investors are nervous during turbulent market conditions.
6. vicious /ˈvɪʃəs/ adj. 恶毒的；凶猛的；恶性的
📖 英文解释
deliberately cruel or violent; having the nature of vice; severe or painful
✏️ 例句
A vicious cycle of poverty and crime is hard to break.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
vicious circle/cycle（恶性循环）；vicious attack（恶意攻击）；vicious rumour（恶意谣言）；考研社会类
🧠 助记
vici（vice 恶习）+ ous → 充满恶习的 → 恶毒的
vicious::/ˈvɪʃəs/:adj. 恶毒的:Society is trapped in a vicious cycle of inequality.
7. ample /ˈæmpl/ adj. 充足的；宽裕的；足够的
📖 英文解释
enough or more than enough; plentiful; large and accommodating
✏️ 例句
There is ample evidence to support this conclusion.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
ample time/room/evidence（充足的时间/空间/证据）；more than ample（绰绰有余）；考研学术写作常用
🧠 助记
ample 谐音[安普了] → 安安稳稳很充足 → 充足的
ample::/ˈæmpl/:adj. 充足的:The report provides ample proof of the system's failure.
8. atom /ˈætəm/ n. 原子；微量；极小量
📖 英文解释
the smallest particle of a chemical element that can exist; an extremely small amount
✏️ 例句
Nuclear fission splits the atom, releasing enormous energy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
atom bomb（原子弹）；split the atom（裂变原子）；not an atom of（一点…都没有）；考研科学类
🧠 助记
a（一个）+ tom（音：拆）→ 一个最小可拆分的粒子 → 原子
atom::/ˈætəm/:n. 原子:There is not an atom of truth in what he said.
9. banquet /ˈbæŋkwɪt/ n. 宴会；盛宴 v. 设宴款待
📖 英文解释
an elaborate and formal meal for many people, often followed by speeches
✏️ 例句
A state banquet was held in honour of the visiting president.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
state banquet（国宴）；banquet hall（宴会厅）；attend a banquet（出席宴会）；考研外交/社交类
🧠 助记
ban（禁止）+ quet → 禁止轻松，要正式的宴会 → 宴会
banquet::/ˈbæŋkwɪt/:n. 宴会:The company hosted a lavish banquet for its investors.
10. bleak /bliːk/ adj. 萧瑟的；无希望的；阴郁的
📖 英文解释
cold, exposed, and lacking vegetation; not encouraging; dreary
✏️ 例句
The economic outlook for the region remains bleak.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
bleak future/outlook/prospect（黯淡的未来/前景）；bleak landscape（萧索的景色）；考研经济/环境类
🧠 助记
bleak 谐音[不力克] → 无力克服困境 → 无希望的
bleak::/bliːk/:adj. 萧瑟的:The report paints a bleak picture of rising unemployment.
11. concise /kənˈsaɪs/ adj. 简明的；简洁的；简短的
📖 英文解释
giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words; brief but comprehensive
✏️ 例句
The report should be concise and focused on key findings.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
concise summary/answer（简明摘要/回答）；be concise in expression（表达简洁）；考研写作指导
🧠 助记
con（共同）+ cise（cut 切）→ 把冗余都切掉 → 简洁的
concise::/kənˈsaɪs/:adj. 简明的:Good academic writing should be clear and concise.
12. disperse /dɪˈspɜːs/ v. 分散；驱散；散开
📖 英文解释
to distribute or spread over a wide area; to break up a crowd by causing individuals to leave
✏️ 例句
Police used tear gas to disperse the protesters.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
disperse a crowd（驱散人群）；disperse information（传播信息）；考研社会/科学类
🧠 助记
dis（分开）+ perse（per 每个）→ 分散到每个地方 → 分散
disperse::/dɪˈspɜːs/:v. 分散:Seeds are dispersed by wind and animals.
13. dividend /ˈdɪvɪdend/ n. 被除数；股息；红利
📖 英文解释
a sum of money paid regularly by a company to its shareholders; a number to be divided
✏️ 例句
Shareholders received a generous dividend at the end of the fiscal year.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
pay a dividend（支付股息）；dividend yield（股息收益率）；peace dividend（和平红利）；考研经济/金融类
🧠 助记
divide（分）+ nd → 被分开的数 → 被除数；公司利润分给股东 → 股息
dividend::/ˈdɪvɪdend/:n. 被除数:The company declared its highest dividend in ten years.
14. documentary /ˌdɒkjuˈmentri/ n. 纪录片 adj. 文件的；记录的

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
documentary film/evidence（纪录片/书面证据）；shoot a documentary（拍摄纪录片）；考研媒体/法律类
🧠 助记
document（文件/记录）+ ary → 用来记录真实的 → 纪录片
documentary::/ˌdɒkjuˈmentri/:n. 纪录片 adj. 文件的:Documentary evidence is required to support the claim.
15. exempt /ɪɡˈzempt/ v. 免除 adj. 被免除的；豁免的

✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
exempt from（免除…的义务）；tax-exempt（免税的）；grant exemption（给予豁免）；考研法律/经济类
🧠 助记
ex（出）+ empt（empty 清空）→ 从义务中清空出去 → 免除
exempt::/ɪɡˈzempt/:v. 免除 adj. 被免除的:Students are exempt from the fee if they qualify.

🧠 助记

facet::/ˈfæsɪt/:n. 方面:The project explored every facet of urban poverty.
17. freight /freɪt/ n. 货运；货物 v. 运送货物
📖 英文解释
goods transported in bulk by truck, train, ship, or aircraft; the transport of goods in bulk
✏️ 例句
The company ships freight by rail to reduce carbon emissions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
freight train（货运列车）；air/sea freight（空运/海运）；freight charges（运费）；考研交通/经济类
🧠 助记
freight 谐音[福瑞特] → 运送货物的服务 → 货运
freight::/freɪt/:n. 货运:Sea freight is cheaper but slower than air cargo.
18. hover /ˈhɒvə(r)/ v. 盘旋；悬停；徘徊
📖 英文解释
to remain in one place in the air; to wait in a state of uncertainty near someone or something
✏️ 例句
A helicopter hovered above the scene of the accident.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
hover around（在…附近徘徊）；hover over（盘旋在…上方）；hover between（在两者间摇摆）；考研描写场景
🧠 助记
hover 谐音[霍弗] → 像霍弗特效一样悬浮 → 盘旋
hover::/ˈhɒvə(r)/:v. 盘旋:Inflation has hovered around 3% for the past year.
19. hum /hʌm/ v. 哼；嗡嗡响 n. 哼声；嗡嗡声
📖 英文解释
to make a low, steady continuous sound; to be in a state of activity
✏️ 例句
She hummed a tune while working in the kitchen.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
hum a tune（哼一首曲子）；hum with activity（忙碌地运转）；hum and haw（踌躇）；考研日常/描写类
🧠 助记
hum 模拟嗡嗡声 → 哼
hum::/hʌm/:v. 哼:The office was humming with excitement on launch day.
20. impair /ɪmˈpeə(r)/ v. 损害；削弱；减弱
📖 英文解释
to weaken or damage something; to diminish the value, quality, or strength of something
✏️ 例句
Alcohol can seriously impair your ability to drive safely.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
impair judgment/vision/performance（损害判断力/视力/表现）；visually impaired（视力受损的）；考研医疗/安全类
🧠 助记
im（在内部）+ pair（损害/配对）→ 内部损坏 → 损害
impair::/ɪmˈpeə(r)/:v. 损害:Lack of sleep can severely impair cognitive function.
21. levy /ˈlevi/ v. 征收（税）；征召 n. 税收；征兵
📖 英文解释
to impose a tax, fee, or fine; to enlist for military service; a tax or financial charge
✏️ 例句
The government decided to levy a new tax on carbon emissions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
levy a tax/fine（征税/处以罚款）；levy on/upon（向…征收）；考研经济/法律类高频词
🧠 助记
levy 谐音[勒维] → 勒索维持税收 → 征税
levy::/ˈlevi/:v. 征收（税）:A new tariff was levied on imported steel products.
22. mainland /ˈmeɪnlænd/ n. 大陆；本土（与岛或附属领土相对）
📖 英文解释
a large continuous extent of land that includes the greater part of a country or territory
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
mainland China（中国大陆）；the mainland（本土）；mainland Europe（欧洲大陆）；考研地理/政治类
🧠 助记
main（主要）+ land（陆地）→ 主要的大陆 → 大陆
mainland::/ˈmeɪnlænd/:n. 大陆:The policy applies to the mainland but not the territories.
23. northeast /ˌnɔːθˈiːst/ n./adj. 东北；东北方向的
📖 英文解释
the direction or compass point midway between north and east; the region in this direction
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
northeast of（在…东北方）；the Northeast（东北地区）；考研地理/气候类
🧠 助记

northeast::/ˌnɔːθˈiːst/:n./adj. 东北:The industrial base is concentrated in the northeast of the country.
24. notorious /nəʊˈtɔːriəs/ adj. 臭名昭著的；声名狼藉的
📖 英文解释
famous or well known, typically for some bad quality or deed
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
notorious for（因…而臭名昭著）；a notorious criminal（臭名昭著的罪犯）；考研社会/法律类
🧠 助记
not（不好的）+ or（ious）+ ious → 以不好的方式出名 → 臭名昭著的
notorious::/nəʊˈtɔːriəs/:adj. 臭名昭著的:The area is notorious for its high crime rate.

a small booklet or leaflet containing information or arguments about a single subject

pamphlet::/ˈpæmflɪt/:n. 小册子:The pamphlet called for an end to government corruption.
