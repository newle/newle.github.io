1. prosecute /ˈprɒsɪkjuːt/ v. 起诉；检举
📖 英文解释
to institute legal proceedings against a person in court; to carry on or continue with an activity
✏️ 例句
The district attorney decided to prosecute the suspect.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
prosecute sb for sth；prosecutor（n. 检察官）；考研：法律语境
🧠 助记
pro（向前）+ secute（追）→ 向前追着人去法庭 → 起诉
prosecute::/ˈprɒsɪkjuːt/:v. 起诉:He was prosecuted for tax evasion.
2. savage /ˈsævɪdʒ/ adj. 凶猛的；野蛮的；n. 野蛮人；v. 猛烈抨击
📖 英文解释
fierce, cruel, or uncontrolled; uncivilized; to attack or criticize fiercely
✏️ 例句
The new policy received savage criticism from the opposition.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
savage attack/criticism；savage irony；考研：比喻义「猛烈」常考
🧠 助记
savage → save（拯救）的反义→ 无法被文明拯救的 → 野蛮的
savage::/ˈsævɪdʒ/:adj. 凶猛的:She delivered a savage critique of the government.
3. surveillance /sɜːˈveɪləns/ n. 监视；监控
📖 英文解释
close observation, especially of a suspected person; monitoring of behavior or activities
✏️ 例句
Security cameras provide round-the-clock surveillance of public spaces.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
under surveillance；surveillance camera/state；考研：安全/隐私语境
🧠 助记
sur（上方）+ veill（看）+ ance → 从上方监视 → 监控
surveillance::/sɜːˈveɪləns/:n. 监视:The area was under constant surveillance.
4. transient /ˈtrænziənt/ adj. 短暂的；临时的；n. 过客
📖 英文解释
lasting only for a short time; not permanent; a person staying only briefly
✏️ 例句
Fame can be transient; lasting influence requires sustained effort.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
transient population/phenomenon；transient pleasure；考研：哲学/社会语境
🧠 助记
trans（经过）+ ient → 经过就走的 → 短暂的
transient::/ˈtrænziənt/:adj. 短暂的:His success proved transient.
5. abrupt /əˈbrʌpt/ adj. 突然的；陡峭的；唐突的
📖 英文解释
sudden and unexpected; steep; brief to the point of rudeness
✏️ 例句
The meeting came to an abrupt end when the fire alarm sounded.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
abrupt end/change/manner；abruptly（adv.）；考研：副词形式常考
🧠 助记
ab（离开）+ rupt（破裂）→ 突然断开 → 突然的
abrupt::/əˈbrʌpt/:adj. 突然的:His departure was abrupt and unexplained.
6. affiliate /əˈfɪlieɪt/ v. 使附属；加入；n. 附属机构
📖 英文解释
to officially join or become attached to a larger organization; a company connected to another
✏️ 例句
The university is affiliated with several research institutes.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
be affiliated with/to；affiliate company/member；考研：组织/机构语境
🧠 助记
af + filiate（儿子）→ 像儿子一样附属 → 使附属
affiliate::/əˈfɪlieɪt/:v. 使附属:The branch is affiliated with the national organization.
7. align /əˈlaɪn/ v. 调整；使排成直线；支持
📖 英文解释
to place or arrange in a straight line; to bring into agreement or cooperation
✏️ 例句
The two countries aligned their policies on climate change.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
align with sb/sth；be in alignment；考研：政治/战略语境
🧠 助记
a + line（线）→ 使成一条线 → 对齐、调整
align::/əˈlaɪn/:v. 调整:They aligned their strategy with market trends.
8. assault /əˈsɔːlt/ n./v. 攻击；袭击；侵犯
📖 英文解释
a physical attack; an unlawful threat to harm; to attack violently

🧠 助记
as（加强）+ salt（盐，刺激）→ 给人刺激的攻击 → 袭击
assault::/əˈsɔːlt/:n./v. 攻击:She was charged with assault.
9. beginning /bɪˈɡɪnɪŋ/ n. 开始；起点
📖 英文解释
the time or place at which something starts; an initial stage or part

🧠 助记
begin（开始）+ ning → 开始的状态 → 开始
beginning::/bɪˈɡɪnɪŋ/:n. 开始:The beginning of the novel sets the tone.
10. conjunction /kənˈdʒʌŋkʃn/ n. 结合；连词；连接
📖 英文解释
the action of joining or combining; a word used to connect clauses (e.g. and, but, because)
✏️ 例句
The project was completed in conjunction with local authorities.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in conjunction with（与…协同）；conjunction（语法连词）；考研：语法/正式语境
🧠 助记
con（共同）+ junct（连接）+ ion → 共同连接 → 结合、连词
conjunction::/kənˈdʒʌŋkʃn/:n. 结合:They worked in conjunction with the government.
11. consolidate /kənˈsɒlɪdeɪt/ v. 巩固；统一；加强
📖 英文解释
to make something stronger or more solid; to combine smaller companies or organizations into a larger one
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
consolidate power/gains/position；consolidation（n.）；考研：政治/商务语境
🧠 助记

consolidate::/kənˈsɒlɪdeɪt/:v. 巩固:She worked hard to consolidate her lead.
12. counsel /ˈkaʊnsl/ n. 建议；律师；v. 劝告

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记

counsel::/ˈkaʊnsl/:n. 建议:The doctor counseled the patient on healthy habits.

criticise::/ˈkrɪtɪsaɪz/:v. 批评:She criticised the policy as ineffective.

hers::/hɜːz/:pron. 她的（所有格）:The decision was entirely hers to make.

overhead::/ˌəʊvəˈhed/:adv./adj. 在头顶上:Overhead costs must be kept low.
