1. decree /dɪˈkriː/ n. 法令；政令；判决 v. 颁布法令；命令
📖 英文解释
an official order made by a government, ruler, or court; to issue such an order.
✏️ 例句
The president issued a decree banning the use of plastic bags nationwide.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ issue/issue a decree 颁布法令 ★ decree that 命令；规定 royal decree 皇家令 常考：政治/法律语境词
🧠 助记
de（下达）+ cree（呼喊）→ 向下大声呼喊命令 → 法令；颁布
decree::/dɪˈkriː/:n. 法令:The court issued a decree ordering the company to pay compensation.
2. dilute /daɪˈluːt/ v. 稀释；削弱 adj. 稀释的；淡的
📖 英文解释
to make a liquid thinner by adding water; to weaken the strength of something.
✏️ 例句
The government's policies were diluted before they were passed into law.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ dilute with water 用水稀释 ★ dilute the impact/effect 削弱效果 常考：化学语境和比喻义都有考查
🧠 助记
di（分开）+ lute（流液体）→ 往液体里分散稀释 → 稀释
dilute::/daɪˈluːt/:v. 稀释:The acid must be diluted before use.
3. disappoint /ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt/ v. 使失望；使沮丧
📖 英文解释
to make someone sad because something they hoped for did not happen.
✏️ 例句
The test results disappointed everyone who had worked so hard to prepare.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ disappoint sb 使某人失望 ★ be disappointed in/with 对...感到失望 disappointing adj. 令人失望的 常考：注意 disappointed（人感到失望）vs disappointing（令人失望的）
🧠 助记
dis（不）+ appoint（约定）→ 没有实现约定 → 使失望
disappoint::/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt/:v. 使失望:The film disappointed critics despite the high expectations.
4. discreet /dɪˈskriːt/ adj. 谨慎的；慎重的；含蓄的
📖 英文解释
careful and prudent in speech or action, especially to avoid causing offense or embarrassment.
✏️ 例句
She was discreet about her personal life and rarely discussed it with colleagues.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ be discreet about 对...谨慎低调 ★ discreet inquiry 低调的询问 常考：注意与 discrete（分离的）拼写区别！
🧠 助记
dis（分开）+ creet（辨别）→ 能辨别分清情况而谨慎 → 谨慎的
discreet::/dɪˈskriːt/:adj. 谨慎的:A discreet word in private solved the misunderstanding.
5. dissipate /ˈdɪsɪpeɪt/ v. 消散；驱散；挥霍
📖 英文解释
to scatter and gradually disappear; to waste money or resources.
✏️ 例句
The morning mist dissipated as the sun rose higher in the sky.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ dissipate energy/tension 消散能量/紧张 ★ dissipate one's fortune 挥霍财富 常考：物理/比喻两种义都有考查
🧠 助记
dis（分散）+ sipate（喝掉）→ 慢慢喝散 → 消散；挥霍
dissipate::/ˈdɪsɪpeɪt/:v. 消散:The thick fog slowly dissipated by midday.
6. dynasty /ˈdɪnəsti/ n. 朝代；王朝；（名门）望族
📖 英文解释
a sequence of rulers from the same family, or a powerful family or group.
✏️ 例句
The Tang Dynasty is considered a golden age in Chinese history.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ ruling dynasty 统治王朝 ★ business dynasty 商业王朝 常考：历史类阅读高频词
🧠 助记
dyna（权力，同 dynamic）+ sty → 有权力的家族统治 → 王朝
dynasty::/ˈdɪnəsti/:n. 朝代:The Ming Dynasty lasted for nearly three centuries.
7. edible /ˈedɪbl/ adj. 可食用的；适合食用的
📖 英文解释
safe or suitable to eat.
✏️ 例句
Not all wild mushrooms are edible; some can be deadly if consumed.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ edible plants 可食用的植物 edible oil 食用油 常考：注意与 eatable（吃得下去的）区分
🧠 助记
ed（eat 的变形）+ ible（可以...的）→ 可以吃的 → 可食用的
edible::/ˈedɪbl/:adj. 可食用的:The guide taught us which wild plants are edible.
8. elastic /ɪˈlæstɪk/ adj. 弹性的；有弹力的；灵活的 n. 橡皮筋
📖 英文解释
able to stretch and return to original shape; adaptable and flexible.
✏️ 例句
The demand for luxury goods is not always elastic and may vary unpredictably.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ elastic demand 弹性需求（经济学） ★ elastic band 橡皮筋 elastic clause 弹性条款 常考：经济学 elastic/inelastic demand 高频
🧠 助记
elast（弹）+ ic → 能弹回来的 → 弹性的
elastic::/ɪˈlæstɪk/:adj. 弹性的:The price of bread is relatively inelastic compared to luxuries.
9. escalate /ˈeskəleɪt/ v. 升级；逐步升高；扩大
📖 英文解释
to become or make something more serious or intense, especially a conflict.
✏️ 例句
The trade dispute escalated into a full-scale economic war between the two nations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ escalate into 升级为... ★ escalating tensions 日益升级的紧张局势 de-escalate 缓和局势 常考：政治/冲突类阅读高频
🧠 助记
escal（阶梯，同 escalator 自动扶梯）+ ate → 一步一步上升 → 升级
escalate::/ˈeskəleɪt/:v. 升级:The conflict gradually escalated despite peace talks.
10. escort /ˈeskɔːt/ v./n. 护送；陪同；护卫
📖 英文解释
to accompany and protect someone; a group of people or vehicles providing protection.
✏️ 例句
The president was escorted to the venue by a team of security guards.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ escort sb to 护送某人到某处 under escort 在护卫下 常考：军事/外交/安全语境
🧠 助记
e（出去）+ scort（同 court 宫廷侍卫）→ 侍卫出门护送 → 护送
escort::/ˈeskɔːt/:v./n. 护送:She was escorted safely through the crowd by the police.
11. exceedingly /ɪkˈsiːdɪŋli/ adv. 极其；非常；极度地
📖 英文解释
to a very great degree; extremely.
✏️ 例句
The exam was exceedingly difficult, even for the top students.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ exceedingly important/difficult 极其重要/困难 常考：正式书面语中表示"非常"的强调词 近义词：extremely, exceptionally
🧠 助记
exceed（超过）+ ing + ly → 超过一般程度地 → 极其
exceedingly::/ɪkˈsiːdɪŋli/:adv. 极其:He was exceedingly grateful for her help.
12. excite /ɪkˈsaɪt/ v. 使兴奋；激起；刺激
📖 英文解释
to cause strong feelings of enthusiasm or eagerness; to stimulate.
✏️ 例句
The announcement of the new policy excited considerable debate among economists.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ excite interest/debate 引起兴趣/争论 ★ excited adj. 兴奋的 excitement n. 兴奋 常考：excite 在学术语境中常表示"引起（反应）"
🧠 助记
ex（出来）+ cite（引用/激起）→ 激起情绪出来 → 使兴奋
excite::/ɪkˈsaɪt/:v. 使兴奋:The discovery excited great interest in the scientific community.
13. exquisite /ɪkˈskwɪzɪt/ adj. 精美的；精致的；剧烈的（痛苦）
📖 英文解释
extremely beautiful and delicate; or (of pain) intense.
✏️ 例句
The museum displayed an exquisite collection of Ming Dynasty porcelain.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ exquisite craftsmanship 精湛的工艺 ★ exquisite pain 剧烈的疼痛 exquisite taste 高雅的品味 常考：正面义（精美）与负面义（剧烈痛苦）都有考查
🧠 助记
ex（向外，完全）+ quisite（追求）→ 极致追求完美 → 精美的
exquisite::/ɪkˈskwɪzɪt/:adj. 精美的:She wore an exquisite necklace to the ceremony.
14. fairy /ˈfeəri/ n. 仙女；小精灵 adj. 仙境般的

fairy::/ˈfeəri/:n. 仙女:His rapid success story read like a fairy tale.

fist::/fɪst/:n. 拳头 v. 用拳打:He raised his fist in triumph after winning.

fortnight::/ˈfɔːtnaɪt/:n. 两周:They plan to visit again in a fortnight.

furious::/ˈfjʊəriəs/:adj. 狂怒的:He was furious about the last-minute cancellation.
