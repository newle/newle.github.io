1. bloody /ˈblʌdi/ adj. 血腥的；残忍的；（英俚）该死的
📖 英文解释
adj. covered in or involving blood; used as an intensifier in British English informal speech
✏️ 例句
It was a bloody battle that left many soldiers wounded.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
bloody nose（流血的鼻子）；bloody war（血腥的战争）；常考：描述暴力冲突
🧠 助记
blood（血）+ y → 充满血的 → 血腥的
bloody::/ˈblʌdi/:adj. 血腥的:The soldiers fought a long and bloody campaign.
2. centigrade /ˈsentɪɡreɪd/ adj. 摄氏的；百分度的
📖 英文解释
adj. relating to the Celsius temperature scale, on which water freezes at 0 degrees and boils at 100 degrees
✏️ 例句
Water boils at 100 degrees centigrade.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
degrees centigrade（摄氏度）；centigrade thermometer（摄氏温度计）；=Celsius
🧠 助记
centi（百）+ grade（刻度）→ 以100刻度为基准 → 百分度；摄氏的
centigrade::/ˈsentɪɡreɪd/:adj. 摄氏的:The temperature outside was 35 degrees centigrade during the heatwave.
3. choir /ˈkwaɪər/ n. 唱诗班；合唱团
📖 英文解释
n. an organized group of singers, especially one that performs in a church or at public events
✏️ 例句
She sang in the school choir for three years.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
choir practice（合唱排练）；church choir（教堂唱诗班）；choir boy（唱诗班男孩）
🧠 助记
choir发音奎尔 → 奎尔们在一起唱歌 → 合唱团
choir::/ˈkwaɪər/:n. 唱诗班:The choir performed beautifully at the Christmas concert.
4. clap /klæp/ v. 鼓掌；拍手 n. 鼓掌声
📖 英文解释
v. to strike the palms of the hands together repeatedly to show approval; to pat someone on the back n. a loud, sharp sound; the act of clapping
✏️ 例句
The audience clapped enthusiastically after the performance.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
clap hands（拍手）；clap sb on the back（拍背）；a clap of thunder（一声雷鸣）
🧠 助记
clap谐音咔啪 → 咔啪一声鼓掌 → 鼓掌
clap::/klæp/:v. 鼓掌:Everyone clapped as the winner received the trophy.
5. cloak /kləʊk/ n. 斗篷；遮掩物 v. 遮掩；掩盖
📖 英文解释
n. a loose outer garment without sleeves; something that covers or conceals v. to cover with or as if with a cloak; to conceal
✏️ 例句
The spy operated under a cloak of secrecy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
cloak of darkness（黑暗的掩护）；cloak and dagger（充满阴谋的）；cloak room（衣帽间）
🧠 助记
cloak谐音克劳克 → 克劳克用斗篷隐藏自己 → 斗篷；掩盖
cloak::/kləʊk/:n. 斗篷:His friendly manner served as a cloak for his true intentions.
6. comb /kəʊm/ n. 梳子 v. 梳理；搜寻
📖 英文解释
n. a flat device with teeth used for untangling or arranging hair v. to arrange hair with a comb; to search an area thoroughly
✏️ 例句
She combed her hair carefully before the interview.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
comb through（仔细搜查）；comb one's hair（梳头）；fine-tooth comb（细齿梳；仔细检查）
🧠 助记
comb谐音烤面包 → 梳齿像烤面包上的纹路 → 梳子
comb::/kəʊm/:n. 梳子 v. 梳理:Police combed the area for clues about the missing child.

debut::/ˈdeɪbjuː/:n. 初次登场:The new model debuted at the international auto show last month.
8. dwelling /ˈdwelɪŋ/ n. 住宅；居所

dwelling::/ˈdwelɪŋ/:n. 住宅:The report called for more affordable dwellings in the city center.
9. harden /ˈhɑːdn/ v. 硬化；变硬；使坚定

🧠 助记

harden::/ˈhɑːdn/:v. 硬化:Exposure to cold air will harden the clay model overnight.
10. inhale /ɪnˈheɪl/ v. 吸入；深呼吸
📖 英文解释

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
in（里）+ hale（吸）→ 向里吸气 → 吸入
inhale::/ɪnˈheɪl/:v. 吸入:Firefighters wear masks to avoid inhaling toxic smoke.
11. mourn /mɔːn/ v. 哀悼；悲痛

mourn::/mɔːn/:v. 哀悼:She still mourns the loss of her childhood home.
12. necessitate /nɪˈsesɪteɪt/ v. 迫使；使成为必要

necessitate::/nɪˈsesɪteɪt/:v. 迫使:Changes in technology necessitate constant retraining of workers.
