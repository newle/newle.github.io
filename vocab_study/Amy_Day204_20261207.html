1. linen /ˈlɪnɪn/ n. 亚麻布；亚麻制品
📖 英文解释
A type of cloth made from the fibers of the flax plant, known for being cool and smooth.
✏️ 例句
The hotel rooms were furnished with crisp white linen sheets that felt luxurious against the skin.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ linen cloth/sheets 亚麻布/床单 ★ linen suit 亚麻西装（常考搭配） wash one's dirty linen in public 家丑外扬 linen closet 存放床单的柜子
🧠 助记
line（线）+ n → 用细线织成的布料 = 亚麻布
linen::/ˈlɪnɪn/:n. 亚麻布:She wore a light linen dress in summer.
2. living /ˈlɪvɪŋ/ n. 生活；生计 adj. 活着的；现存的
📖 英文解释
The way in which a person lives, or the money needed to maintain one's lifestyle.
✏️ 例句
He moved to the countryside to enjoy a simpler living away from the hustle of the city.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ make a living 谋生（高频考点） ★ standard of living 生活水平 living conditions 生活条件 living wage 最低工资
🧠 助记
live（生活）的现在分词形式，引申为名词"生计"
living::/ˈlɪvɪŋ/:n. 生活:She makes a living by teaching music.
3. living room /ˈlɪvɪŋ ruːm/ n. 客厅；起居室
📖 英文解释
The main room in a house where people sit, relax, and entertain guests.
✏️ 例句
The family gathered in the living room to watch the championship game together.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ living room furniture 客厅家具 in the living room 在客厅里 同义词：sitting room, lounge（英式）
🧠 助记
living（生活）+ room（房间）= 生活的房间 = 客厅
living room::/ˈlɪvɪŋ ruːm/:n. 客厅:We redecorated the living room last weekend.
4. locomotive /ˌloʊkəˈmoʊtɪv/ n. 机车；火车头 adj. 运动的；移动的
📖 英文解释
A powered railway vehicle used to pull or push trains along a track.
✏️ 例句
The old steam locomotive was restored and now serves as a museum exhibit.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ steam locomotive 蒸汽机车 diesel/electric locomotive 柴油/电力机车 locomotive engine 机车引擎 ★ locomotive industry 机车工业
🧠 助记
loco（地方）+ motive（动力）= 移动的动力 = 机车
locomotive::/ˌloʊkəˈmoʊtɪv/:n. 机车:The locomotive pulled twenty freight cars.
5. magistrate /ˈmædʒɪstreɪt/ n. 地方法官；治安法官
📖 英文解释
A civil officer who administers the law, especially one who conducts a court dealing with minor criminal offenses.
✏️ 例句
The magistrate sentenced the shoplifter to community service rather than imprisonment.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ magistrate court 治安法院（考研法律词汇） chief magistrate 首席法官 appear before a magistrate 出庭 magistrate's jurisdiction 法官管辖权
🧠 助记
magist（拉丁语master主人）+ rate → 掌握权力的人 = 地方法官
magistrate::/ˈmædʒɪstreɪt/:n. 地方法官:The magistrate reviewed all the evidence carefully.
6. majesty /ˈmædʒɪsti/ n. 威严；雄伟；陛下（称谓）
📖 英文解释
Impressive stateliness, dignity, or beauty; also used as a title for a monarch.
✏️ 例句
The majesty of the mountain range left the tourists speechless with awe.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ Your/His/Her Majesty 陛下（敬称） ★ the majesty of nature 大自然的雄伟 majestic adj. 雄伟的（派生词考点） royal majesty 皇家威仪
🧠 助记
major（主要的）的变形 → 最重要的气势 = 威严
majesty::/ˈmædʒɪsti/:n. 威严:The majesty of the ancient temple was breathtaking.
7. March /mɑːrtʃ/ n. 三月；行进 v. 行军；游行
📖 英文解释
The third month of the year; also means to walk in a military manner or a protest parade.
✏️ 例句
Thousands of protesters marched through the capital demanding climate action.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ march against/for sth 游行反对/支持某事 ★ on the march 在行进中 march past 分列式行进 March 大写时指三月
🧠 助记
联想军队的March（三月）里举行阅兵march（行军）
March::/mɑːrtʃ/:n. 三月:Students marched for equal rights on campus.
8. married /ˈmærid/ adj. 已婚的；婚姻的
📖 英文解释
Having a husband or wife; united in marriage.
✏️ 例句
They have been married for twenty years and still share the same interests.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ get married to sb 与某人结婚 ★ married life/couple 婚姻生活/已婚夫妇 be married with children 已婚有孩子 married name 婚后姓氏
🧠 助记
marry（结婚）+ ed = 已经结婚的 = 已婚的
married::/ˈmærid/:adj. 已婚的:She has been married to her college sweetheart for a decade.
9. Marxist /ˈmɑːrksɪst/ adj. 马克思主义的 n. 马克思主义者
📖 英文解释
Relating to the political and economic theories of Karl Marx, or a person who follows these theories.
✏️ 例句
The professor offered a Marxist analysis of the widening income gap in modern societies.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ Marxist theory/ideology 马克思主义理论/意识形态 Marxist philosophy 马克思主义哲学 neo-Marxist 新马克思主义的 Marxism n. 马克思主义
🧠 助记
Marx（马克思）+ ist（主义者/相关的）= 马克思主义的
Marxist::/ˈmɑːrksɪst/:adj. 马克思主义的 n. 马克思主义:He wrote his thesis from a Marxist perspective.
10. mathematics /ˌmæθəˈmætɪks/ n. 数学
📖 英文解释
The abstract science of number, quantity, and space, studied in its own right or applied to other disciplines.
✏️ 例句
A solid foundation in mathematics is essential for students pursuing careers in engineering.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ applied/pure mathematics 应用/纯数学 do mathematics 做数学 mathematics teacher 数学老师 ★ mathematical adj. 数学的（派生词）
🧠 助记
math（数学）是其缩写形式，mathematics是完整拼写
mathematics::/ˌmæθəˈmætɪks/:n. 数学:Mathematics is required for all science majors.
11. means /miːnz/ n. 手段；方法；财富（常用复数）
📖 英文解释
Methods or resources used to achieve something; also refers to financial resources.
✏️ 例句
The government tried every possible means to reduce unemployment in the region.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ by means of 通过……方式（高频考点） ★ by all means 当然可以；尽一切努力 means of transport 交通方式 a man of means 有钱人
🧠 助记
mean（意味着；方式）的名词复数，引申为"手段"和"财富"
means::/miːnz/:n. 手段:They used every available means to solve the problem.
12. media /ˈmiːdiə/ n. 传播媒介；媒体（medium的复数）
📖 英文解释
The main means of mass communication, including television, radio, newspapers, and the internet.
✏️ 例句
Social media has transformed the way people consume news and interact with each other.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ the media 媒体（集合名词，常考） ★ social media 社交媒体 mass media 大众媒体 media coverage 媒体报道
🧠 助记
medium（媒介）的复数，来自拉丁语，现在常用media指代整个媒体行业
media::/ˈmiːdiə/:n. 传播媒介:The media played a crucial role in shaping public opinion.
13. millimetre /ˈmɪlɪmiːtər/ n. 毫米（英式拼写，美式为millimeter）
📖 英文解释
A unit of length equal to one thousandth of a meter.
✏️ 例句
The surgeon had to be precise to within a single millimetre during the delicate operation.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ millimetre of rain 毫米降水量 millimetre wave 毫米波（科技词汇） 缩写为mm 1 centimetre = 10 millimetres
🧠 助记
milli（千分之一）+ metre（米）= 千分之一米 = 毫米
millimetre::/ˈmɪlɪmiːtər/:n. 毫米（英式拼写，美式为millim:The gap was only two millimetres wide.
14. mischief /ˈmɪstʃɪf/ n. 恶作剧；调皮；伤害
📖 英文解释
Playful misbehavior or troublemaking, especially in children; or harm or trouble caused.
✏️ 例句
The children were always getting into mischief when the teacher left the classroom.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ get into mischief 惹麻烦；做恶作剧 do mischief 造成伤害 ★ mischievous adj. 调皮的（派生词） full of mischief 非常调皮
🧠 助记
mis（坏的）+ chief（首要的）→ 主要的坏事 = 恶作剧
mischief::/ˈmɪstʃɪf/:n. 恶作剧:The puppy was full of mischief and chewed on everything.
15. missing /ˈmɪsɪŋ/ adj. 失踪的；缺少的；遗失的
📖 英文解释
Not present or not able to be found; absent from the expected place.
✏️ 例句
Police launched a search operation for the missing child who had not returned home.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ go missing 失踪（高频搭配） missing person 失踪人员 ★ missing link 缺失的环节 missing in action (MIA) 战场上失踪
🧠 助记
miss（错过；想念）+ ing → 被错过的/找不到的 = 失踪的
missing::/ˈmɪsɪŋ/:adj. 失踪的:Three hikers were reported missing after the storm.
16. mistress /ˈmɪstrɪs/ n. 情妇；女主人；女教师（旧用法）
📖 英文解释
A woman who is in a long-term sexual relationship with a married man; historically also the female head of a household.
✏️ 例句
The scandal involving a politician and his mistress dominated the headlines for weeks.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ keep a mistress 包养情妇 mistress of the house 女主人 old mistress 旧情人 注意：也可指女教师（Mrs.的本源词）
🧠 助记
master（主人）的阴性形式，历史上指"女主人"，后演变为"情妇"
mistress::/ˈmɪstrɪs/:n. 情妇:The story revolved around a lord and his mistress.
17. mob /mɒb/ n. 暴民；乌合之众；黑手党 v. 聚众骚扰
📖 英文解释
A large disorderly crowd; also used as a verb meaning to crowd around someone in a threatening or enthusiastic way.
✏️ 例句
The police struggled to control the angry mob that had gathered outside the parliament building.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ mob rule 暴民统治；无政府状态 ★ a mob of people 一群乌合之众 mob violence 暴力骚乱 be mobbed by fans 被粉丝围堵
🧠 助记
mob源自拉丁语mobile vulgus（流动的大众），想象乌合之众在移动
mob::/mɒb/:n. 暴民:The celebrity was mobbed by fans at the airport.
18. mobilise /ˈmoʊbɪlaɪz/ v. 动员；调动；组织（英式拼写，美式为mobilize）
📖 英文解释
To organize and prepare people or resources for a particular purpose, especially military action.
✏️ 例句
The government mobilised thousands of volunteers to help with disaster relief efforts.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ mobilise troops/forces 调动军队 ★ mobilise public support 动员公众支持 mobilise resources 调动资源 mobilisation n. 动员（派生词）
🧠 助记
mobile（移动的）+ ise（使…化）= 使运动起来 = 动员
mobilise::/ˈmoʊbɪlaɪz/:v. 动员:The charity mobilised volunteers across the country.
19. modernisation /ˌmɒdənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ n. 现代化（英式拼写，美式为modernization）
📖 英文解释
The process of adapting something to modern needs, attitudes, or standards.
✏️ 例句
The modernisation of China's infrastructure has been one of the most significant economic achievements.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ modernisation of industry/agriculture 工业/农业现代化（重要政治词汇） drive modernisation 推动现代化 ★ the four modernisations 四个现代化（中国历史） modernise v. 使现代化
🧠 助记
modern（现代的）+ isation（过程）= 现代化的过程 = 现代化
modernisation::/ˌmɒdənaɪˈzeɪʃən/:n. 现代化（英式拼写，美式为moder:The modernisation of the railway system took five years.
20. Monday /ˈmʌndeɪ/ n. 星期一
📖 英文解释
The day of the week before Tuesday and after Sunday.
✏️ 例句
The meeting was rescheduled from Friday to the following Monday due to the holiday.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ on Monday 在星期一 last/next Monday 上/下个星期一 Monday morning 周一早上 Blue Monday 忧郁的星期一（文化词汇）
🧠 助记
Moon's day（月亮之日）= Monday，古英语中星期一以月亮命名
Monday::/ˈmʌndeɪ/:n. 星期一:The new policy takes effect on Monday.
21. mug /mʌɡ/ n. 杯；马克杯；脸（俚语） v. 抢劫；扮鬼脸
📖 英文解释
A large cup with a handle, used for hot drinks; also means to attack and rob someone.
✏️ 例句
She sipped her morning coffee from a large ceramic mug while reading the newspaper.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ a mug of coffee/tea 一杯咖啡/茶 ★ mug sb (抢劫某人，考研完形填空词汇) mug shot 嫌疑人照片（俚语） mugger n. 街头抢劫犯
🧠 助记
想象一个大杯子(mug)的样子，古代杯子有人脸浮雕，故引申为"脸"
mug::/mʌɡ/:n. 杯:He was mugged on his way home late at night.
22. naughty /ˈnɔːti/ adj. 淘气的；不乖的；下流的
📖 英文解释
Behaving badly or disobediently; mildly rude or indecent.
✏️ 例句
The naughty child kept interrupting the class with silly jokes and paper airplanes.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ a naughty child 淘气的孩子 naughty but nice 坏而可爱的 naughty joke 下流笑话 naughtiness n. 顽皮（派生词）
🧠 助记
nought（零/什么都没有）+ y → 什么价值都没有的行为 = 淘气的
naughty::/ˈnɔːti/:adj. 淘气的:The naughty puppy chewed through the garden hose.
23. normalisation /ˌnɔːrməlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ n. 正常化；规范化（英式拼写，美式为normalization）
📖 英文解释
The process of making something conform to a standard or returning it to a normal state.
✏️ 例句
The normalisation of relations between the two countries took decades of diplomatic effort.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ normalisation of relations 关系正常化（外交词汇） normalise v. 正常化 ★ the normalisation of abnormal behavior 异常行为的正常化 data normalisation 数据规范化
🧠 助记
normal（正常的）+ isation（过程）= 使之正常的过程 = 正常化
normalisation::/ˌnɔːrməlaɪˈzeɪʃən/:n. 正常化:The normalisation of diplomatic ties boosted trade.

north（北）+ west（西）= 北+西 = 西北方向
northwest::/ˌnɔːrθˈwest/:n./adj./adv. 西北（方向/的:The expedition headed northwest through the mountains.
