1. law /lɔː/ n. 法律；法则；规律；法学
📖 英文解释
The system of rules that a particular country or community recognizes as regulating the actions of its members; a statement of fact that is invariably true.
✏️ 例句
Environmental activists are pushing for stronger laws to penalize companies that illegally dispose of industrial waste.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ obey/break the law 遵守/违反法律 ★ pass a law 通过一项法律 ★ law of nature 自然规律（科学语境）
🧠 助记
law → l + aw → 联想：违法要「挨打（aw！）」→ 法律就是规矩。
law::/lɔː/:n. 法律:New environmental laws require companies to disclose their emissions.
2. environment /ɪnˈvaɪrənmənt/ n. 环境；自然环境；氛围
📖 英文解释
The surroundings or conditions in which a person, animal, or plant lives; the natural world as a whole.
✏️ 例句
Protecting the environment requires coordinated international action, as pollution and climate change do not respect national borders.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ protect/preserve the environment 保护环境（必背） ★ learning environment 学习环境 ★ hostile/supportive environment 恶劣/有利环境
🧠 助记
en-（使）+ viron（圈，同 viral/around）+ ment → 围绕着我们的东西 → 环境。
environment::/ɪnˈvaɪrənmənt/:n. 环境:A positive classroom environment promotes student engagement.
3. paper /ˈpeɪpər/ n. 纸；论文；试卷；报纸 v. 贴墙纸
📖 英文解释
Material in thin sheets, used for writing on or wrapping; a newspaper; a scholarly article.
✏️ 例句
The researcher published a paper in a leading journal arguing that social inequality is the primary driver of poor health outcomes.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ academic/research paper 学术/研究论文（考研语境最重要） ★ on paper 在纸面上；理论上 ★ paper over 掩盖（问题）
🧠 助记
paper → pa（爸爸）+ per（通过）→ 爸爸通过考「试卷（paper）」→ 试卷。
paper::/ˈpeɪpər/:n. 纸:Her paper on climate policy was widely cited by researchers.
4. mind /maɪnd/ n. 思维；想法；意见 v. 介意；注意；照看
📖 英文解释
The element of a person that enables them to be aware of the world and their experiences; to be bothered by something.
✏️ 例句
Some philosophers argue that the relationship between mind and body remains one of the most profound unsolved problems in science.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ keep/bear in mind 记住；牢记 ★ change one's mind 改变主意 ★ mind the gap 注意差距（伦敦地铁语；引申义很广）
🧠 助记
mind → m + ind → 联想「我（m-）内心（ind-）的声音」→ 思维、想法。
mind::/maɪnd/:n. 思维:Keep in mind that consistent effort leads to long-term success.
5. kid /kɪd/ n. 孩子；小孩 v. 开玩笑；欺骗
📖 英文解释
A child or young person; to deceive someone in a playful way.
✏️ 例句
Research suggests that kids who read for pleasure from an early age develop stronger academic skills and greater empathy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ as a kid 小时候 ★ no kidding 不是开玩笑的（强调真实） ★ you're kidding me 你在开玩笑吧（口语）
🧠 助记
kid → k + id → 有「id」（身份证）的小「k」→ 小孩。or: kid 联想「小羊（kid goat）」→ 可爱的小东西 → 孩子。
kid::/kɪd/:n. 孩子:Every kid deserves access to quality education.
6. economy /ɪˈkɒnəmi/ n. 经济；经济体系；节约
📖 英文解释
The system of production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services; careful management of resources.
✏️ 例句
The global economy is increasingly interconnected, meaning that a financial crisis in one country can rapidly affect others.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ market economy 市场经济 ★ global/national economy 全球/国家经济 ★ economic growth/recovery 经济增长/复苏（形容词形式）
🧠 助记
eco-（家，同 ecology）+ nomy（管理，同 autonomy）→ 管理家务 → 经济。
economy::/ɪˈkɒnəmi/:n. 经济:Education is a key driver of long-term economic growth.
7. percent /pərˈsent/ n./adv. 百分之…；百分比
📖 英文解释
A proportion or share in relation to a whole, expressed as a fraction of 100.
✏️ 例句
According to the report, nearly 40 percent of all food produced globally is wasted before it reaches consumers.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ percent of 百分之…（数字 + percent + of + 名词） ★ by ... percent 增加/减少了百分之… percentage（百分比，可数名词）vs. percent（不可数）
🧠 助记
per-（每）+ cent（百，同 century/centimeter）→ 每一百 → 百分之。
percent::/pərˈsent/:n./adv. 百分之…:Over 60 percent of students passed the final exam.
8. sleep /sliːp/ v. 睡觉；处于休眠状态 n. 睡眠；睡觉
📖 英文解释
To rest with eyes closed and the mind and body in a state of reduced activity; a period of sleeping.
✏️ 例句
Chronic sleep deprivation has been linked to increased risks of obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ sleep deprivation 睡眠剥夺（健康话题） ★ get enough sleep 获得足够睡眠 ★ sleep on it 过一夜再做决定（习语）
🧠 助记
sleep → sl（滑）+ eep（深）→ 滑入深处 → 进入深度睡眠。
sleep::/sliːp/:v. 睡觉:Adults need at least seven hours of sleep per night.
9. recent /ˈriːsənt/ adj. 近来的；最新的；近期的
📖 英文解释
Having happened or begun only a short time ago; belonging to a past period of time comparatively close to the present.
✏️ 例句
Recent studies have challenged the long-held assumption that multitasking improves productivity in the workplace.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ in recent years/decades 近年来/几十年（阅读最高频时间状语） ★ recent research/evidence 最新研究/证据 ★ recently（副词形式）
🧠 助记
re-（再）+ cent（百年，近）→ 再次出现在「近」处 → 近来的。
recent::/ˈriːsənt/:adj. 近来的:Recent advances in AI have transformed many industries.
10. half /hɑːf/ n./adj./adv. 一半；半个；半…
📖 英文解释
Either of two equal or corresponding parts into which something is or can be divided; to the extent of half.
✏️ 例句
The report found that almost half of all university students experience significant mental health challenges during their studies.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ half of 一半的…（数量表达） ★ half the time 一半时间；往往（习语） ★ go halves 平摊费用
🧠 助记
half → h + alf → 联想：「ha！只剩一半（lf）了」→ 一半。
half::/hɑːf/:n./adj./adv. 一半:Half of the participants reported improved well-being after the program.
11. attention /əˈtenʃən/ n. 注意；关注；注意力
📖 英文解释
The action of dealing with or taking special care of someone or something; notice taken of someone or something.
✏️ 例句
Paying attention to student feedback is essential for teachers who want to improve the quality of their instruction.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ pay attention to 注意；关注（超高频） ★ attract/draw attention to 吸引注意力 ★ attention span 注意力持续时间
🧠 助记
at-（朝向）+ ten-（伸展，同 extend）+ tion → 向某处伸展感知 → 注意。
attention::/əˈtenʃən/:n. 注意:The report drew attention to the growing inequality in education.
12. body /ˈbɒdi/ n. 身体；主体；团体；尸体
📖 英文解释
The physical structure of a person or animal; the main or central part of something; an organized group.
✏️ 例句
A growing body of evidence suggests that urban green spaces have significant benefits for both physical and mental health.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ a body of evidence/research 大量证据/研究（学术高频） ★ governing body 管理机构 ★ body language 肢体语言
🧠 助记
body → b + ody → 联想：「哦，滴（ody）血了」→ 身体受伤 → 身体。
body::/ˈbɒdi/:n. 身体:A growing body of research supports the benefits of mindfulness.
13. measure /ˈmeʒər/ v. 衡量；测量 n. 措施；程度；尺度
📖 英文解释
To ascertain the size, amount, or degree of something; an action taken to achieve a desired outcome.
✏️ 例句
Governments are under pressure to take concrete measures to reduce greenhouse gas emissions before the next climate summit.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ take measures to do sth 采取措施做某事（写作必备） ★ measure up to 达到（标准/期望） ★ beyond measure 无法衡量；极度地
🧠 助记
mea-（月亮，古代用月相计量）+ sure（确定）→ 用月相确定时间 → 测量、措施。
measure::/ˈmeʒər/:v. 衡量:The government took emergency measures to control the spread of disease.

office::/ˈɒfɪs/:n. 办公室:The new government came to office with ambitious reform plans.

major::/ˈmeɪdʒər/:adj. 主要的:Pollution is a major public health concern in many cities.

19. remain /rɪˈmeɪn/ v. 保持；仍然是；留下；剩余

remain::/rɪˈmeɪn/:v. 保持:Poverty remains a major obstacle to social development.
20. though /ðoʊ/ conj. 尽管；虽然 adv. 然而；不过
📖 英文解释
Despite the fact that; however; used to introduce a concessive clause.
✏️ 例句
Economic growth is desirable, though it must be pursued in ways that do not compromise environmental sustainability.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
★ even though 即使（强调让步，比 though 更强） ★ as though 好像；仿佛 ★ though 放句末 = however（副词用法）
🧠 助记
though → t + hough → 联想「tho-ugh」读「虽」→ 尽管（让步转折）。
though::/ðoʊ/:conj. 尽管:The plan is promising, though it requires more research.

design::/dɪˈzaɪn/:v. 设计:The curriculum was redesigned to focus on practical skills.
