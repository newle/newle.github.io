1. dress /dres/ v. 穿衣；n. 连衣裙
📖 英文解释
to put clothes on oneself or another person; a one-piece garment worn by women
✏️ 例句
She dressed quickly and ran to catch the bus.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
dress up 盛装打扮；dress code 着装规范；well-dressed 穿着得体的
🧠 助记
dress = d + ress，想象穿上'礼服(dress)'，每天要'打扮(d-ress)'自己
dress::/dres/:v. 穿衣:She dressed up for the formal dinner.
2. vacation /veɪˈkeɪʃn/ n. 假期；休假
📖 英文解释
a period of time spent away from home or work for rest and enjoyment
✏️ 例句
They spent their summer vacation traveling across Europe.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
on vacation 在度假；take a vacation 休假；vacation days 休假天数
🧠 助记
vac-ation，vac = vacant（空的），假期就是让日程'空'下来的时间
vacation::/veɪˈkeɪʃn/:n. 假期:We took a two-week vacation in July.
3. reasonable /ˈriːznəbl/ adj. 合理的；通情达理的
📖 英文解释
fair and sensible; not too expensive or extreme; based on good judgment
✏️ 例句
The price of the hotel was very reasonable for that area.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
reasonable price 合理价格；reasonable request 合理要求；beyond reasonable doubt 排除合理怀疑
🧠 助记
reason（理由）+ able（能够），能够给出理由的就是'合理的'
reasonable::/ˈriːznəbl/:adj. 合理的:His demands were perfectly reasonable.
4. odd /ɒd/ adj. 奇怪的；奇数的；零散的
📖 英文解释
strange or unexpected; not divisible by two; occasional or extra
✏️ 例句
It was odd that she didn't reply to any of my messages.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
odd number 奇数；odd jobs 零工；odds and ends 零散物品；at odds with 与…不一致
🧠 助记
odd谐音'哦的'，看到奇怪的事情说'哦的！'表示惊讶
odd::/ɒd/:adj. 奇怪的:There's something odd about his behavior.
5. significance /sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəns/ n. 重要性；意义
📖 英文解释
the quality of being important or having a special meaning
✏️ 例句
The discovery was of great significance to the scientific community.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
of great significance 非常重要；historical significance 历史意义；significance of 的重要性
🧠 助记
signif-icance，sign（符号/迹象）→ signify（意味着）→ significance（意义/重要性），逐步升级
significance::/sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəns/:n. 重要性:The significance of this event cannot be overstated.
6. examination /ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃn/ n. 考试；检查；审查
📖 英文解释
a formal test of knowledge or ability; a close and careful inspection
✏️ 例句
The doctor conducted a thorough physical examination.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
pass an examination 通过考试；under examination 正在审查；medical examination 体检
🧠 助记
exam（考试）+ ination，就是'考试'的正式说法，比exam更正式
examination::/ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃn/:n. 考试:Students must pass a final examination.
7. detect /dɪˈtekt/ v. 察觉；发现；探测
📖 英文解释
to discover or notice the presence or existence of something
✏️ 例句
Scientists detected traces of water on the planet's surface.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
detect a problem 发现问题；hard to detect 难以察觉；detect crime 侦查罪案
🧠 助记
de-tect，tect = protect（保护）的tect，去掉保护就能'看清'真相，即'探测/发现'
detect::/dɪˈtekt/:v. 察觉:The sensors can detect small changes in temperature.
8. host /həʊst/ n. 主人；主持人；v. 主办
📖 英文解释
a person who receives or entertains guests; to organize and provide facilities for an event
✏️ 例句
The country will host the Olympic Games next year.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
host country 东道国；host a party 举办派对；TV host 电视主持人；a host of 大量的
🧠 助记
host谐音'好斯特'，好客的人才能做'主人/主持人'
host::/həʊst/:n. 主人:China hosted the 2008 Olympic Games.
9. estate /ɪˈsteɪt/ n. 房地产；庄园；遗产
📖 英文解释
a large area of land with a large house on it; property left by a deceased person
✏️ 例句
He inherited a large estate from his grandfather.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
real estate 房地产；estate agent 房产经纪人；housing estate 住宅区
🧠 助记
e-state，state（状态/州）→ estate（房产/庄园），不动产就是你的'地盘状态'
estate::/ɪˈsteɪt/:n. 房地产:She left her entire estate to charity.
10. initiative /ɪˈnɪʃətɪv/ n. 主动性；倡议；n./adj. 首创的
📖 英文解释
the ability to assess and initiate things independently; a plan or strategy designed to deal with a problem
✏️ 例句
She showed great initiative by starting the project without being asked.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
take the initiative 采取主动；on one's own initiative 主动地；peace initiative 和平倡议
🧠 助记
init（初始化）+ iative，'初始化'行动就是'主动出击'，即'主动性/倡议'
initiative::/ɪˈnɪʃətɪv/:n. 主动性:The government launched a new initiative to reduce poverty.
11. divorce /dɪˈvɔːs/ n./v. 离婚；分离
📖 英文解释
the legal ending of a marriage; to end a marriage legally; to separate from
✏️ 例句
After years of conflict, they finally decided to divorce.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
get a divorce 离婚；divorce rate 离婚率；divorce from reality 脱离现实
🧠 助记
di-vorce，di=two（两个），vorce联想force（力量），两股力量'分开'就是'离婚'
divorce::/dɪˈvɔːs/:n./v. 离婚:The divorce rate has increased in recent decades.
12. dispute /dɪˈspjuːt/ n./v. 争论；争议；纠纷
📖 英文解释
an argument or disagreement; to argue about or question the truth of something
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
dis-pute，dis（分开）+ pute联想compute（计算），各自'算账'就产生'争议'
dispute::/dɪˈspjuːt/:n./v. 争论:The workers went on strike over a pay dispute.
13. accurate /ˈækjərət/ adj. 准确的；精确的
📖 英文解释

🧠 助记
accur-ate，cur = care（关心），关心到细节就能做到'准确'
accurate::/ˈækjərət/:adj. 准确的:Scientists need accurate measurements in experiments.
14. brown /braʊn/ adj. 棕色的；n. 棕色

snow::/snəʊ/:n. 雪:It started to snow heavily in the afternoon.

weigh::/weɪ/:v. 称重:The doctor asked the patient to weigh themselves weekly.

23. evolve /ɪˈvɒlv/ v. 进化；演变；逐步发展

mislead::/ˌmɪsˈliːd/:v. 误导:Politicians should never deliberately mislead voters.

distinction::/dɪˈstɪŋkʃn/:n. 区别:There is a clear distinction between the two theories.
