1. sequence /ˈsiːkwəns/ n. 顺序；序列；一系列事件
📖 英文解释
a particular order in which related things follow each other; a set of related events or things
✏️ 例句
The DNA sequence determines the genetic traits of an organism.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in sequence（按顺序）；sequence of events（事件序列）；logical sequence（逻辑顺序）
🧠 助记
sequ（跟随/follow）+ ence → 一个接一个跟随 → 顺序、序列
sequence::/ˈsiːkwəns/:n. 顺序:The tasks must be completed in a specific sequence.
2. systematic /ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk/ adj. 系统的；成体系的；有条理的
📖 英文解释
done or acting according to a fixed plan or system; methodical
✏️ 例句
A systematic approach to problem-solving yields better results.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
systematic review（系统综述）；systematic approach（系统方法）；systematic error（系统误差）
🧠 助记
system（系统）+ atic → 按系统的 → 系统的、有条理的
systematic::/ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk/:adj. 系统的:The researchers conducted a systematic analysis of all available data.
3. horizon /həˈraɪzən/ n. 地平线；视野；眼界
📖 英文解释
the line at which the earth's surface and the sky appear to meet; the limit of a person's knowledge or interests
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
hori（水平/horizontal）+ zon → 水平线处 → 地平线；由此延伸 → 视野
horizon::/həˈraɪzən/:n. 地平线:New technologies are on the horizon that will transform the industry.
