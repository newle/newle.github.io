1. departure /dɪˈpɑːrtʃər/ n. 离开；出发；背离
📖 英文解释
The act of leaving a place, especially to start a journey; a deviation from an accepted or usual course of action.
✏️ 例句
The flight's departure was delayed by two hours due to bad weather.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考搭配：departure from (偏离)；on sb's departure (某人离开之际)；point of departure (出发点/起点)。常与 arrival 对比考查。
🧠 助记
de-(离开) + part(部分/离开) + ure(名词后缀)→ 离开的动作。联想：部门(part)的人离开(de-)就是 departure。
departure::/dɪˈpɑːrtʃər/:n. 离开:The sudden departure from tradition shocked everyone.
2. float /floʊt/ v. 漂浮；浮动 n. 彩车；浮标
📖 英文解释
To rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid without sinking; to move gently through the air.
✏️ 例句
Dead leaves floated on the surface of the pond.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：float a proposal/idea (提出建议)；float on water (漂在水面)；注意作名词时指游行彩车。
🧠 助记
float 和 boat(船)押韵——船浮在水面上，float 就是漂浮。
float::/floʊt/:v. 漂浮:The company decided to float new shares on the stock market.
3. array /əˈreɪ/ n. 系列；大量；排列 v. 排列；布置
📖 英文解释
An impressive display or range of a particular type of thing; to arrange in order.
✏️ 例句
The store offers an array of products to suit every budget.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：a wide/vast array of (大量的)；array of problems/options/choices。注意：in battle array (列阵)；计算机语境中指数组。
🧠 助记
array = a + ray(射线)，一条条射线排成一列→ 排列、系列。
array::/əˈreɪ/:n. 系列:Scientists use an array of instruments to measure climate change.

🧠 助记
en- + gage(抵押) + ment → 互相抵押承诺 → 订婚/约定。engage 动词，加 -ment 变名词。
engagement::/ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt/:n. 订婚:Student engagement in classroom activities improved test scores significantly.

fertile::/ˈfɜːrtl/:adj. 肥沃的:The university proved to be fertile ground for new scientific ideas.

rocket::/ˈrɒkɪt/:n. 火箭 v. 迅速上升:Her career rocketed after she published her first bestselling novel.

alien::/ˈeɪliən/:n. 外星人:Such aggressive behavior was completely alien to his gentle nature.
9. devise /dɪˈvaɪz/ v. 设计；发明；制定（计划）

🧠 助记
de + vise(视) → 仔细看(思考)之后 → 设计出来。联想 vision(视野) → 有远见地设计。
devise::/dɪˈvaɪz/:v. 设计:The team devised an innovative solution to reduce carbon emissions.
10. exaggerate /ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/ v. 夸大；言过其实
📖 英文解释
To represent something as being larger, better, or worse than it really is.
✏️ 例句
He tends to exaggerate his achievements when applying for jobs.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：exaggerate the importance/risk/effect of (夸大重要性/风险/影响)；without exaggeration (毫不夸张地说)；exaggeration n. 夸张；exaggerated adj. 夸大的。
🧠 助记
ex-(超出) + agger(堆积) + ate → 超量堆积 → 夸大。记住 exagger- 这个核心，像把东西堆得过高。
exaggerate::/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/:v. 夸大:It's hard to exaggerate the importance of a good education.
11. inherit /ɪnˈherɪt/ v. 继承；经遗传获得

🧠 助记
in + herit(heir 继承人) → 成为继承人 → 继承。记住 heir(继承人)就记住了 inherit。
inherit::/ɪnˈherɪt/:v. 继承:The new government inherited a massive economic debt from its predecessor.
12. intimate /ˈɪntɪmət/ adj. 亲密的；私密的 n. 密友 v. 暗示

intimate::/ˈɪntɪmət/:adj. 亲密的:She has an intimate knowledge of the company's financial affairs.

16. dilemma /dɪˈlemə/ n. 困境；进退两难的局面
📖 英文解释

✏️ 例句

🧠 助记
di-(两个) + lemma(命题/前提) → 两个命题都成立 → 进退两难。di = 二，牢记！
dilemma::/dɪˈlemə/:n. 困境:The company faces a dilemma between cutting costs and maintaining quality.

spell::/spel/:v. 拼写:Another rise in interest rates could spell disaster for small businesses.

tribe::/traɪb/:n. 部落:Young urban professionals have become a tribe with their own customs and values.

30. distress /dɪˈstres/ n. 忧虑；悲伤；困难 v. 使痛苦；使忧虑
📖 英文解释
Extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain; a situation of danger or difficulty; to cause someone to feel very upset.
✏️ 例句
The loss of her job caused her great financial distress.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考：in distress (处于困难/危险中)；distress signal (求救信号)；emotional/financial distress (情感/经济困难)；distressing adj. 令人痛苦的；distressed adj. 困窘的。
🧠 助记
dis-(不好的) + stress(压力) → 不好的压力 → 痛苦/忧虑。和 stress(压力) 有相同词根。
distress::/dɪˈstres/:n. 忧虑:The charity provides support for families in financial distress.
