1. lure /lʊər/ v./n. 诱惑；吸引；引诱
📖 英文解释
To attract or tempt someone by offering something desirable; something that attracts or tempts.
✏️ 例句
The promise of high salaries lured many graduates into finance.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
lure sb into sth 引诱某人做某事；lure away 诱离；常用于消极语境，表示用利益诱惑
🧠 助记
lure = l + ure，想象一根鱼竿（l形）在水里（ure），用诱饵钓鱼→诱惑
lure::/lʊər/:v./n. 诱惑:The advertisement lured customers with false promises.
2. rigid /ˈrɪdʒɪd/ adj. 僵硬的；严格的；刻板的
📖 英文解释
Unable to bend or be forced out of shape; strict and difficult to change.
✏️ 例句
The rigid rules of the institution left no room for flexibility.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
rigid regulations/rules/structure 严格的规定/刻板的结构；反义词 flexible；考研常考抽象义：思想僵化
🧠 助记
rigid 含 rig（钻井架），钻井架非常坚硬不弯曲→僵硬、严格
rigid::/ˈrɪdʒɪd/:adj. 僵硬的:She was too rigid in her thinking to accept new ideas.
3. offspring /ˈɒfsprɪŋ/ n. 后代；子女；产物
📖 英文解释
A person's child or children; the young of an animal; a result or product of something.
✏️ 例句
The research shows that parental habits heavily influence their offspring.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
offspring 单复数同形；human offspring 人类后代；也可比喻为某事物的产物
🧠 助记
off（从…出来）+ spring（春天/弹出），从父母身上弹出来的→后代
offspring::/ˈɒfsprɪŋ/:n. 后代:The animal protected its offspring from predators.
4. violate /ˈvaɪəleɪt/ v. 违反；侵犯；亵渎
📖 英文解释
To break or fail to comply with a rule or agreement; to fail to respect someone's rights or privacy.
✏️ 例句
The company was fined for violating environmental regulations.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
violate the law/rules/rights/privacy 违法/违规/侵犯权利/侵犯隐私；名词 violation
🧠 助记
viol（小提琴vi-o-l）+ ate，拿小提琴打人→侵犯/违反
violate::/ˈvaɪəleɪt/:v. 违反:Spying on citizens violates their right to privacy.
5. poisonous /ˈpɔɪzənəs/ adj. 有毒的；有害的；恶毒的
📖 英文解释
Containing or producing poison; having a harmful or destructive effect.
✏️ 例句
Some mushrooms are highly poisonous and can cause death if eaten.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
poisonous substance/gas/snake 有毒物质/气体/蛇；比喻义：poisonous relationship 有毒的关系
🧠 助记
poison（毒药）+ ous（形容词后缀）→有毒的
poisonous::/ˈpɔɪzənəs/:adj. 有毒的:The factory released poisonous chemicals into the river.
6. silent /ˈsaɪlənt/ adj. 沉默的；无声的；不说话的
📖 英文解释
Making no sound; not speaking; not expressed in words.
✏️ 例句
The audience fell silent as the speaker took the stage.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
remain/stay silent 保持沉默；silent majority 沉默的大多数；silent letter 不发音的字母
🧠 助记
sil（谐音"悉"）+ ent，把一切都悉知但不说→沉默
silent::/ˈsaɪlənt/:adj. 沉默的:He remained silent throughout the entire trial.
7. thereby /ˌðeəˈbaɪ/ adv. 因此；从而；由此
📖 英文解释
By that means; as a result of that.
✏️ 例句
He worked hard and thereby achieved his goals.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
thereby + doing sth 从而做某事；连接前后因果，常出现在书面语和学术写作中
🧠 助记
there（那里/那样）+ by（通过），通过那种方式→从而、因此
thereby::/ˌðeəˈbaɪ/:adv. 因此:The new policy reduces waste, thereby saving costs.
8. cling /klɪŋ/ v. 紧抓；依附；坚持
📖 英文解释
To hold on tightly to something; to refuse to abandon a belief or habit.
✏️ 例句
She clung to her mother's hand as they crossed the busy street.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
cling to sth/sb 紧抓某物/依附某人；cling to beliefs/ideas 固执地坚持信念；过去式 clung
🧠 助记
cling 发音像"紧"，紧紧抓住→依附、紧抓
cling::/klɪŋ/:v. 紧抓:Some people cling to old habits even when they are harmful.
9. divert /daɪˈvɜːrt/ v. 转移；使转向；娱乐消遣
📖 英文解释
To cause someone or something to change direction; to distract attention; to entertain.
✏️ 例句
Traffic was diverted due to the road construction.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
divert attention/funds/traffic 转移注意力/资金/交通；divert from 从…转移；名词 diversion
🧠 助记
di（=dis 离开）+ vert（转），转离原方向→转移、使转向
divert::/daɪˈvɜːrt/:v. 转移:The government diverted funds from education to defense.
10. fraction /ˈfrækʃn/ n. 分数；小部分；片段
📖 英文解释
A numerical quantity that is not a whole number; a small part or amount of something.
✏️ 例句
Only a fraction of the applicants were accepted into the program.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
a fraction of 一小部分；in fractions 分数形式；fractional adj. 极小的
🧠 助记
fract（破碎，如fracture骨折）+ ion，破碎成小部分→分数/小部分
fraction::/ˈfrækʃn/:n. 分数:The medicine is available at a fraction of the original price.
11. applicable /ˈæplɪkəbl/ adj. 适用的；可应用的；合适的
📖 英文解释
Relevant or appropriate to a particular situation; able to be applied.
✏️ 例句
These rules are applicable to all students regardless of age.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
applicable to sb/sth 对…适用；not applicable (N/A) 不适用；反义词 inapplicable
🧠 助记
apply（应用）→ applicable，能够被应用的→适用的
applicable::/ˈæplɪkəbl/:adj. 适用的:The discount is only applicable to members of the club.
12. bachelor /ˈbætʃələr/ n. 学士；未婚男子；单身汉
📖 英文解释
A person who holds a first degree from a university; an unmarried man.
✏️ 例句
He earned his bachelor's degree in economics from Peking University.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
bachelor's degree 学士学位；bachelor of arts/science 文/理学士；confirmed bachelor 坚定的单身汉
🧠 助记
bach（Bach巴赫）+ elor，巴赫没结婚是单身汉→单身汉/学士
bachelor::/ˈbætʃələr/:n. 学士:She completed her bachelor's degree in three years.
13. carrier /ˈkæriər/ n. 运输者；载体；航空公司；携带者
📖 英文解释
A person or thing that carries something; a company that transports goods or people; a person or animal that transmits a disease without symptoms.
✏️ 例句
The airline carrier announced new routes to Southeast Asia.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
aircraft carrier 航空母舰；carrier of disease 病菌携带者；mail carrier 邮递员
🧠 助记
carry（携带）+ er（人/物），携带东西的人/物→运输者、载体
carrier::/ˈkæriər/:n. 运输者:The patient was identified as a carrier of the virus.
14. hole /həʊl/ n. 洞；漏洞；缺陷 v. 打洞
📖 英文解释
A hollow space in a solid object or surface; a gap or weakness in a plan or argument.
✏️ 例句
The investigator found a significant hole in the suspect's alibi.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
hole in one 一杆进洞；pick holes in 挑剔/找漏洞；in a hole 陷入困境
🧠 助记
hole 和 whole 同音，整体（whole）去掉一部分就变成洞（hole）
hole::/həʊl/:n. 洞:There is a big hole in your argument that needs addressing.
15. initiate /ɪˈnɪʃieɪt/ v. 开始；发起；使入门 n. 新成员
📖 英文解释
To cause something to begin; to introduce someone to a new activity or skill.
✏️ 例句
The government initiated a new program to reduce unemployment.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
initiate a process/project/reform 启动程序/项目/改革；initiate sb into 使某人入门；名词 initiative
🧠 助记
init（初始，如计算机初始化）+ iate，初始化→开始、发起
initiate::/ɪˈnɪʃieɪt/:v. 开始:The company initiated negotiations with potential partners.
16. mate /meɪt/ n. 伴侣；同伴；配偶 v. 交配
📖 英文解释
A friend or companion; a partner in marriage or a sexual relationship; to come together to breed.

🧠 助记
mate 和 match 相关，匹配的人→伴侣、同伴
mate::/meɪt/:n. 伴侣:Penguins are known to mate for life.
17. meaning /ˈmiːnɪŋ/ n. 意义；含义；目的 adj. 意味深长的
📖 英文解释
What is meant by a word, action, or concept; importance or purpose.
✏️ 例句
The meaning of life is a question philosophers have debated for centuries.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
the meaning of sth …的含义；meaningful/meaningless 有/无意义的；double meaning 双关义
🧠 助记
mean（意味）+ ing，表示含有意义的状态→含义、意义
meaning::/ˈmiːnɪŋ/:n. 意义:The poem has a deeper meaning than it first appears.
18. presumably /prɪˈzjuːməbli/ adv. 大概；据推测；可能
📖 英文解释
Used to convey that what is asserted is very likely though not known for certain.
✏️ 例句
He is presumably the best candidate for the position.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
presumably + 完整句子，表示推测；近义词 probably, supposedly；presume v. 假定、推测
🧠 助记
pre（提前）+ sume（assume假设）+ ably，提前假设地→大概、据推测
presumably::/prɪˈzjuːməbli/:adv. 大概:She is presumably still at the office working late.
19. reap /riːp/ v. 收割；获得（回报）；收获
📖 英文解释
To cut or gather a crop; to receive as a consequence of one's own actions.
✏️ 例句
Students who study hard will reap the rewards during exam season.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
reap benefits/rewards/profits 获得利益/回报/利润；reap what one sows 种什么因得什么果
🧠 助记
reap 和 ripe（成熟）相关，庄稼成熟了去收割→收割、收获
reap::/riːp/:v. 收割:The investor reaped huge profits from the early investment.
20. brake /breɪk/ n. 刹车；制动器 v. 刹车；减速
📖 英文解释
A device used to slow down or stop a vehicle; to apply such a device.
✏️ 例句
She slammed on the brakes to avoid hitting the dog.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
apply/put on the brake 刹车；brake system 制动系统；注意与 break 拼写区别
🧠 助记
brake 含 bra（内衣），想象用内衣缠住车轮让车停下→刹车（记忆区别break）
brake::/breɪk/:n. 刹车:The driver braked sharply when a child ran onto the road.
21. calendar /ˈkælɪndər/ n. 日历；历法；日程表

calendar year 历年；academic calendar 学历；social calendar 社交日程

calendar::/ˈkælɪndər/:n. 日历:Mark the important deadlines on your calendar.
22. certificate /səˈtɪfɪkət/ n. 证书；证明书；执照 v. 授予证书
📖 英文解释
An official document attesting a fact, especially birth, marriage, or academic achievement.
✏️ 例句
She received a certificate of completion after finishing the online course.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
birth/death/marriage certificate 出生/死亡/结婚证；certificate of deposit 存款证明；certify v.
🧠 助记
certif（=certify证明）+ icate，能证明的东西→证书
certificate::/səˈtɪfɪkət/:n. 证书:You need a valid certificate to practice medicine.
23. cousin /ˈkʌzn/ n. 堂/表兄弟姐妹；同类事物
📖 英文解释
A child of one's uncle or aunt; a thing related to or resembling another.
✏️ 例句
My cousin and I grew up in the same neighborhood and were very close.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
first cousin 亲表兄妹；second cousin 远亲；country cousin 乡下来的亲戚（比喻土气）

cous（谐音"亲"）+ in，亲里面的人→堂/表兄弟姐妹
cousin::/ˈkʌzn/:n. 堂/表兄弟姐妹:The chimpanzee is often considered our closest evolutionary cousin.
24. dairy /ˈdeəri/ n. 乳制品；乳品场 adj. 乳制品的
📖 英文解释
A place where milk and milk products are produced; relating to milk products.
✏️ 例句
People with lactose intolerance should avoid dairy products.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
dairy product/farm/industry 乳制品/奶牛场/乳业；注意与 diary（日记）区别
🧠 助记
dairy = day + ry，每天（day）产出的东西→乳品（牛每天产奶）；注意与diary区分
dairy::/ˈdeəri/:n. 乳制品:The dairy section of the supermarket includes milk, cheese, and butter.
25. decay /dɪˈkeɪ/ v./n. 腐烂；衰退；衰减
📖 英文解释
To rot or decompose through natural processes; a gradual process of deterioration.
✏️ 例句
Leaving food uncovered in hot weather causes it to decay rapidly.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
moral/urban/tooth decay 道德/城市/牙齿腐蚀；decay rate 衰变率（物理）；in decay 衰败中
🧠 助记
de（向下）+ cay（谐音"凯"倒下），凯撒大帝倒下腐烂→腐烂、衰退
decay::/dɪˈkeɪ/:v./n. 腐烂:Without proper maintenance, old buildings will gradually decay.

grab::/ɡræb/:v. 抓住:The headline grabbed the reader's attention immediately.

qualify::/ˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/:v. 使具备资格:Her international experience qualifies her for the overseas assignment.
