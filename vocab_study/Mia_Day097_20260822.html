1. sole /səʊl/ adj. 唯一的；单独的 n. 脚底；鞋底；鳎鱼
📖 英文解释
One and only; belonging to or restricted to one person or group; the underside of a foot or shoe.
✏️ 例句
She was the sole survivor of the plane crash.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
sole purpose/responsibility/survivor 唯一目的/责任/幸存者；solely adv. 仅仅；注意与soul区别
🧠 助记
sole 和 soul（灵魂）同音，一个人只有一个灵魂→唯一的
sole::/səʊl/:adj. 唯一的:The sole reason for his visit was to collect his documents.
2. spark /spɑːrk/ n. 火花；导火线；活力 v. 引发；激发
📖 英文解释
A small fiery particle; something that triggers a response; to stimulate or trigger something.
✏️ 例句
The documentary sparked a national debate about immigration policy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
spark a debate/controversy/interest 引发辩论/争议/兴趣；spark off 触发；spark of genius 灵感
🧠 助记
sp（喷）+ ark（方舟），喷出来的火花点燃了方舟→火花、引发
spark::/spɑːrk/:n. 火花:The speech sparked widespread protests across the country.
3. sportsman /ˈspɔːrtsmən/ n. 运动员；体育爱好者
📖 英文解释
A person who takes part in sports, especially someone who observes the rules and shows fairness.
✏️ 例句
He was admired as a true sportsman for accepting defeat gracefully.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
sportsmanship n. 运动员精神；a good sportsman 优秀运动员；sportswoman 女运动员
🧠 助记
sports（运动）+ man（人），从事运动的人→运动员
sportsman::/ˈspɔːrtsmən/:n. 运动员:A true sportsman respects both opponents and officials.
4. tissue /ˈtɪʃuː/ n. 组织；薄纸；面巾纸
📖 英文解释
A type of biological material forming body structures; thin soft paper used for wiping.
✏️ 例句
Scientists studied the tissue samples to understand the spread of the disease.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
tissue paper/box 纸巾/纸巾盒；muscle/brain tissue 肌肉/脑组织；tissue of lies 一派谎言
🧠 助记
tissue 谐音"体修"，修复身体的材料→组织（生物）；也像软纸一摩擦
tissue::/ˈtɪʃuː/:n. 组织:The biopsy revealed abnormal tissue growth in the patient.
5. toll /təʊl/ n. 通行费；代价；伤亡人数 v. 鸣响（钟）
📖 英文解释
A charge to use a road or bridge; the number of deaths or casualties; the adverse effect of something.
✏️ 例句
The long war took a heavy toll on the country's economy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
toll road/booth 收费路/收费站；death toll 死亡人数；take a toll on 对…造成损害
🧠 助记
toll 谐音"多了"，过桥要多交钱→通行费；损耗也是多了代价
toll::/təʊl/:n. 通行费:Smoking takes a serious toll on your health over time.
6. verify /ˈverɪfaɪ/ v. 核实；证实；验证
📖 英文解释
To make sure or confirm something is true or accurate by investigation or evidence.
✏️ 例句
The police were unable to verify the suspect's alibi.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
verify information/identity/facts 核实信息/身份/事实；verified account 认证账号；verification n.
🧠 助记
ver（=true真实，如very很）+ ify（使），使变真实→核实、证实
verify::/ˈverɪfaɪ/:v. 核实:Please verify your email address to complete the registration.
7. compulsory /kəmˈpʌlsəri/ adj. 强制的；必须做的；义务的
📖 英文解释
Required by law or a rule; obligatory.
✏️ 例句
Education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 16.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
compulsory education/subject/service 义务教育/必修科目/兵役；反义词 optional/voluntary
🧠 助记
compel（强迫）→ compulsory，被强迫→强制性的、必须做的
compulsory::/kəmˈpʌlsəri/:adj. 强制的:Military service is compulsory for male citizens in some countries.
8. cyberspace /ˈsaɪbəspeɪs/ n. 网络空间；虚拟空间
📖 英文解释
The online world of computer networks and the internet, where information is stored and communication occurs.
✏️ 例句
Governments are increasingly concerned about security threats in cyberspace.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
cyberspace security/crime 网络空间安全/犯罪；in cyberspace 在网络空间；cyber- 前缀表示网络
🧠 助记
cyber（网络/电脑）+ space（空间），网络构成的空间→网络空间
cyberspace::/ˈsaɪbəspeɪs/:n. 网络空间:Personal data is increasingly vulnerable to theft in cyberspace.
9. dawn /dɔːn/ n. 黎明；开端 v. 破晓；开始理解
📖 英文解释
The first light of day; the beginning of something; to begin to become light or to be understood.
✏️ 例句
The rescue team set out at dawn to search for survivors.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
at dawn 黎明时；from dawn to dusk 从早到晚；dawn on sb 被某人开始意识到；the dawn of 的开端
🧠 助记
dawn = day + on，白天开始的时刻→黎明；类比 dusk 黄昏
dawn::/dɔːn/:n. 黎明:It suddenly dawned on her that she had left her keys at home.
10. kick /kɪk/ v./n. 踢；刺激；反冲
📖 英文解释
To strike something with one's foot; a blow with the foot; a thrill or sense of excitement.
✏️ 例句
The project finally kicked off after months of preparation.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
kick off 开始/开球；kick out 踢出/驱逐；get a kick out of 从…得到乐趣；kick-start 启动
🧠 助记
kick 的发音模拟踢的声音，ki-ck 像脚踢出去的感觉→踢
kick::/kɪk/:v./n. 踢:The committee voted to kick him out of the organization.
11. mayor /meər/ n. 市长；镇长
📖 英文解释
The elected head of a city or town.
✏️ 例句
The mayor announced new measures to tackle traffic congestion in the city.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
mayor of a city 某市市长；deputy mayor 副市长；mayoralty n. 市长任期；mayoral adj.
🧠 助记
mayor = major（主要），主要的官员→市长
mayor::/meər/:n. 市长:The mayor pledged to improve public transportation in the city.
12. subjective /səbˈdʒektɪv/ adj. 主观的；个人的；主格的
📖 英文解释
Based on or influenced by personal feelings or opinions rather than facts.
✏️ 例句
Art appreciation is largely subjective and varies from person to person.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
subjective opinion/judgment/experience 主观意见/判断/体验；反义词 objective；subjectivity n.
🧠 助记
subject（主体）+ ive，主体自己决定的→主观的；相对的 object→objective 客观的
subjective::/səbˈdʒektɪv/:adj. 主观的:The assessment of beauty is entirely subjective.
13. timely /ˈtaɪmli/ adj. 及时的；适时的 adv. 及时地
📖 英文解释
Done or occurring at a favorable or useful time; happening at the right moment.
✏️ 例句
The timely intervention of paramedics saved his life.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
timely manner/warning/intervention 及时的方式/警告/干预；in a timely fashion 及时地
🧠 助记
time（时间）+ ly（形容词/副词后缀），在正确时间→及时的
timely::/ˈtaɪmli/:adj. 及时的:A timely reminder helped them avoid the costly mistake.
14. ward /wɔːrd/ n. 病房；选区；监护人/被监护人 v. 避开
📖 英文解释
A room in a hospital; a division of a city for electoral purposes; to guard or protect.
✏️ 例句
The patient was moved to the intensive care ward after surgery.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
hospital ward 医院病房；ward off 抵御/避开；ward of court 法院受监护人；warden n. 监护人
🧠 助记
ward 和 guard 相关，ward off → guard off，守卫抵御→病房（守护病人的地方）
ward::/wɔːrd/:n. 病房:She was placed in the maternity ward after giving birth.
15. wrap /ræp/ v. 包裹；包装；缠绕 n. 包装纸；披肩
📖 英文解释
To cover something by folding paper or other material around it; a piece of material used to cover something.
✏️ 例句
She wrapped the gift carefully before placing it under the tree.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
wrap up 包好/结束/穿暖；gift wrap 礼品包装；wrapped up in 全神贯注于；under wraps 保密
🧠 助记
wrap 和 lap（膝盖）押韵，把东西放在膝盖上绕着包→包裹
wrap::/ræp/:v. 包裹:Let's wrap up the meeting and summarize the key points.
16. altitude /ˈæltɪtjuːd/ n. 高度；海拔；高处
📖 英文解释
The height of something above sea level or above the ground.
✏️ 例句
The plane was flying at an altitude of 35,000 feet.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
altitude sickness 高原反应；high/low altitude 高/低海拔；gain/lose altitude 上升/下降高度
🧠 助记
alt（高，如alto高音）+ itude（名词后缀），高的状态→高度、海拔
altitude::/ˈæltɪtjuːd/:n. 高度:Mountain climbers must acclimatize to the high altitude.
17. cattle /ˈkætl/ n. 牛（总称）；牲畜
📖 英文解释
Large domesticated bovine animals raised for beef or dairy; used collectively.
✏️ 例句
The farmer raised cattle on his large ranch in the countryside.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
cattle ranch/farm/market 牧场/牛场/牲畜市场；treat sb like cattle 像牲口一样对待某人
🧠 助记
cattle 含 cat，想象猫在管牛群→牛（总称）；注意无单数形式
cattle::/ˈkætl/:n. 牛（总称）:Hundreds of cattle were lost during the severe drought.
18. cottage /ˈkɒtɪdʒ/ n. 小屋；村舍；度假屋
📖 英文解释
A small house, typically in the country; a modest-sized house used for vacations.

They rented a cozy cottage by the lake for their summer vacation.

cottage industry 家庭手工业；country cottage 乡村小屋；cottage cheese 农家奶酪
🧠 助记
cottage = cot（小床）+ tage，放着小床的小屋→村舍、小屋
cottage::/ˈkɒtɪdʒ/:n. 小屋:The family retreated to their countryside cottage every summer.
19. drown /draʊn/ v. 淹死；溺水；淹没（声音）

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
drown in 淹没于；drown out 盖过（声音）；drown one's sorrows 借酒浇愁；drowning adj.
🧠 助记
drown = d +rown（皇冠），国王皇冠掉水里去捞→淹死
drown::/draʊn/:v. 淹死:She was drowning in work and barely had time to eat.
20. empire /ˈempaɪər/ n. 帝国；庞大的企业王国
📖 英文解释
A group of countries or territories ruled by one powerful authority; a large commercial organization.
✏️ 例句
The Roman Empire once stretched across most of Europe and North Africa.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
Roman/British Empire 罗马/英帝国；business empire 商业帝国；imperial adj. 帝国的
🧠 助记
empire = em（在内）+ pire（=fire 火），用火势控制的领土→帝国
empire::/ˈempaɪər/:n. 帝国:He built a media empire worth billions of dollars.
21. ignorance /ˈɪɡnərəns/ n. 无知；愚昧；不了解
📖 英文解释
Lack of knowledge or information; the state of being uninformed.

Ignorance of the law is not an acceptable excuse for breaking it.

in ignorance of 不了解；ignorance is bliss 无知是福；out of ignorance 出于无知；ignorant adj.
🧠 助记

ignorance::/ˈɪɡnərəns/:n. 无知:His ignorance of basic safety rules put others at risk.
22. landlord /ˈlændlɔːrd/ n. 房东；地主；客栈老板
📖 英文解释
A person who rents land, a building, or an apartment to a tenant; an owner of an inn or pub.
✏️ 例句
The landlord agreed to repair the heating system before winter.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
landlord-tenant relationship 房东租客关系；landlady 女房东；absentee landlord 不在场地主
🧠 助记
land（土地）+ lord（主人），土地的主人→地主、房东
landlord::/ˈlændlɔːrd/:n. 房东:The landlord raised the rent by 15% at the start of the year.
23. precaution /prɪˈkɔːʃn/ n. 预防措施；谨慎；防范
📖 英文解释
A measure taken in advance to prevent possible dangers or problems.
✏️ 例句
As a precaution, all visitors to the lab must wear protective goggles.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
take precautions 采取预防措施；as a precaution 作为预防；precautionary adj. 预防性的
🧠 助记
pre（提前）+ caution（小心），提前小心→预防措施
precaution::/prɪˈkɔːʃn/:n. 预防措施:Wearing sunscreen is a simple precaution against skin cancer.
24. prosperous /ˈprɒspərəs/ adj. 繁荣的；兴旺的；富裕的
📖 英文解释
Successful in material terms; flourishing financially.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
prosperous economy/nation/business 繁荣的经济/国家/企业；prosper v.；prosperity n.
🧠 助记
prosper（繁荣）+ ous（形容词后缀），充满繁荣→繁荣的
prosperous::/ˈprɒspərəs/:adj. 繁荣的:The small town grew into a prosperous city over two decades.
25. tense /tens/ adj. 紧张的；绷紧的 n. 时态 v. 拉紧
📖 英文解释
Feeling, causing, or showing anxiety and nervousness; stretched tight; a form of a verb showing time of action.
✏️ 例句
The atmosphere in the negotiation room was extremely tense.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
tense situation/atmosphere/muscle 紧张局势/氛围/肌肉；in the past/present tense 过去/现在时态
🧠 助记
tense 和 tension（紧张）同源，绷紧的状态→紧张的；语法上绷紧时间→时态
tense::/tens/:adj. 紧张的:Relations between the two countries remain tense.
26. versus /ˈvɜːrsəs/ prep. 对；与…相对；与…相比

versus = ver（转向）+ sus，转向对立面→对抗、与…相比
versus::/ˈvɜːrsəs/:prep. 对:The trial was a landmark case: the state versus the corporation.

allocate::/ˈæləkeɪt/:v. 分配:Each department was allocated a specific budget for the year.
