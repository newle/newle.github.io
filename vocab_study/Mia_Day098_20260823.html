1. mess /mes/ n. 混乱；脏乱 v. 弄乱；胡弄
📖 英文解释
A dirty or untidy state; a situation that is full of problems; to make something untidy.
✏️ 例句
The children made a mess of the kitchen while cooking.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
make a mess 弄乱；mess up 搞砸/弄乱；in a mess 一团糟；mess around 瞎混/乱搞
🧠 助记
mess 谐音"没事"，没事干就乱搞→混乱、脏乱
mess::/mes/:n. 混乱:Don't mess up your chances by being late to the interview.
2. troublesome /ˈtrʌblsəm/ adj. 令人烦恼的；麻烦的；困难的
📖 英文解释
Causing difficulty or annoyance; hard to deal with.
✏️ 例句
The troublesome student constantly disrupted the classroom.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
troublesome problem/behavior/symptom 棘手的问题/麻烦的行为/令人烦恼的症状
🧠 助记
trouble（麻烦）+ some（充满…的），充满麻烦的→令人烦恼的
troublesome::/ˈtrʌblsəm/:adj. 令人烦恼的:The most troublesome aspect of the project was coordinating schedules.
3. delegate /ˈdelɪɡət/ (n.) /ˈdelɪɡeɪt/ (v.) n. 代表；代理人 v. 委派；授权
📖 英文解释
A person sent to represent others; to entrust a task or authority to someone else.
✏️ 例句
The manager learned to delegate responsibilities to her team members.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
delegate authority/power/tasks 授权/下放权力/委派任务；a delegate to a conference 会议代表
🧠 助记
de（下）+ legate（使节/代理），派下去的使节→代表；向下授权→委派
delegate::/ˈdelɪɡət/ (n.) /ˈdelɪɡeɪt/ (v.):n. 代表:Effective leaders know when to delegate and when to act directly.
4. dive /daɪv/ v./n. 俯冲；潜水；猛冲
📖 英文解释
To plunge headfirst into water; to move downward sharply; to rush into something.
✏️ 例句
She dived into the pool and swam to the other end.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
dive into 投入（工作）；nosedive 急剧下降；dive for cover 扑向掩护物；deep-sea diving 深海潜水
🧠 助记
dive 和 drive 形似，开车（drive）vs 跳水（dive），头朝下冲入→潜水
dive::/daɪv/:v./n. 俯冲:The researcher dived deep into the historical archives.
5. hop /hɒp/ v./n. 跳；单脚跳；短途旅行
📖 英文解释
To move by jumping on one foot; to make a quick trip; a short journey.
✏️ 例句
The students hopped on the bus to get to the museum.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
hop on/off 跳上/下；island hop 逐岛游；hip-hop 嘻哈音乐；bar-hopping 轧吧
🧠 助记
hop 像青蛙跳的声音，蹦蹦跳→单脚跳、短途旅行
hop::/hɒp/:v./n. 跳:We hopped from one coffee shop to another on a rainy afternoon.

📖 英文解释
A school or class for young children, usually between the ages of 4 and 6.
✏️ 例句
Her daughter started kindergarten this September.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
kindergarten teacher/class/student 幼儿园老师/班级/学生；attend/enroll in kindergarten 上幼儿园
🧠 助记
kinder（德语=children儿童）+ garten（德语=garden花园），儿童花园→幼儿园（德语原词）
kindergarten::/ˈkɪndərɡɑːrtn/:n. 幼儿园:The kindergarten program focuses on play-based learning.
7. litter /ˈlɪtər/ n. 垃圾；一窝（幼崽）v. 乱扔垃圾
📖 英文解释
Garbage left in a public place; a number of young animals born at the same time; to leave rubbish in a public area.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

litter 含 little，小小的垃圾散落各地→垃圾；小动物生一窝小的→一窝幼崽
litter::/ˈlɪtər/:n. 垃圾:Littering in public areas carries a heavy fine in Singapore.
8. passport /ˈpɑːspɔːrt/ n. 护照；通行证；手段

passport::/ˈpɑːspɔːrt/:n. 护照:Hard work is your passport to achieving your dreams.
9. pat /pæt/ v./n. 轻拍；拍打 adj. 恰好合适的
📖 英文解释

pat::/pæt/:v./n. 轻拍:The coach gave each player a pat on the back after the game.
10. prospective /prəˈspektɪv/ adj. 预期的；未来的；潜在的

uncle::/ˈʌŋkl/:n. 叔叔:His uncle helped him find his first job in the city.
