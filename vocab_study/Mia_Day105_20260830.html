1. metropolitan /ˌmetrəˈpɒlɪtən/ adj. 大都市的；首都的
📖 英文解释
Relating to a large, densely populated city or its surrounding area; characteristic of a major city.
✏️ 例句
The metropolitan area includes the city and its surrounding suburbs.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
metropolitan area 大都市区；metropolitan city 特大城市；常考搭配：metropolitan government 都市政府
🧠 助记
metro（地铁）+ politan（城市）→ 有地铁的大城市 → 大都市的
metropolitan::/ˌmetrəˈpɒlɪtən/:adj. 大都市的:The metropolitan area has a population of over 10 million.
2. restraint /rɪˈstreɪnt/ n. 克制；限制；束缚
📖 英文解释
The action of keeping someone or something under control; the quality of being controlled or moderate.
✏️ 例句
She showed great restraint in not responding to his insults.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
show restraint 表现克制；without restraint 毫无节制；self-restraint 自我克制；考研常考：restraint on/upon sth 对…的限制
🧠 助记
re-（再）+ strain（拉紧）→ 再次拉紧 → 克制、限制
restraint::/rɪˈstreɪnt/:n. 克制:He exercised restraint and avoided conflict.
3. accordingly /əˈkɔːdɪŋli/ adv. 相应地；因此；于是
📖 英文解释
In a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances; as a result; consequently.
✏️ 例句
The rules changed, and we adjusted our plans accordingly.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常作连接副词表因果，类似 therefore/thus；accordingly + 句子 表因此做某事
🧠 助记
accord（一致）+ -ing + -ly → 与之一致地 → 相应地
accordingly::/əˈkɔːdɪŋli/:adv. 相应地:The situation changed, and she acted accordingly.
4. drawing /ˈdrɔːɪŋ/ n. 绘图；素描；图纸
📖 英文解释
A picture or diagram made with a pencil, pen, or crayon; the art or skill of making such pictures.
✏️ 例句
Her drawing of the landscape was remarkably detailed.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
drawing board 绘图板；back to the drawing board 重头再来；technical drawing 工程制图
🧠 助记
draw（画）+ -ing → 正在画 → 绘图、素描
drawing::/ˈdrɔːɪŋ/:n. 绘图:He made a careful drawing of the machine parts.
5. eighty /ˈeɪti/ num. 八十
📖 英文解释
The cardinal number equal to eight times ten (80).
✏️ 例句
My grandfather is eighty years old this year.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in one's eighties 在某人八十多岁时；the eighties 八十年代；eighty percent 百分之八十
🧠 助记
eight（八）+ -ty（表十倍）→ 八十
eighty::/ˈeɪti/:num. 八十:There were eighty students in the auditorium.
6. inertia /ɪˈnɜːʃə/ n. 惯性；惰性；不活动
📖 英文解释
A tendency to do nothing or to remain unchanged; (in physics) a property of matter by which it continues in its state of rest or motion.
✏️ 例句
Organizational inertia makes it difficult to implement changes.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
由于惰性 due to inertia；克服惰性 overcome inertia；物理：law of inertia 惯性定律
🧠 助记
in-（不）+ ert（活动）+ -ia → 不活动 → 惰性、惯性
inertia::/ɪˈnɜːʃə/:n. 惯性:The project stalled due to bureaucratic inertia.
7. lately /ˈleɪtli/ adv. 最近；近来
📖 英文解释
Recently; in the recent past; not long ago.
✏️ 例句
Have you been following the news lately?
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常与完成时连用；lately = recently；区别：late（晚的/迟到的）vs lately（最近）
🧠 助记
late（晚、近）+ -ly → 最近（在晚近的时间里）
lately::/ˈleɪtli/:adv. 最近:She has been feeling tired lately.
8. lump /lʌmp/ n. 块；肿块 v. 混为一谈
📖 英文解释
A compact mass of a substance; a swelling under the skin; to put together indiscriminately.
✏️ 例句
He felt a lump in his throat when he heard the news.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
lump sum 一次性总付；a lump of sugar 一块方糖；lump together 混为一谈
🧠 助记
形似一块隆起 → 块、肿块
lump::/lʌmp/:n. 块:She received a lump sum payment after retiring.
9. ninety /ˈnaɪnti/ num. 九十
📖 英文解释
The cardinal number equal to nine times ten (90).
✏️ 例句
The temperature reached ninety degrees Fahrenheit yesterday.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in the nineties 在九十年代；ninety percent 百分之九十；the Nineties 九十年代
🧠 助记
nine（九）+ -ty（十倍）→ 九十
ninety::/ˈnaɪnti/:num. 九十:Ninety percent of students passed the exam.
10. plentiful /ˈplentɪfl/ adj. 丰富的；充足的
📖 英文解释
Existing in or yielding great quantities; more than enough; abundant.
✏️ 例句
Food was plentiful during the harvest season.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
plentiful supply 充足的供应；plentiful resources 丰富的资源；同义词：abundant, ample
🧠 助记
plenty（大量）+ -ful → 充满大量的 → 丰富的
plentiful::/ˈplentɪfl/:adj. 丰富的:The region has plentiful rainfall throughout the year.
11. revolve /rɪˈvɒlv/ v. 旋转；围绕；反复思考
📖 英文解释
To move in a circle around a central point; to consider a problem from various points of view.
✏️ 例句
The Earth revolves around the Sun once a year.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
revolve around 以…为中心；revolve on one's axis 绕轴旋转；考研辨析：revolve vs rotate（自转）
🧠 助记
re-（反复）+ volve（滚动）→ 反复滚动 → 旋转、围绕
revolve::/rɪˈvɒlv/:v. 旋转:Her life revolved around her children's education.
12. theirs /ðeəz/ pron. 他们的（所有物）
📖 英文解释
Used to refer to something belonging to or associated with two or more people previously mentioned.
✏️ 例句
Our house is smaller than theirs.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
绝对所有格，不加名词；区别：their（形容词性所有格）vs theirs（名词性）
🧠 助记
their（他们的）+ s → 他们的东西
theirs::/ðeəz/:pron. 他们的（所有物）:The decision was theirs to make.
13. harmony /ˈhɑːməni/ n. 和谐；协调；和声
📖 英文解释
The quality of forming a pleasing and consistent whole; agreement of feelings, opinions, or actions.
✏️ 例句
The two cultures lived in harmony for centuries.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in harmony with 与…和谐；social harmony 社会和谐；harmony of interests 利益协调；考研高频词
🧠 助记
harm（伤害）的反面 + ony → 没有伤害 → 和谐
harmony::/ˈhɑːməni/:n. 和谐:They worked in perfect harmony to complete the project.
14. breakdown /ˈbreɪkdaʊn/ n. 崩溃；故障；分类统计
📖 英文解释
A failure of a relationship, system, or machine; an analysis of statistics or information.
✏️ 例句
The breakdown of negotiations led to a trade war.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
nervous breakdown 精神崩溃；breakdown of communication 沟通失败；a breakdown of costs 费用明细
🧠 助记
break（断裂）+ down（向下）→ 向下断裂 → 崩溃、故障
breakdown::/ˈbreɪkdaʊn/:n. 崩溃:There was a complete breakdown in communication between the teams.
15. dash /dæʃ/ v./n. 猛冲；破折号；少量
📖 英文解释
To move or travel in a great hurry; a short rapid movement; a punctuation mark (—).
✏️ 例句
She dashed out of the office to catch the bus.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
make a dash for 冲向；dash off 匆忙写完；100-meter dash 100米短跑；a dash of salt 一点盐
🧠 助记
发音像快冲时的「哒」声 → 猛冲
dash::/dæʃ/:v./n. 猛冲:He made a dash for the exit when the alarm rang.
16. flee /fliː/ v. 逃跑；逃离
📖 英文解释
To run away from a place or situation of danger; to escape by running.
✏️ 例句
Thousands of refugees fled the war-torn country.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
flee from 逃离；flee to safety 逃到安全地带；过去式：fled；考研：flee vs fly（fly无逃的含义）
🧠 助记
和fly（飞）发音相近 → 像鸟一样飞逃 → 逃跑
flee::/fliː/:v. 逃跑:The villagers were forced to flee their homes.
17. humorous /ˈhjuːmərəs/ adj. 幽默的；诙谐的

🧠 助记

humorous::/ˈhjuːmərəs/:adj. 幽默的:The professor's humorous examples made the lecture enjoyable.

magnify::/ˈmæɡnɪfaɪ/:v. 放大:The media tended to magnify the scandal unnecessarily.
