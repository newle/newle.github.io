1. crew /kruː/ n. 全体船员；机组人员；小组 v. 充当机组成员
📖 英文解释
A group of people who work together on a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle; a group of people who work together on a task.
✏️ 例句
The crew of the spacecraft trained for two years before the mission.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
film crew 摄制组；crew member 成员；ground crew 地勤人员；flight crew 飞行机组
🧠 助记
crew = 齐心协力（Create Real Energy Working）的团队
crew::/kruː/:n. 全体船员:The rescue crew worked through the night to find survivors.
2. envy /ˈenvi/ n./v. 嫉妒；羡慕
📖 英文解释
A feeling of discontented longing for someone else's possessions or qualities; to feel envy toward someone.
✏️ 例句
She looked at her colleague's promotion with undisguised envy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
green with envy 非常嫉妒；envy sb sth 嫉妒某人某物；out of envy 出于嫉妒；envious adj. 嫉妒的
🧠 助记
en-（使）+ vy（vie，竞争）→ 使人竞争攀比 → 嫉妒
envy::/ˈenvi/:n./v. 嫉妒:He envied his friend's ability to speak five languages.
3. illuminate /ɪˈluːmɪneɪt/ v. 照明；阐明；启迪
📖 英文解释
To light up an area; to make something clearer and easier to understand; to enlighten.
✏️ 例句
The lecture illuminated several key aspects of the problem.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
illuminate a problem 阐明问题；illuminating adj. 有启发性的；illuminate + 名词：illuminate the truth/issue
🧠 助记
il-（使）+ lumin（光）+ ate → 使发光 → 照明、阐明
illuminate::/ɪˈluːmɪneɪt/:v. 照明:The documentary illuminated the harsh realities of poverty.
4. metric /ˈmetrɪk/ adj. 公制的；度量的 n. 度量标准
📖 英文解释
Relating to the metric system; a set of measurements used to evaluate performance.
✏️ 例句
The company uses several key metrics to measure success.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
metric system 公制；metric ton 公吨；performance metric 绩效指标；key metric 关键指标
🧠 助记
metre（米，公制单位）→ metric（公制的）
metric::/ˈmetrɪk/:adj. 公制的:Customer satisfaction is one of our most important metrics.
5. molecule /ˈmɒlɪkjuːl/ n. 分子
📖 英文解释
The smallest unit of a chemical compound that can take part in a chemical reaction, consisting of two or more atoms.
✏️ 例句
A water molecule consists of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
molecule of water 水分子；molecular adj. 分子的；molecular biology 分子生物学
🧠 助记
mol（克分子）+ ecule（小）→ 很小的克分子单位 → 分子
molecule::/ˈmɒlɪkjuːl/:n. 分子:Scientists studied the molecule's structure under the electron microscope.
6. noisy /ˈnɔɪzi/ adj. 嘈杂的；吵闹的
📖 英文解释
Making a lot of noise; full of noise; loud and disturbing.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
noise（噪声）+ y → 充满噪声的 → 嘈杂的
noisy::/ˈnɔɪzi/:adj. 嘈杂的:The children became noisy and restless during the long wait.
7. particle /ˈpɑːtɪkl/ n. 粒子；微粒；小品词
📖 英文解释
A minute portion of matter; the least possible amount; a minor function word in grammar.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
elementary particle 基本粒子；particle physics 粒子物理学；not a particle of doubt 毫无疑问
🧠 助记
part（部分）+ icle（小）→ 极小的部分 → 粒子、微粒
particle::/ˈpɑːtɪkl/:n. 粒子:Dust particles filled the air after the explosion.
8. residence /ˈrezɪdəns/ n. 住宅；居住；居留权

✏️ 例句

🧠 助记
resid-（住）+ ence → 住的地方 → 住宅、居所
residence::/ˈrezɪdəns/:n. 住宅:The prime minister's official residence is located in the capital.
9. stain /steɪn/ n./v. 污点；着色；污染
📖 英文解释
A colored patch or mark that is difficult to remove; damage to someone's reputation; to mark with a stain.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
stain = 留下的痕迹（stays in）→ 污点、着色
stain::/steɪn/:n./v. 污点:The scandal left a permanent stain on his reputation.
10. tangible /ˈtændʒɪbl/ adj. 有形的；切实的；可触摸的
📖 英文解释

✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
tang-（触摸）+ ible → 可触摸的 → 有形的、切实的
tangible::/ˈtændʒɪbl/:adj. 有形的:The training program showed tangible improvements in employee performance.

chaos = 吃（chao）→ 乱吃的状态 → 混乱
chaos::/ˈkeɪɒs/:n. 混乱:The announcement caused chaos at the stock exchange.

13. harness /ˈhɑːnɪs/ v. 利用；控制 n. 马具；安全带

14. legend /ˈledʒənd/ n. 传说；传奇人物；图例

premise::/ˈpremɪs/:n. 前提:His plan was built on the false premise that costs would remain stable.

deputy::/ˈdepjəti/:n./adj. 副手:The deputy manager handled all affairs while the boss was away.
