1. certainty /ˈsɜːtnti/ n. 确定；必然的事；把握
📖 英文解释
the quality of being reliably true; a fact that is definitely true or an event that will definitely happen
✏️ 例句
There is no certainty that the project will be completed on time.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
with certainty（确定地）；a certainty（必然的事）；for a certainty（确实地）；certain（adj.）→ certainty（n.）
🧠 助记
certain（确定的）+ ty → 确定性
certainty::/ˈsɜːtnti/:n. 确定:We cannot predict the future with absolute certainty.
2. clause /klɔːz/ n. 条款；从句
📖 英文解释
a distinct article or provision in a contract, treaty, or law; a unit of grammatical organization
✏️ 例句
A confidentiality clause was included in the employment contract.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
escape clause（免责条款）；main clause / subordinate clause（主句/从句，语法）；考研法律、合同类阅读
🧠 助记
close（关闭）→ clause 是合同中一个封闭的条款单元
clause::/klɔːz/:n. 条款:The contract contained a clause about intellectual property rights.
3. curse /kɜːs/ n. 诅咒；祸根 v. 诅咒；骂人
📖 英文解释
a solemn utterance intended to invoke a supernatural power to inflict harm; a cause of harm or misery
✏️ 例句
He cursed himself for forgetting the important meeting.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
curse sb/sth；be under a curse（受诅咒）；curse of（...的祸根）；cursed（adj.）
🧠 助记
curse 发音类似 cross（交叉/愤怒）→ 愤怒地诅咒
curse::/kɜːs/:n. 诅咒:She considered the unexpected rain a curse on the wedding day.
4. disguise /dɪsˈɡaɪz/ v. 伪装；掩饰 n. 伪装；变装
📖 英文解释
to give someone or something a false appearance; a means of changing one's appearance to conceal identity
✏️ 例句
The spy disguised himself as a delivery driver.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
in disguise（伪装成）；a blessing in disguise（因祸得福）；disguise one's feelings（掩饰情感）
🧠 助记
dis（去除）+ guise（外表）→ 去除真实外表 = 伪装
disguise::/dɪsˈɡaɪz/:v. 伪装:The setback turned out to be a blessing in disguise.
5. doorway /ˈdɔːweɪ/ n. 门口；门道；途径
📖 英文解释
an opening in a wall in which a door is fitted; a means of access to something
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
stand in the doorway（站在门口）；a doorway to success（成功的途径）；doorway vs gateway（门口 vs 大门/通道）
🧠 助记
door（门）+ way（路）→ 门的通道 = 门口
doorway::/ˈdɔːweɪ/:n. 门口:Education is a doorway to better opportunities.
6. faithful /ˈfeɪθfl/ adj. 忠实的；如实的；虔诚的
📖 英文解释
remaining loyal and steadfast; true to the facts; having strong religious belief
✏️ 例句
The film remained faithful to the original novel.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
faithful to sb/sth；faithfully（adv.）；the faithful（n. 信徒）；faithful vs loyal（忠实 vs 忠诚，faithful 强调承诺）
🧠 助记
faith（信念/信任）+ ful → 充满信念的 = 忠实的
faithful::/ˈfeɪθfl/:adj. 忠实的:The translator tried to be faithful to the author's original meaning.
7. ghost /ɡəʊst/ n. 鬼魂；幽灵 v. 为...代笔
📖 英文解释
the soul of a dead person believed to appear to the living; a trace or vestige; to ghostwrite
✏️ 例句

🧠 助记
ghost 联想幽灵，发音时有气音，像鬼的叹气声
ghost::/ɡəʊst/:n. 鬼魂:The abandoned factory had the atmosphere of a ghost town.
8. greedy /ˈɡriːdi/ adj. 贪婪的；贪吃的
📖 英文解释

🧠 助记
greed（贪婪）+ y → 充满贪婪的
greedy::/ˈɡriːdi/:adj. 贪婪的:The greedy investor tried to take over the small company.
9. illustration /ˌɪləˈstreɪʃn/ n. 插图；说明；例证
📖 英文解释

illustration::/ˌɪləˈstreɪʃn/:n. 插图:Her speech was full of vivid illustrations from real life.
10. imaginary /ɪˈmædʒɪnəri/ adj. 虚构的；想象中的；假想的

🧠 助记
imagine（想象）+ ary → 只存在于想象中的
imaginary::/ɪˈmædʒɪnəri/:adj. 虚构的:The story takes place in an imaginary kingdom called Avalon.
11. marginal /ˈmɑːdʒɪnl/ adj. 微小的；边际的；边缘的
📖 英文解释

🧠 助记
margin（边缘）+ al → 处于边缘的 = 微小的、边际的
marginal::/ˈmɑːdʒɪnl/:adj. 微小的:The profit margin showed only a marginal increase this quarter.

obsession::/əbˈseʃn/:n. 困扰:His obsession with work left no time for family.
