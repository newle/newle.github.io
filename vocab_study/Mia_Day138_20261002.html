1. relativity /ˌreləˈtɪvəti/ n. 相对论；相对性
📖 英文解释
The quality of being relative; the theory in physics (especially Einstein's) that space, time, and motion are relative to the observer.
✏️ 例句
Einstein's theory of relativity changed our understanding of space and time.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
theory of relativity 相对论；cultural relativity 文化相对主义；常考搭配：special/general relativity
🧠 助记
relative（相对的）+ -ity（名词后缀）→ 相对性、相对论
relativity::/ˌreləˈtɪvəti/:n. 相对论:Einstein's theory of relativity reshaped modern physics.
2. removal /rɪˈmuːvl/ n. 除去；搬迁；撤职
📖 英文解释
The act of taking something away from a place or position; the process of eliminating something.
✏️ 例句
The removal of toxic waste from the site took several weeks.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
removal of 去除……；removal from office 免职；常考：surgical removal 手术切除
🧠 助记
re-（反）+ move（移动）+ -al → 移走 → 除去、搬迁
removal::/rɪˈmuːvl/:n. 除去:The removal of obstacles is essential for progress.
3. rifle /ˈraɪfl/ n. 步枪 v. 搜查、翻找
📖 英文解释
A gun with a long barrel designed to be fired from the shoulder; also to search through something quickly looking for valuables.
✏️ 例句
The soldier carried a rifle on his shoulder during patrol.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
rifle through 翻寻（搜查）；sniper rifle 狙击步枪；考研中多作名词
🧠 助记
联想 rife（充斥）→ 战争中步枪充斥 → rifle 步枪
rifle::/ˈraɪfl/:n. 步枪 v. 搜查、翻找:The hunter raised his rifle and aimed carefully.
4. sincere /sɪnˈsɪər/ adj. 真诚的；真实的
📖 英文解释
Free from pretense or deceit; genuinely felt or expressed; honest.
✏️ 例句
She gave a sincere apology for her mistake.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
sincere apology/effort/gratitude 真诚的道歉/努力/感谢；sincerely adv.（书信结尾）
🧠 助记
sin（无）+ cere（蜡）→ 古罗马雕像无蜡遮瑕 → 真实无伪 → 真诚
sincere::/sɪnˈsɪər/:adj. 真诚的:He made a sincere effort to improve his relationship with his team.
5. undo /ʌnˈduː/ v. 取消；解开；使失败
📖 英文解释
To cancel or reverse an action; to unfasten or loosen; to ruin someone's plans or reputation.
✏️ 例句
You can undo the last action by pressing Ctrl+Z.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
undo damage/harm 消除损害；undo a button/knot 解开扣子/结；考研中常考"取消"义
🧠 助记
un-（反）+ do（做）→ 反做 → 取消、撤销
undo::/ʌnˈduː/:v. 取消:Ctrl+Z will undo your last edit in most text editors.
6. alienate /ˈeɪliəneɪt/ v. 疏远；使感到隔阂
📖 英文解释
To cause someone to feel isolated or estranged; to make someone feel no longer friendly or welcome.
✏️ 例句
His aggressive behavior alienated many of his colleagues.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
alienate sb from sth/sb 使某人疏远……；feel alienated 感到疏离；alienation n. 异化
🧠 助记
alien（外星人、陌生人）+ -ate → 使变得像外星人一样陌生 → 疏远
alienate::/ˈeɪliəneɪt/:v. 疏远:Poor leadership can alienate talented employees from the organization.
7. ascend /əˈsend/ v. 上升；攀登；登位
📖 英文解释
To go up; to climb or move upward; to rise to a higher position or level.
✏️ 例句
The hikers ascended the mountain before dawn.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
ascend to power/throne 登上权力/王位；ascending order 升序；ascent n. 上升
🧠 助记
a-（向）+ scend（=climb爬）→ 向上爬 → 上升、攀登
ascend::/əˈsend/:v. 上升:The team began to ascend the steep slope as the sun rose.
8. ascertain /ˌæsəˈteɪn/ v. 确定；查明；弄清
📖 英文解释
To find out for certain; to discover or establish with confidence.
✏️ 例句
Investigators tried to ascertain the cause of the accident.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
ascertain the truth/cause/facts 查明真相/原因/事实；ascertain that… 确认……
🧠 助记
a（加强）+ certain（确定的）→ 使确定 → 查明、弄清
ascertain::/ˌæsəˈteɪn/:v. 确定:Police worked to ascertain the facts before making an arrest.
9. astonish /əˈstɒnɪʃ/ v. 使吃惊；使惊讶
📖 英文解释
To surprise or impress someone greatly; to fill with amazement.
✏️ 例句
The magician's performance astonished the entire audience.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
astonish sb 使某人惊讶；be astonished at/by 对……感到惊讶；astonishing adj. 令人吃惊的
🧠 助记
a-（加强）+ ston（石头）+ -ish → 像石头砸中般震惊 → 使吃惊
astonish::/əˈstɒnɪʃ/:v. 使吃惊:Her rapid recovery from surgery astonished the medical team.
10. banner /ˈbænər/ n. 旗帜；横幅；标语 adj. 出色的
📖 英文解释
A long strip of cloth bearing a slogan or design; a flag; also used figuratively for a cause or movement.
✏️ 例句
Protesters carried banners calling for environmental protection.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
under the banner of 打着……的旗号；banner year 丰收年/出色的一年
🧠 助记
联想 ban（禁止）+ ner → 举横幅禁止某事 → 旗帜、横幅
banner::/ˈbænər/:n. 旗帜:The activists marched under a banner calling for climate action.
11. baseball /ˈbeɪsbɔːl/ n. 棒球
📖 英文解释
A ball game played between two teams of nine players on a diamond-shaped field, using a bat and ball.
✏️ 例句
Baseball is one of the most popular sports in the United States.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
baseball cap 棒球帽；play baseball 打棒球；baseball bat 棒球棒
🧠 助记
base（基地）+ ball（球）→ 以垒为基础的球类运动 → 棒球
baseball::/ˈbeɪsbɔːl/:n. 棒球:He wore a baseball cap during the afternoon game.
12. bolt /bəʊlt/ n. 螺栓；门闩；闪电 v. 飞奔；插上门闩
📖 英文解释
A metal pin or bar used to fasten things; a lightning flash; to run or move very fast; to lock a door with a bolt.
✏️ 例句
He bolted the door before going to sleep.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
bolt of lightning 闪电；bolt the door 插门；a bolt from the blue 晴天霹雳
🧠 助记
联想 bolt（螺栓插入）→ bolt the door 插门；bolt out 飞奔而出
bolt::/bəʊlt/:n. 螺栓:A bolt of lightning struck the old oak tree during the storm.
13. bowl /bəʊl/ n. 碗；盆；碗状物 v. 投球
📖 英文解释
A round deep dish used for food or liquid; a bowl-shaped depression; in sports, to throw a ball.
✏️ 例句
She filled the bowl with fresh salad.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
a bowl of rice/soup 一碗米饭/汤；bowling 保龄球；Super Bowl 美式橄榄球超级杯
🧠 助记
联想 bowl 发音似"保"→ 保住食物的碗 → 碗
bowl::/bəʊl/:n. 碗:He poured the soup into a large ceramic bowl.
14. congratulation /kənˌɡrætʃuˈleɪʃn/ n. 祝贺；恭喜（常用复数）
📖 英文解释
An expression of pleasure and approval at someone's success or achievement.
✏️ 例句

congratulation::/kənˌɡrætʃuˈleɪʃn/:n. 祝贺:She received warm congratulations from her colleagues after winning the award.
15. consecutive /kənˈsekjʊtɪv/ adj. 连续的；连贯的
📖 英文解释
Following in order without interruption; successive.
✏️ 例句

consecutive::/kənˈsekjʊtɪv/:adj. 连续的:The team won three consecutive championship titles.
16. console /kənˈsəʊl/ v. 安慰；慰问 n. 控制台；安慰物

console::/kənˈsəʊl/:v. 安慰:He tried to console his sister after she lost the competition.
17. crush /krʌʃ/ v. 压碎；击败；使崩溃 n. 迷恋；拥挤

联想 crush 发音「卡瞎」→ 压碎、粉碎；也联想迷恋是心被压住的感觉
crush::/krʌʃ/:v. 压碎:The government moved swiftly to crush the rebellion.
18. deaf /def/ adj. 聋的；不愿听的

Unable to hear; unwilling to listen or pay attention.

🧠 助记

deaf::/def/:adj. 聋的:His repeated warnings fell on deaf ears.
19. dinosaur /ˈdaɪnəsɔːr/ n. 恐龙；守旧的人/物

✏️ 例句
Scientists study dinosaur fossils to learn about prehistoric life.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
dinosaur fossils 恐龙化石；比喻用法：corporate dinosaur 老派企业
🧠 助记
dino-（可怕的）+ saur（蜥蜴）→ 可怕的大蜥蜴 → 恐龙
dinosaur::/ˈdaɪnəsɔːr/:n. 恐龙:The museum's dinosaur exhibit attracted thousands of visitors.

dread::/dred/:v./n. 恐惧:Many students dread the pressure of final exams.

drum::/drʌm/:n. 鼓:She drummed her fingers on the table while waiting nervously.

emperor::/ˈempərər/:n. 皇帝:The emperor issued a decree that changed the entire country's policy.
23. execute /ˈeksɪkjuːt/ v. 执行；实施；处决
📖 英文解释
To carry out or put into effect a plan or action; to perform a task; to put to death as a legal penalty.
✏️ 例句
The plan was carefully executed by the team.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
execute a plan/order/task 执行计划/命令/任务；execute a criminal 处决罪犯；execution n.
🧠 助记
ex-（出）+ ecute（= act 行动）→ 把计划付诸行动 → 执行；也联想 exit（出口）
execute::/ˈeksɪkjuːt/:v. 执行:The team executed the strategy flawlessly under pressure.
24. famine /ˈfæmɪn/ n. 饥荒；严重匮乏
📖 英文解释

✏️ 例句
The famine of the 1840s devastated Ireland's population.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
famine relief 饥荒救济；suffer a famine 遭受饥荒；famine of ideas 思路枯竭（引申）
🧠 助记

famine::/ˈfæmɪn/:n. 饥荒:International aid was deployed to combat the severe famine in the region.

fence::/fens/:n. 栅栏:She sat on the fence when asked to choose sides in the debate.
