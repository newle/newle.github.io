1. hatch /hætʃ/ v. 孵化；策划 n. 舱口；孵化
📖 英文解释
To emerge from an egg; to devise a plan, especially a secret one; a small opening or door in a floor, ceiling, or wall.
✏️ 例句
The conspirators hatched a plan to overthrow the government.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研双义词。hatch a plan/scheme（策划阴谋）；hatch from an egg（从蛋孵化）。
🧠 助记
hatch → 蛋破开孵出，也像在黑暗中策划 → 孵化；策划
hatch::/hætʃ/:v. 孵化:The chicks hatched after three weeks of incubation.
2. heap /hiːp/ n. 堆；大量 v. 堆积；大量给予
📖 英文解释
A disorderly pile of something; a large number or amount; to put in a pile; to give or assign in large quantities.
✏️ 例句
A heap of applications arrived after the job posting.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常见词。heaps of（大量的）；heap praise on（大加赞扬）；heap on（堆积）。
🧠 助记
heap → 叠起来的'哈' → 堆成一堆 → 堆；大量
heap::/hiːp/:n. 堆:The teacher heaped praise on the student for the excellent essay.
3. heave /hiːv/ v. 用力拉/推/扔；起伏 n. 用力拉
📖 英文解释
To lift or haul with great effort; to throw something heavy; to move rhythmically up and down; a great effort to lift something.
✏️ 例句
The sailors heaved the anchor onto the deck.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研词汇。heave a sigh（叹一口气）；heave to（海浪使船摇晃）。
🧠 助记
heave → heavy（沉重）→ 举起沉重的东西 → 用力拉；起伏
heave::/hiːv/:v. 用力拉/推/扔:He heaved a sigh of relief when the crisis was finally over.
4. hike /haɪk/ n./v. 远足；大幅提高
📖 英文解释
A long walk in the country; a sharp increase in prices or costs; to go on a long walk; to increase sharply.
✏️ 例句
The central bank hiked interest rates to control inflation.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研双义词。price hike（价格大涨）；go hiking（去徒步）。经济类阅读高频。
🧠 助记
hike → high（高）→ 价格高起来；在高山上远足 → 远足；大幅提高
hike::/haɪk/:n./v. 远足:The tax hike drew strong opposition from business groups.
5. hollow /ˈhɒləʊ/ adj. 空洞的；虚伪的 n. 洞；凹地 v. 挖空
📖 英文解释
Having a hole or empty space inside; without meaningful content; a sunken area; to make hollow.
✏️ 例句
His promises turned out to be hollow and meaningless.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考引申：hollow victory/promise（空洞的胜利/承诺）。hollow out（掏空）。
🧠 助记
hollow → hole（洞）的扩展 → 里面是空的 → 空洞的；洞
hollow::/ˈhɒləʊ/:adj. 空洞的:The general's hollow victory came at too great a cost.

To crowd together; to curl one's body into a small space; a close gathering of people for a private discussion.

7. inland /ˈɪnlənd/ adj./adv. 内陆的；内地
📖 英文解释
Situated in or toward the interior of a country, away from the coast; into or toward the interior.
✏️ 例句
Inland areas tend to experience more extreme temperatures than coastal regions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研地理类词汇。inland city/region（内陆城市/地区）；Inland Revenue（英国税务局）。

inland::/ˈɪnlənd/:adj./adv. 内陆的:The storm moved inland and caused flooding in several cities.
8. insulate /ˈɪnsjuleɪt/ v. 隔热；绝缘；使隔离
📖 英文解释
To protect something by surrounding it with a material that prevents heat, cold, or electricity from passing through; to isolate.
✏️ 例句
Proper insulation can significantly reduce heating costs in winter.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常考引申：insulate from（使免受影响）；insulate oneself from criticism（避免批评）。
🧠 助记
insul（岛）+ ate → 像岛一样隔开 → 隔热；使隔离
insulate::/ˈɪnsjuleɪt/:v. 隔热:The country tried to insulate its economy from global shocks.
9. intermediate /ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət/ adj. 中间的；中级的 n. 中间物
📖 英文解释
Coming between two things in time, place, or order; at a level between basic and advanced; something that is intermediate.
✏️ 例句

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研常见词。intermediate level（中级）；intermediate step（中间步骤）。
🧠 助记

intermediate::/ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət/:adj. 中间的:The intermediate stage of the project requires careful coordination.
10. invaluable /ɪnˈvæljuəbl/ adj. 无价的；极宝贵的
📖 英文解释
Extremely useful; indispensable; too valuable to be estimated.
✏️ 例句
Her experience in crisis management proved invaluable to the team.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研易混词：invaluable（极有价值的）≠ valueless（无价值的）。注意前缀in-此处表强调。
🧠 助记
in（不能）+ valuable（定价）→ 不能用价格衡量的 → 无价的；极宝贵的
invaluable::/ɪnˈvæljuəbl/:adj. 无价的:The mentor's guidance was invaluable during the early career stage.
11. jargon /ˈdʒɑːɡən/ n. 行话；术语；黑话
📖 英文解释
Special words or expressions used by a particular profession or group that are difficult for others to understand.
✏️ 例句
The professor was criticized for using too much academic jargon.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
考研写作指导词：避免使用jargon，保持语言清晰。legal/medical jargon（法律/医学术语）。
🧠 助记
jargon → 'jar'（罐子）+ gon → 装在罐子里别人看不懂的行话 → 行话；术语
jargon::/ˈdʒɑːɡən/:n. 行话:Technical jargon can make scientific papers inaccessible to the public.
