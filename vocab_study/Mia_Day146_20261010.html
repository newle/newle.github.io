1. circular /ˈsɜːrkjələr/ adj./n. 圆形的；循环的；通知单
📖 英文解释
Having the form of a circle; moving or taking place along a circle; a letter or advertisement distributed to many people.
✏️ 例句
The circular path around the park was popular with joggers.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
circular economy 循环经济；circular letter 通知信；circular argument 循环论证
🧠 助记
circle（圆）+ ar = 圆形的；循环的
circular::/ˈsɜːrkjələr/:adj./n. 圆形的:The company sent out a circular to inform staff of the policy change.
2. crisp /krɪsp/ adj./n. 脆的；清新的；薯片
📖 英文解释
Firm and brittle; (of weather) pleasantly cool and fresh; a thin slice of potato fried until crisp.
✏️ 例句
She bit into the crisp apple with a satisfying crunch.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
crisp weather 清爽的天气；crisp reply 简洁的回答；crisp and clear 清晰明了
🧠 助记
crisp 发音像'卡死破'，脆的东西咬下去咔嚓一声
crisp::/krɪsp/:adj./n. 脆的:His crisp writing style made the report easy to understand.
3. crust /krʌst/ n. 外壳；硬皮；地壳
📖 英文解释
The tough outer part of a loaf of bread; the outer layer of the earth.
✏️ 例句
She cut off the crusts of the sandwich for the children.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
earth's crust 地壳；bread crust 面包外皮；upper crust 上层社会
🧠 助记
crust = cr + ust，外壳像生锈（rust）的外层一样硬
crust::/krʌst/:n. 外壳:The earthquake originated deep below the earth's crust.
4. curl /kɜːrl/ v./n. 卷曲；蜷缩；鬈发
📖 英文解释
To form or cause to form into a curved or spiral shape; a thing having a curved shape.
✏️ 例句
She used a curling iron to curl her straight hair.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
curl up 蜷缩；curl of smoke 一缕烟；curly adj. 卷曲的
🧠 助记
curl 和 swirl（旋转）押韵，卷起来旋转
curl::/kɜːrl/:v./n. 卷曲:The cat curled up in a ball next to the fireplace.
5. curtain /ˈkɜːrtn/ n. 窗帘；帷幕；遮蔽物
📖 英文解释
A piece of material suspended at the top to form a hanging screen; the screen separating the stage from the auditorium.
✏️ 例句
She pulled back the curtains to let the morning light in.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
draw the curtains 拉上窗帘；Iron Curtain 铁幕；curtain call 谢幕
🧠 助记
curtain = cur（隐藏）+ tain，用布遮住 = 窗帘
curtain::/ˈkɜːrtn/:n. 窗帘:The actor took a bow after the curtain fell at the end of the play.
6. deter /dɪˈtɜːr/ v. 阻止；威慑；使不敢
📖 英文解释
To discourage someone from doing something by instilling fear or doubt.
✏️ 例句
The harsh punishment was meant to deter people from committing crimes.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
deter crime 威慑犯罪；deter sb from doing 阻止某人做；deterrent n. 威慑物
🧠 助记
de（去除）+ ter（恐惧词根）= 通过恐惧去除行为 = 威慑
deter::/dɪˈtɜːr/:v. 阻止:Higher fines were introduced to deter illegal dumping of waste.
7. diagram /ˈdaɪəɡræm/ n. 图表；示意图；图解
📖 英文解释
A simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something.
✏️ 例句
The teacher drew a diagram to explain how the heart pumps blood.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
flow diagram 流程图；diagram of a circuit 电路图；schematic diagram 示意图
🧠 助记
dia（穿过）+ gram（写/图）= 画穿解释 = 图解
diagram::/ˈdaɪəɡræm/:n. 图表:The instruction manual included a clear diagram of all the components.
8. epic /ˈepɪk/ adj./n. 史诗般的；史诗；壮丽的
📖 英文解释
A long poem or film dealing with heroic deeds; impressive and large-scale.
✏️ 例句
The movie was an epic adventure set in ancient Rome.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
epic poem 史诗；epic journey 漫长壮阔的旅程；epic battle 史诗级战役
🧠 助记
epic = e + pic（图片），一幅史诗级的大图
epic::/ˈepɪk/:adj./n. 史诗般的:Homer's Iliad is considered one of the greatest epic poems in history.
9. eradicate /ɪˈrædɪkeɪt/ v. 根除；消灭；铲除
📖 英文解释
To completely destroy or put an end to something, especially something regarded as harmful.
✏️ 例句
The government launched a campaign to eradicate poverty.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
eradicate disease 根除疾病；eradicate corruption 铲除腐败；eradication n.
🧠 助记
e + radic（根）+ ate = 连根拔除 = 根除
eradicate::/ɪˈrædɪkeɪt/:v. 根除:Scientists are working to eradicate malaria through vaccination programmes.
10. fabulous /ˈfæbjələs/ adj. 极好的；惊人的；神话般的
📖 英文解释
Extraordinarily good or attractive; resembling or characteristic of a fable.
✏️ 例句
She wore a fabulous dress to the gala event.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
fabulous wealth 惊人的财富；fabulous creature 神话中的生物；fabulously adv.
🧠 助记
fable（神话故事）+ ous = 像神话故事一样精彩 = 极好的
fabulous::/ˈfæbjələs/:adj. 极好的:The band put on a fabulous performance that delighted the crowd.
11. fascinate /ˈfæsɪneɪt/ v. 使着迷；深深吸引
📖 英文解释
To attract and hold the attention and interest of someone strongly.
✏️ 例句
The documentary fascinated her with its detailed account of ocean life.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
fascinate sb 使某人着迷；fascinated by 被……迷住；fascinating adj.
🧠 助记
fascin（迷恋）+ ate，迷恋上了就着迷了
fascinate::/ˈfæsɪneɪt/:v. 使着迷:Ancient history has always fascinated him since childhood.
12. fibre /ˈfaɪbər/ n. 纤维；纤维质；品格
📖 英文解释
A thread or filament from which a textile is formed; dietary fibre that aids digestion; strength of character.
✏️ 例句
A diet rich in fibre can help maintain good digestive health.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
optical fibre 光纤；dietary fibre 膳食纤维；moral fibre 道德品质
🧠 助记
fibre = fi + bre，纤维细如发丝（fi = fine细）
fibre::/ˈfaɪbər/:n. 纤维:The jacket is made from high-quality synthetic fibre.
13. formidable /ˈfɔːrmɪdəbl/ adj. 强大的；令人敬畏的；难以对付的
📖 英文解释
Inspiring fear or respect through being impressively large, powerful, intense, or capable.
✏️ 例句
The team faced a formidable opponent in the final match.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
formidable challenge 巨大的挑战；formidable opponent 强劲对手；formidably adv.
🧠 助记
formid（恐惧）+ able = 可怕的，令人敬畏的
formidable::/ˈfɔːrmɪdəbl/:adj. 强大的:She proved to be a formidable negotiator in the boardroom.
14. hearing /ˈhɪərɪŋ/ n. 听力；听觉；听证会
📖 英文解释
The faculty of perceiving sounds; an opportunity to state your case; a session of a court or tribunal.
✏️ 例句
The parliamentary hearing lasted three hours.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
hearing aid 助听器；hold a hearing 举行听证会；hard of hearing 听力不好
🧠 助记
hear（听）+ ing = 听的能力/行为 = 听力/听证
hearing::/ˈhɪərɪŋ/:n. 听力:The suspect was granted a fair hearing before the tribunal.
15. heroic /hɪˈrəʊɪk/ adj. 英勇的；英雄的；宏大的
📖 英文解释
Having the characteristics of a hero or heroine; courageous and brave; grand in scale.
✏️ 例句
The firefighters made a heroic effort to save the trapped victims.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
heroic act 英勇行为；heroic poem 英雄诗篇；heroically adv.
🧠 助记
hero（英雄）+ ic = 英雄般的 = 英勇的
heroic::/hɪˈrəʊɪk/:adj. 英勇的:Her heroic rescue of the drowning child was praised by all.
16. inn /ɪn/ n. 客栈；小旅馆；酒馆
📖 英文解释
A pub, typically one in the country, offering accommodation.
✏️ 例句
They stopped at a cosy inn by the roadside for the night.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
country inn 乡村客栈；inn keeper 客栈老板；the inn 酒馆
🧠 助记
inn 发音像'引'（进门），进门就是客栈
inn::/ɪn/:n. 客栈:The travellers found shelter at a small inn near the crossroads.
17. kin /kɪn/ n. 亲属；家族；同类
📖 英文解释
One's family and relations; of the same type.
✏️ 例句
She felt a duty to look after all her kin during difficult times.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
next of kin 最近的亲属；kith and kin 亲友；akin to 类似于
🧠 助记
kin 发音像'亲'，亲戚就是 kin
kin::/kɪn/:n. 亲属:He left his estate to his next of kin after passing away.
18. murmur /ˈmɜːrmər/ v./n. 低语；喃喃；抱怨声
📖 英文解释
To say something in a low, soft, or indistinct voice; a low continuous sound.
✏️ 例句
She murmured a few words of comfort to the crying child.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
murmur of discontent 不满的低声；heart murmur 心脏杂音；without a murmur 毫无怨言
🧠 助记
murmur 的发音本身就像低沉模糊的声音 murr-murr
murmur::/ˈmɜːrmər/:v./n. 低语:She could hear the murmur of voices from the next room.
19. outrage /ˈaʊtreɪdʒ/ n./v. 愤怒；暴行；激怒
📖 英文解释
An extremely strong reaction of anger, shock, or indignation; a very cruel or shocking action.
✏️ 例句
The public expressed outrage at the government's decision.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
express outrage 表达愤怒；outrage at 对……感到愤慨；outrageous adj.
🧠 助记
out（超出）+ rage（愤怒）= 超出了容忍的愤怒 = 极度愤慨
outrage::/ˈaʊtreɪdʒ/:n./v. 愤怒:The news of the attack caused widespread outrage across the country.
20. pray /preɪ/ v. 祈祷；祈求；请求
📖 英文解释
To address a solemn request or expression of thanks to a deity; to make a fervent request.
✏️ 例句
She prayed for the safe return of her son from the war.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
pray for 祈求；pray to God 向上帝祈祷；prayer n. 祈祷
🧠 助记
pray 和 play（玩）差一个字母，虔诚祈祷不是在玩
pray::/preɪ/:v. 祈祷:The villagers gathered at the temple to pray for rain.
21. propaganda /ˌprɒpəˈɡændə/ n. 宣传；鼓吹；宣传材料
📖 英文解释
Information, especially of a biased or misleading nature, used to promote a political cause or point of view.
✏️ 例句
During the war, both sides used propaganda to influence public opinion.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
political propaganda 政治宣传；propaganda campaign 宣传运动；propaganda tool 宣传工具
🧠 助记
propa + ganda，不断扩散（propagate）的想法 = 宣传
propaganda::/ˌprɒpəˈɡændə/:n. 宣传:The regime used propaganda to maintain control over the population.
22. rouse /raʊz/ v. 唤醒；激起；振奋
📖 英文解释
To bring out of sleep; to cause someone to feel a particular emotion; to wake up.
✏️ 例句
The general's speech roused the soldiers to fight with renewed energy.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
rouse from sleep 从睡眠中唤醒；rouse sb's interest 激起某人兴趣；rouse to action 激励行动
🧠 助记
rouse 和 raise（举起）相近，把人从沉睡中举起来 = 唤醒
rouse::/raʊz/:v. 唤醒:The speech roused the crowd to a state of great excitement.
23. satire /ˈsætaɪər/ n. 讽刺；讽刺作品
📖 英文解释
The use of humour, irony, or exaggeration to criticise people's stupidity or vices.
✏️ 例句
The play was a sharp satire on the greed of modern politicians.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
political satire 政治讽刺；social satire 社会讽刺；satirical adj.
🧠 助记
satire = sat（坐）+ ire（愤怒），坐下来愤怒地讽刺
satire::/ˈsætaɪər/:n. 讽刺:The novel is a biting satire on the corruption of the ruling class.
24. sneak /sniːk/ v./n. 溜；偷偷走；告密者
📖 英文解释
To move stealthily and secretly to avoid being noticed; to go or do something without being observed.
✏️ 例句
The child tried to sneak a cookie from the jar before dinner.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
sneak out 悄悄溜出；sneak a look 偷看；sneak preview 预先偷看/试映
🧠 助记
sneak = s + 悄悄（neak），像蛇一样悄悄滑行
sneak::/sniːk/:v./n. 溜:He managed to sneak past the guards without being noticed.
25. stalk /stɔːk/ n./v. 茎；跟踪；昂首阔步走
📖 英文解释
The main stem of a plant; to pursue or approach stealthily; to walk in a proud, stiff manner.
✏️ 例句
A corn stalk can grow up to three metres tall.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
flower stalk 花茎；stalk an animal 悄悄跟踪猎物；stalker 跟踪狂
🧠 助记
stalk = st + alk，植物茎干挺直地站（stand）着
stalk::/stɔːk/:n./v. 茎:He was arrested for stalking his former colleague.
26. symphony /ˈsɪmfəni/ n. 交响乐；交响曲
📖 英文解释
An elaborate musical composition for full orchestra, typically in four movements.
✏️ 例句
Beethoven's Ninth Symphony is one of the most famous pieces in classical music.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
symphony orchestra 交响乐团；symphony concert 交响乐演奏会；symphonic adj.
🧠 助记
sym（共同）+ phony（声音）= 共同发出的声音 = 交响乐
symphony::/ˈsɪmfəni/:n. 交响乐:The concert hall was filled for the premiere of the new symphony.
27. tuck /tʌk/ v./n. 卷起；藏起；打褶
📖 英文解释
To push, fold, or turn something so that it is hidden; to put into a snug or confined place.
✏️ 例句
She tucked the children in bed and kissed them good night.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
tuck in 把……塞进；tuck away 藏起来；nip and tuck 旗鼓相当
🧠 助记
tuck 发音像'塞（进）'，把东西塞进去 = 卷起/藏起
tuck::/tʌk/:v./n. 卷起:He tucked the letter into his pocket and walked out the door.
28. whatsoever /ˌwɒtsəʊˈevər/ pron./adj. 无论什么；任何的（强调否定）
📖 英文解释
Used for emphasis instead of 'whatever', especially in negative statements.
✏️ 例句
There is no evidence whatsoever to support that claim.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
no reason whatsoever 毫无理由；nothing whatsoever 什么都没有；常作强调用
🧠 助记
what + so + ever = 无论什么样的'所有'情况
whatsoever::/ˌwɒtsəʊˈevər/:pron./adj. 无论什么:He showed no remorse whatsoever for what he had done.
29. whip /wɪp/ n./v. 鞭子；鞭打；迅速移动
📖 英文解释
A strip of leather used to urge or punish an animal or person; to beat someone with a whip; to move quickly.
✏️ 例句
The trainer used a whip to command the horse during the performance.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
whip up 鼓动/迅速做出；crack the whip 严格督促；whipped cream 奶油
🧠 助记
whip 发音像'挥啪'，挥鞭啪的一声
whip::/wɪp/:n./v. 鞭子:The coach cracked the whip to get the team to train harder.
30. wrist /rɪst/ n. 手腕；腕关节
📖 英文解释
The joint connecting the hand with the forearm.
✏️ 例句
She wore a delicate bracelet around her wrist.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
wrist watch 手表；wrist injury 手腕受伤；slap on the wrist 轻描淡写的责备
🧠 助记
wrist = wr + ist，手腕（wrist）扭转（wr = wring）的地方
wrist::/rɪst/:n. 手腕:He checked his wrist watch nervously as he waited for the results.
