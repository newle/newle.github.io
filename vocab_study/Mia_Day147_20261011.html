1. applause /əˈplɔːz/ n. 鼓掌；掌声
📖 英文解释
The sound of people clapping their hands to show approval or enjoyment, especially at a performance or speech.
✏️ 例句
The audience broke into loud applause after the pianist finished her performance.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：a round of applause（一阵掌声）；win/earn/receive applause；thunderous applause（雷鸣般的掌声）。考研中常出现在文化/艺术类阅读。
🧠 助记
ap-plause：ap（向）+ plause（拍打）→ 向手拍打 → 鼓掌。联想：台下观众齐声 applaud，发出 applause。
applause::/əˈplɔːz/:n. 鼓掌:The speech was met with enthusiastic applause.

🧠 助记
ash 发音像'哎唰'—— 一场火灾后'哎唰'一下全变成了灰烬。
ash::/æʃ/:n. 灰:Volcanic ash covered the village after the eruption.

junct（连接）+ ion → 连接的地方 → 交叉路口。词根 junct = join，联想：conjunction（连词）也是连接词。
junction::/ˈdʒʌŋkʃn/:n. 连接处:Turn left at the junction and follow the road for two miles.
30. kidnap /ˈkɪdnæp/ v./n. 绑架；劫持
📖 英文解释
To take someone away illegally and hold them as a prisoner, usually to demand money or other benefits in return for releasing them.
✏️ 例句
The wealthy businessman's son was kidnapped and held for ransom.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常见搭配：kidnap for ransom（绑架勒索）；kidnapping attempt（绑架企图）；kidnapper（绑架者）。考研社会/法律类常见。
🧠 助记
kid（小孩）+ nap（打盹/抓取）→ 把小孩趁其打盹时抓走 → 绑架。nap 在古英语有'抓取'之意。
kidnap::/ˈkɪdnæp/:v./n. 绑架:The police successfully rescued the kidnapped child unharmed.
