1. agitate /ˈædʒɪteɪt/ v. 煽动；晃动；使不安
📖 英文解释
to stir up public interest and concern; to shake or move briskly; to disturb mentally
✏️ 例句
The activist agitated for better working conditions in the factories.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：agitate for/against（为…鼓动/反对）、agitated state（激动的状态）；考研社会议题高频
🧠 助记
agit-（行动）+ ate → 不断推动行动 → 煽动/晃动
agitate::/ˈædʒɪteɪt/:v. 煽动:He agitated for reforms throughout his political career.
2. amiable /ˈeɪmiəbəl/ adj. 亲切的；和蔼可亲的
📖 英文解释
having or displaying a friendly and pleasant manner
✏️ 例句
Her amiable personality made her popular among colleagues.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：amiable person/manner（亲切的人/态度）；近义词 affable, genial；书面语描述人物常用
🧠 助记
ami-（朋友，法语 ami）+ able → 能交友的 → 亲切的
amiable::/ˈeɪmiəbəl/:adj. 亲切的:The host was amiable and made all the guests feel welcome.
3. bribe /braɪb/ n./v. 贿赂
📖 英文解释
money or a favor given to induce someone to do something; to offer a bribe to
✏️ 例句
The official was arrested for accepting bribes from contractors.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：accept/offer a bribe（收/行贿）、bribery（n.行贿）；法律/反腐语境高频
🧠 助记
bribe 联想 'bride'（新娘）→ 旧时给新娘家送礼 → 引申为贿赂
bribe::/braɪb/:n./v. 贿赂:He was convicted of attempting to bribe a public official.
4. composite /ˈkɒmpəzɪt/ n./adj. 复合材料；合成的
📖 英文解释
made up of distinct parts or elements; a material made from two or more substances
✏️ 例句
Carbon fiber is a composite material used in aircraft manufacturing.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：composite material（复合材料）、composite index（综合指数）；科技/经济学高频
🧠 助记
compo-（组合）+ site（位置）→ 多种成分组合在一起的 → 复合的
composite::/ˈkɒmpəzɪt/:n./adj. 复合材料:The bridge was constructed using composite materials.
5. comrade /ˈkɒmreɪd/ n. 同志；战友
📖 英文解释
a companion who shares one's activities or is a fellow member of an organization
✏️ 例句
He spoke fondly of his old comrades from the army.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：comrade in arms（战友）；政治语境中的 comrade（同志）；友谊类写作可用
🧠 助记
com-（共同）+ rade（像 road，路）→ 共同走一条路的人 → 同志/战友
comrade::/ˈkɒmreɪd/:n. 同志:He remained loyal to his comrades even in the most difficult times.
6. contingent /kənˈtɪndʒənt/ n. 代表团；分遣队；adj. 依情况而定的
📖 英文解释
a group forming part of a larger body; dependent on circumstances or conditions
✏️ 例句
A large contingent of soldiers was sent to the border.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：contingent on/upon（取决于）、a contingent of（一队…）；考研中常考形容词义
🧠 助记
conting-（接触）+ ent → 依据实际接触情况而定 → 依情况而定的/代表团
contingent::/kənˈtɪndʒənt/:n. 代表团:The success of the plan is contingent upon adequate funding.
7. culprit /ˈkʌlprɪt/ n. 罪犯；肇事者
📖 英文解释
a person who is responsible for a crime or other misdeed
✏️ 例句
The police quickly identified the culprit of the robbery.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：the real culprit（真正的罪犯）；比喻用法：the culprit behind the problem（问题的根源）
🧠 助记
culp-（罪，拉丁 culpa）+ rit → 有罪的那个人 → 罪犯
culprit::/ˈkʌlprɪt/:n. 罪犯:Poor diet is often the culprit behind health problems.
8. giggle /ˈɡɪɡəl/ v./n. 咯咯地笑
📖 英文解释
to laugh in a light, silly way; a light, silly laugh
✏️ 例句
The children giggled when the clown made funny faces.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：giggle at sth（因某事咯咯笑）；区分：giggle（抑制不住的笑）vs chuckle（轻声笑）
🧠 助记
giggle 象声词，像咯咯声 'gi-gi-gi' → 咯咯笑
giggle::/ˈɡɪɡəl/:v./n. 咯咯地笑:She tried not to giggle during the serious meeting.
9. interim /ˈɪntərɪm/ adj./n. 临时的；过渡期
📖 英文解释
in or for the intervening period; temporary
✏️ 例句
An interim manager was appointed until a permanent one could be found.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：interim report（中期报告）、interim government（过渡政府）、in the interim（在此期间）
🧠 助记
inter-（之间）+ im（进入）→ 在两者之间的时期 → 临时的
interim::/ˈɪntərɪm/:adj./n. 临时的:The interim agreement will remain in force for six months.
10. lame /leɪm/ adj. 痛的；跛的；无说服力的
📖 英文解释
unable to walk normally due to injury; unconvincing or inadequate
✏️ 例句
He gave a lame excuse for arriving late to the meeting.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：lame excuse（站不住脚的借口）、go lame（变得跛行）；口语中表示无聊/蹩脚
🧠 助记
lame 联想 'blame'（指责）→ 跛腿的人常被指责走路慢 → 跛的/无力的
lame::/leɪm/:adj. 痛的:That was a pretty lame argument that convinced no one.
11. minimise /ˈmɪnɪmaɪz/ v. 尽量减少；最小化
📖 英文解释
to reduce something to the smallest possible amount or degree
✏️ 例句
The government took steps to minimise the economic impact of the crisis.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：minimise risk/damage/cost（降低风险/损害/成本）；英式拼写，美式 minimize
🧠 助记
minim-（最小）+ ise → 使变为最小 → 尽量减少
minimise::/ˈmɪnɪmaɪz/:v. 尽量减少:We must minimise waste to protect the environment.
12. paddle /ˈpædəl/ n. 桨；v. 划桨；涉水
📖 英文解释
a short pole with a broad flat blade used to propel a boat; to row or wade
✏️ 例句
They paddled the canoe down the river at sunset.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：paddle a canoe（划独木舟）、paddle in the water（在水中涉水）
🧠 助记
paddle 联想 'puddle'（水坑）→ 在水坑里划动 → 桨/涉水
paddle::/ˈpædəl/:n. 桨:The children paddled in the shallow end of the pool.
13. pea /piː/ n. 豌豆
📖 英文解释

✏️ 例句

pea 发音像 'P' → P 字形如豆荚中的豌豆
pea::/piː/:n. 豌豆:The children refused to eat their peas at dinner.
14. physiology /ˌfɪziˈɒlədʒi/ n. 生理学
📖 英文解释
the branch of biology dealing with the normal functions of living organisms
✏️ 例句
She studied physiology to understand how the human body functions.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：human physiology（人体生理学）；physiological（形容词），physicist → physics 注意区分
🧠 助记
physio-（身体/自然）+ logy（学科）→ 研究身体自然功能的学科 → 生理学
physiology::/ˌfɪziˈɒlədʒi/:n. 生理学:Understanding physiology is essential for medical students.
15. plaster /ˈplɑːstə/ n. 石膏；创可贴；v. 涂抹
📖 英文解释
a paste that hardens as it dries; a piece of material used to cover wounds
✏️ 例句

plaster 联想 'blast'（爆炸）→ 爆炸后受伤需要贴石膏/创可贴
plaster::/ˈplɑːstə/:n. 石膏:She plastered the walls with political posters.
16. preposition /ˌprepəˈzɪʃən/ n. 介词
📖 英文解释
a word governing a noun or pronoun to express its relation to another word
✏️ 例句
In the sentence 'the cat is on the table', 'on' is a preposition.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：use a preposition（使用介词）、prepositional phrase（介词短语）；语言学基础词汇
🧠 助记
pre（前）+ position（位置）→ 放在名词前表示位置关系的词 → 介词
preposition::/ˌprepəˈzɪʃən/:n. 介词:Students often struggle with the correct use of prepositions.

🧠 助记

radioactive::/ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv/:adj. 放射性的:The radioactive contamination spread quickly after the accident.
18. ruthless /ˈruːθləs/ adj. 无情的；残忍的

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
ruth-（怜悯，古英语 ruthe）+ less（没有）→ 没有怜悯 → 无情的
ruthless::/ˈruːθləs/:adj. 无情的:The company made a ruthless decision to cut half its workforce.
19. watt /wɒt/ n. 瓦特（功率单位）
📖 英文解释
the SI unit of power, equivalent to one joule per second
✏️ 例句
A standard light bulb uses about 60 watts of electricity.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：100-watt bulb（100瓦灯泡）、wattage（功率）；以发明家 James Watt 命名
🧠 助记
Watt = James Watt（蒸汽机发明者）→ 以其名命名的功率单位
watt::/wɒt/:n. 瓦特（功率单位）:The solar panel generates up to 400 watts of power.
20. adore /əˈdɔː/ v. 热爱；崇拜

🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
ad-（朝向）+ ore（金子）→ 朝向金子一样珍视 → 热爱/崇拜
adore::/əˈdɔː/:v. 热爱:The fans adore the singer for her powerful voice.
21. esthetic /esˈθetɪk/ n./adj. 美学；审美的
📖 英文解释
concerned with beauty or the appreciation of beauty; a set of principles about beauty
✏️ 例句
The building was praised for its esthetic design.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
esthetic 联想 'athlete'（运动员）→ 运动员的优美姿态充满美感 → 审美的
esthetic::/esˈθetɪk/:n./adj. 美学:She has a refined esthetic that influences her interior design choices.
22. anguish /ˈæŋɡwɪʃ/ n. 痛苦；苦恼
📖 英文解释
severe mental or physical pain or suffering
✏️ 例句
The mother cried in anguish when she heard the news of the accident.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：in anguish（在痛苦中）、cry/scream in anguish（痛苦地哭喊）；文学性强，考研写作增色
🧠 助记
angu-（狭窄，拉丁 angustus）+ ish → 被痛苦压缩得喘不过气 → 极度痛苦
anguish::/ˈæŋɡwɪʃ/:n. 痛苦:She was in anguish over the difficult decision she had to make.
23. appal /əˈpɔːl/ v. 吓坏；使惊骇
📖 英文解释
to greatly dismay or horrify someone
✏️ 例句
The cruelty of the attack appalled the entire community.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：be appalled by/at（对…感到震惊）、appalling conditions（令人震惊的条件）；英式拼写，美式 appall
🧠 助记
ap-（加强）+ pal-（苍白）→ 使人吓得脸色苍白 → 使惊骇
appal::/əˈpɔːl/:v. 吓坏:She was appalled by the living conditions in the shelter.
24. appendix /əˈpendɪks/ n. 附录；阑尾
📖 英文解释
supplementary material at the end of a book; a small tube attached to the large intestine
✏️ 例句
Additional data can be found in the appendix at the back of the report.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配
常用搭配：see Appendix A（见附录A）、appendicitis（阑尾炎）；一词两义，注意语境区分
🧠 助记
append（附加）+ ix → 附加上去的东西 → 附录/阑尾（附属器官）
appendix::/əˈpendɪks/:n. 附录:The full statistical tables are provided in the appendix.
25. assassinate /əˈsæsɪneɪt/ v. 暗杀；刺杀
📖 英文解释
to murder an important or famous person, especially for political reasons
✏️ 例句
President Lincoln was assassinated in 1865.
🌍 考研用法 & 搭配

🧠 助记
ass + ass + in + ate → 双重 ass（驴）→ 极端行为 → 暗杀（谐音记忆法）
assassinate::/əˈsæsɪneɪt/:v. 暗杀:The plot to assassinate the prime minister was foiled by security forces.
